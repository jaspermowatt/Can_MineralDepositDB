Qualified comments: (Applies to: regional geology) Early Paleoproterozoic Huronian strata in the Elliot Lake area are regionally folded into the gently westward plunging Quirke Syncline, the limbs of which dip 10 to 40 degrees toward its axial plane. The Denison shafts are sited in the lower quartzites and conglomerates of the Huronian Matinenda Formation, on the north limb of the syncline. These beds are relatively thick in this area, occupying the Quirke Channel, which fills a west-northwest striking depression in the unconformably underlying Archean basement. Although the coarser grained quartzites commonly contain low grade mineralization, higher grade and relatively consistent mineralization is found in beds of quartz pebble bearing conglomerate with disseminated pyrite in the matrix. These are referred to as reefs or blankets, and the better grade reefs are thick, have minimal numbers of thin quartzite bands, and their clasts are well packed.

(Applies to: mineralization) Uranium occurs as detrital thorium-bearing uraninite, which accompanies detrital pyrite in the conglomerate matrix, and also as the secondary minerals brannerite, rutile, monazite and zircon The deposits are thus modified paleoplacers.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Denison acquired a block of 83 claims covering the original Denison Mine area in 1954, and the Denison deposit was discovered by surface drilling that same year. In 1957, reserves at 136.8 million tons averaging 2.78 lbs/ton U3O8 were estimated in the conglomerate reefs within the Main Zone, and 2 shafts were sunk. Production began in 1958. About the same time, two shafts were sunk at the nearby Can-Met Mine, where reserves were estimated at 6.6 million short tons grading 1.83 lbs/ton. Can-Met amalgamated with Denison in 1960. The Stanrock property south of Can-Met was explored by Stancan Uranium Mines Ltd between 1956 and 1957, when two shafts were sunk on that property. Denison amalgamated with Stanrock in 1973, and the two mines continued to operate until March, 1992, when they closed due to price decline and over-supply of uranium.

(Applies to: production) The total production from the Denison Mine workings was 69.4 million short tons grading 2.25 lbs/ton U3O8 (0.123% U3O8) (Cochrane and Hwozdyk, 2007).

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) Historical resource estimates for developed and undeveloped areas for six different cut-off grades were computer tabulated by Cochrane and Hwozdyk (2007), with the caution that these figures are not 43.101 compliant. Total mineralization remaining is: 169.7 million short tons @ 1.21 lbs/ton using a cut-off grade of 0.80 lb/ton. Of this, 89.2 million short tons @ 1.29 lbs/ton U3O8 is developed (in areas already accessed by mine workings where 24% of the ore has already been mined) and 80.5 million short tons @ 1.13 lbs/ton U3O8 is undeveloped. No resources above a 70% recovery mining limit are included, either in developed or undeveloped ground.
