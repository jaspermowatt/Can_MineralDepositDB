Qualified comments: (Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) Nine vertical holes totalling 931.3 m were drilled in 1985 to test a large surface radon anomaly (up to 6.657.8 T/sqm). Two drillholes encountered significant mineralization 1) DH TK-78-1 maximum peak 7350 cps with 12 m anomalous zone (>500 cps), and 2) DH TK78-5 maximum 9500 cps with 17.5 m anomalous zone.

(Applies to: mineralization) Correlation of mineralized zones in drillholes and the mineralized felsenmeer on surface indicate an irregular elongate lens. The mineralized zone strikes E-W, dips 70 degrees S and plunges 30 degrees The true thickness in the 2 mineralized holes is 5-8 m but is discontinuous and weakly mineralized. The uranium mineralized zone runs appr. 10 m above and sub-parallel to the two major E-W striking faults.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Epidotization, sericitization and weak kaolinitization were observed in drill core, but these are considered as regional features and not strictly related to the mineralization.
