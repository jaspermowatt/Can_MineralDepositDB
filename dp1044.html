Qualified comments: (Applies to: regional geology) According to the Ontario Mineral Deposit Inventory: "A metasedimentary belt, predominantly marble, is intruded by granite and syenite pegmatites which carry thorian uraninite, uraninite and carnotite with minor py and po. Radioactivity is associated with hematization and occurs within diopside-calcite skarns (uraninite) within the marble as well as in the pegmatites (thorian uraninite). Mo and graphite also occur. A zone, 3,000 x 400 ft has been outlined on this property. Drilling assays ranged up to 0.21 % U3O8 over 2.0 ft. Innumerable occurrences of high grade uranium-bearing skarn were found by drilling and one continuous skarn was identified over an elongated, Z-shaped strike for more than 6 miles. This takes in the Canadian All Metals and Wadasa prospects to the E, in Monmouth township."
