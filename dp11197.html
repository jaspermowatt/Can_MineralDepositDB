Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Reports by Monahan (1980; 1981) refer to a program in which five holes were drilled to find the source of a radioactive boulder train. A attempt was made to drill in the region (up-ice) in the apex where the fan of the boulder train started and where radon gas was detected in the soil.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) This project involved the drilling of 5 holes to test the radioactive source. Each hole was to be drilled to a depth of 60 metres. They were all drilled vertically and penetrated paragneiss and ended in granodioritic gneiss. The paragneiss is highly biotitic with zones of anatexis which contain the slightly higher radioactivity readings. Total count hole logs (analog) were done using a Mt. Sopris logging tool.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) A sample of 0.3 m core of radioactive biotite rich paragneiss assayed 0.025% U3O8. Based upon this assay, the rest of the radioactivity found in the holes is interpreted as being due to U3O8.
