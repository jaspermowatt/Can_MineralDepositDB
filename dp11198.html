Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The point on the map marks the centre of a large regional survey consisting of reconnaissance geology, soil and lake geochemistry and geophysics.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) It was a G.S.C. airborne radiometric surveys that led to the establishment of this area for concentrated prospecting. Ground traverses were done using scintillometers to detect areas where rocks with high levels of radioactivity occur. These rocks were sent for uranium analysis. An EM-16 unit was used to detect linear structures or conductors that could be associated with uranium mineralization.

(Applies to: geochemistry) Surface water and soil samples were collected for radon analysis. High background levels of radon and two anomalous areas were detected in late granitic terrain. Lake water and bottom sediment samples were also collected for uranium analysis. Results from both surveys are presented in maps included in the report.

(Applies to: regional geology) The main rock type in this region is granite gneiss. Later intrusives are of granite to quartz-monzonite composition. Some of them are porphyritic. The area also contains many metavolcanic and metasedimentary rocks, especially in the southern part of the permit boundary.

(Applies to: mineralization) Although no significant concentrations of uranium were found, many areas of metavolcanics contain gossan zones and quartz-rich sedimentary rocks that could be prospected for base metals or gold. That was not the purpose of this survey, however.
