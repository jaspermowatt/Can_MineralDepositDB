Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) - The location point of this area is in the approximate centre of a survey area on the Manitoba side of the Provincial boundary. These surveys included geophysics and geochemistry. This region was chosen because there had been an airborne INPUT survey done in 1968 which indicated strong conductors in the area. Also two calc-silicate boulders on the Saskatchewan side had very high radioactivity counts and it was thought that the belt could continue into Manitoba. Most of the results presented in this report refer to the Saskatchewan side. -The 1980 Wahl (A. Gubins) report (99880) outlines the results of a more extensive geophysical ground survey which provided good picture of the conductors and magnetic patterns.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) - The geophysical surveys in this area involved scintillometer prospecting of boulders and outcrop, EM-16 and magnetics. A few outcrops of calc-silicate or quartz-rich biotite gneiss did give high scintillometer readings of 5000 - 12000 cps. Two strong EM conductors were found in the vicinity of Miller Lake but it was uncertain whether they are due to graphite or sulphides. - The Wahl (A. Gubins) report (99880) discusses, in detail, the conductors outlined using an Apex MaxMin II five frequency EM system and a Scintrex MP-2 proton precession magnetometer. In all, a total of 11 bedrock and at least 6 overburden conductors were identified. Some of these are closely associated with magnetic features. An attempt was made to interpret these features based on the information available.

(Applies to: geochemistry) Samples include lake and soil samples. There were few soil samples taken (24) and analyses indicated fairly normal levels (in ppm) of copper (0-5), uranium (0-7), nickel (3-28), lead (3-31), zinc (3-24), molybdenum (0-10) and silver (0-0.5).
