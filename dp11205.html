Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) This point marks the centre of a large survey area which was covered by airborne and ground geophysics.

(Applies to: property geology) Most of the rocks from this area have been identified as quartz monzonite with varying amounts of accessory minerals such as monazite, apatite and zircon. The distribution of these accessories are presumably the source of the anomalous radioactivity in this area. The drill cores pass through mostly quartz monzonite which contain varying amounts of apatite and monazite.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) The anomalous localized area was identified from an airborne radiometric map. This area was then covered with ground geophysics which included magnetometer, scintillometer and Spectra 44 gamma ray spectrometer as well as radon surveys.

(Applies to: geochemistry) Water and sediment samples were taken from all significant drainage areas and analyzed for uranium and molybdenum. Soil sampling and a radon survey were also conducted in areas where lake geochemistry had indicated high uranium values. Rock samples were taken within the survey area from boulders and outcrops, where available, and assayed for uranium and molybdenum.
