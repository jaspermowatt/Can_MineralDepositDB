Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The project area corresponds to a small inlier of Precambrian, probably Paleoproterozoic, metasedimentary rocks of the Great Island Group which are found along the shore of Hudson's Bay about 26 km east of the town of Churchill, Manitoba. The area contains fairly abundant outcrop and is conveniently accessible by road from Churchill, as it lies near the abandoned Churchill Rocket Range.

(Applies to: property geology) Impure (grey) quartzite in eastern part of survey area grading into bluish-grey subgreywacke to west. Rock is designated on map as 5a2. The north half of the exposure area is shown on the map as 5a1 and consists of pebble beds and medium grained pebble conglomerates.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) Work on this property consisted of a ground radiometric survey using a Scintrex scintillometer. The radiometrics readings are higher in value to the east and increase slightly from south to north across strike. Background readings are generally around 60-70 cps. In the eastern portion, near the rocket range, there is an occurrence in an area about 60 metres long and 2 metres wide which averages 2 - 3 times background. The radioactivity is associated with purplish quartz veinlets about 5 mm thick that may be 6 - 7 times background. These veins make up about 5% of showing area.

(Applies to: mineralization) Several pockets of radioactivity were observed associated with dark, heavy mineral bands ranging in thickness from 0.5 mm to 3 mm. In most cases readings did not exceed twice background, but where heavy mineral concentrations were high, readings of 5 - 6 times background were observed. Most of these pockets were located within the eastern exposures of 5a1. Such occurrences seem to indicate detrital uranium.

(Applies to: structure) The strike of the rocks is generally east-west and dip south between 50 and 70 degrees. Cross-bedding is extensive and indicates that the rocks are right-side up. H.H. Bostock interprets this area as the northern limb of a major syncline.

(Applies to: rock assays) Bird Cove showing described on RJK website, May 2007: chip samples returned an average grade of 1/2 lb U3O8 and 1 to 3 grams gold/ton. The same location produced gold values up to 2.27 grams/ton gold in the early 70's.

(Applies to: property geology) Bird Cove showing described on RJK website, May 2007: mineralization hosted by quartzite and quartz pebble conglomerates (Omarolluk Formation).
