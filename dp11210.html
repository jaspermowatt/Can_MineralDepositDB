Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The most promising showing is referred to as the Donini North showing.

(Applies to: mineralization) There is a high concentration of uraniferous boulders that contain occasional pyrrhotite. A total of 178 rock samples were analyzed, some for uranium and thorium and others uranium, thorium, copper, nickel, zinc, lead, cobalt, arsenic, antimony and molybdenum. Uranium assays were sporadic in nature and reflected the spotty nature of the mineralization. The same can be said of assay results for the other elements. A strong uranium - lead correlation in the south central portion of the permit was confirmed in the analytical results. No lead-bearing mineral was observed in hand specimen or polished section, but in this area, there was a high concentration of uraniferous boulders containing occasional pyrrhotite.

(Applies to: geochemistry) Analytical results show a strong U-Pb association and in places, a strong U-Mo association. Most of the samples were soil taken along a pre-established grid.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) Spectrometer readings were taken along the grid near the mineralized outcrop. The results of these readings are presented in accompanying maps in the report.
