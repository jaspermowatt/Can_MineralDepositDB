Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Prospecting and mapping shows that granitic rock containing spots of high uranium is common in the Moorby Lake area. Angular boulders containing good chalcopyrite mineralization were also found in the area of the drill holes. However, nothing was found in the drill holes themselves.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) Early work in 1977 and 1978 indicated that the Moorby Lake area contained the highest radiometric anomaly in all the Kasmere Permit area. Also, a magnetometer and VLF EM survey was done in which a strong conductor running for many kilometres was detected.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) Drilling indicated that the magnetic anomalies detected in the area are caused by magnetite in quartzites and in granitic rock. The very intense magnetic highs may be locally caused by amphibolite.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) Samples were taken at five metre intervals along the length of both holes. They were assayed for uranium, copper, lead and nickel. Values for copper and nickel are very low. Uranium and lead show somewhat more variation with the highest being 25 ppm.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The program in this area failed to reach its objective of explaining the radiometric anomaly beyond a reasonable doubt. However it added substantially to an understanding of the geology and stratigraphy of the area and indicated the existence of copper-bearing strata in a belt extending from Moorby South through Ronahan to Graves Lake for a total length of about twenty km.
