Qualified comments: (Applies to: geophysical surveys) Magnetometer and electromagnetic EM-16 VLF surveys were conducted on the ground as a follow-up to airborne magnetic and radiometric anomalies. This resulted in finding two dykes, two sets of faults and three magnetic anomalies paralleling the strike of the pelitic rocks. It is thought that these are due to magnetite concentrated in three different horizons.

(Applies to: geochemistry) Spruce twig samples were taken for geochemical analyses. Results were inconclusive indicating several small 1 line anomalies with no association with any structures.

(Applies to: mineralization) Chalcopyrite was found in numerous boulders and 1 outcrop in a zone east of the base line. Chalcopyrite, pyrite and /or pyrrhotite and magnetite occurred as disseminations and as fracture fillings in biotite-rich pelitic gneiss and in more siliceous pelitic rocks. Because of the localization of mineralized boulders, it is thought they are close to source, probably the zone of spotty magnetics along the pelitic gneiss-quartzite contact.

(Applies to: rock assays) Three samples returned values of between 50 and 2000 ppm copper.
