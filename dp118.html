Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The deposit is contained within the Lackner Lake Complex, which consists of peripheral and core nepheline syenites, separated by a partial ring of alkalic mafic rocks (ijolite and malignite). Dikes of magnetite and apatite cut both the mafic and syenitic phases of the complex. Lamprophyre dikes have also been reported. The enclosing country rocks consist of ortho and paragneisses of the Kapuskasing Structural Zone. (Description from Ontario Mineral Deposit Inventory).
