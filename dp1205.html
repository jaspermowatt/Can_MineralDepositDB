Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization in the zone currently being drilled on the Monmouth Property occurs in gently southeast-dipping grey-green diopside skarn that contains bright orange calcite; millimetre-scale uraninite crystals are visible, along with biotite, phlogopite, tremolite, and minor pyrite and molybdenite (Marchand, 2008). The calc-silicate rocks include layers of sandier carbonate, and some local siliceous magnetitic layers (BIF?), and it is overlain with a non-uraniferous bone white, silica bearing carbonate. Thin, fine-grained mafic and felsic dykes intrude the skarn; felsic dykes vary in texture from pegmatitic through medium grained to microcrystalline; mafic dikes are have micaceous altered margins and internal fractures. Pale pink to reddish, medium to medium coarse-grained leucogranite forms the footwall to the mineralized skarn and is in sharp contact with it. Orange to pink calcite patchy alteration and small veinlets, associated with fine-grained uraninite, pyrite, pyrrhotite, and minor magnetite, affect the mineralized skarn.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) While the main target is uraninite mineralization in skarn, the skarn is cut by pegmatite that locally contains uraninite.
