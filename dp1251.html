Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) Gummite stain on contact between Precambrian calc-silicate marble and grey pegmatite dyke. The marble contains disseminated chalcopyrite and pyrrhotite. Pegmatite dykes strike NE and NW (1975 report).

(Applies to: location and access) The occurrence is located on the island near the NW shore of Snyder Lake, approximately 280 km NNE of Lynn Lake.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) Ground and airborne mag, EM and radioactivity surveys were done in 1967, and a 3-channel EM anomaly and a uranium anomaly were found (1975 report). More ground geophysics were done in 1979 (VLF, mag, radon soil gas, scintillometer survey). Minor mag and EM anomalies are not associated with anomalous radioactivity.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) Grab sample assays (1967) yielded 0.02% U3O8, 0.01% Th, 0.01%Cu , 0.02% Ni.

(Applies to: discovery and development) - Exploration Reservation 37 was taken out by Goldray Mines Limited in 1967. Ground and airborne magnetic electromagnetic and radiometric surveys were conducted as part of 'Project SAM' in conjunction with Denison Mines Limited and Roman Corporation Limited. At this location a 3-channel electromagnetic anomaly and a uranium anomaly were found. - The Reservation was reduced in 1969. This portion was dropped. A Manitoba Mines Branch field party visited the occurrence in 1971. Their assays gave only a trace each of Cu, Ni, Pb, Zn and Co. (Weber et al., 1975, Table 11).

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) This property must lie within CanAlaska Uranium's North East Wollaston Project. The relationship must be investigated, and the most pertinent geological observations unravelled.
