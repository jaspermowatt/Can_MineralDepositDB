Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The showings occur in a 60 foot wide zone of alternating bands of uraniferous pegmatite and biotite schist which extends for about 10 miles.

(Applies to: mineralization) The radioactive zone corresponds to yellow-weathering pegmatite. Pegmatite zones occur near porphyritic granodiorite and gneissic granodiorite, and are associated with altered Keewatin volcanics and sediments (Manitoba Mineral Resources Library).

(Applies to: discovery and development) - The showings were first staked as Found No. 1 and Found No. 2 in 1948. Radioactivity as much as 5-6 times background was reported. and pitchblende or uraninite was identified. In 1950, a chemical analysis of a pegmatite sample containing some secondary uranium minerals and small grains of sparsely disseminated uraninite yielded 0.051% U3O8. After concentration, a microscopic examination of the first sample showed uraninite, pyrite, pyrrhotite, hornblende and some quartz. A spectrographic analysis of the superpan tip indicated a very small amount of yttrium and ytterbium. - In 1950, the Whiteshell Uranium Syndicate made a crude map showing the position of radioactive outcrops on Found No. 1, 9, 13, 14, and 16 mining claims. A. H. Lang described these occurrences as part of the East Found Group in 1952, and reported nine select samples that yielded up to 0.70% U3O8 . G. D. Springer (1952) reported two selected samples from Found No. 9 with beta activities equivalent to 0.006-0.08% U3O8; heavy minerals were identified as uraninite, thorite, zircon and apatite. X-ray photographs indicated 70% uraninite-30% thorite. Four selected samples yielded 0.008, 0.014, 0.07, and 0.70% U3O8, and a 700-gram sample was panned to a 1.2 gram concentrate assaying 43.2% U3O8. - The showings were restaked several times in the 1950s, but dropped in 1958. Details can be found in the Manitoba Mineral Resources Library.
