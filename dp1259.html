Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) - The showings occur in persistent pegmatite zones (Springer, 1952, p. 20) which lie near porphyritic granodiorite and gneissic granodiorite and are associated with altered Keewatin volcanics and sediments. On the Found and Found No. 2 M.C., the radioactive zones consist of dirty yellowish green weathering medium grained pegmatite. On the Found No. 3 M.C., the radioactive zones coincide with pink feldspar, dark smoky quartz, and abundant biotite with minor pyrite, pyrrhotite, and molybdenite. A reconnaissance radioactivity survey was undertaken in 1954, resulting in the delineation of anomalies which were then sampled. Uranium values were erratic, and suggest a pegmatitic distribution and/or large massive areas of low radioactivity. - In 1955 after Eco Explorations showed some interest in the properties, they were assigned back to the Whiteshell Uranium Syndicate, and cancelled a year later.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Discovery and development details are reported in the Manitoba Mineral Resources Library.

(Applies to: location and access) The deposits are located approximately five miles west of Caddy Lake, and in the immediate vicinity of Highway No. 44.

(Applies to: regional geology) The deposit occurs in a 60 foot wide zone for about 10 miles and this is the same zone of alternating bands of pegmatite and biotite schist in which U1 and U2 occur.
