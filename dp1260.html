Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The property is located approximately 4.8 km east of Lamprey Falls on the Winnipeg River.

(Applies to: property geology) - The showing coincides with a pegmatite and numerous dykes and sills which intrude Archean andesites, basalts, and some minor tuffs. The pegmatite is complexly zoned, with the lithium-bearing minerals occurring in the central portion of the pegmatite. - The central, most economic, portion of the pegmatite consists of lepidolite surrounded by an envelope of spodumene and quartz. A K-Ar date for lepidolite is given as 2480 million years (Bannatyne, 1985). A few masses of tantalite-columbite, up to 5 cm across, were reported in one lepidolite outcrop (Stockwell, 1933). Spodumene is known to outcrop over a 334 m2 (3600 sq ft) area (Wright, 1926). Outside of this core is a zone of banded aplite, and a narrow rim of albite granite forms the lower part of the trough. A quartz-feldspar-muscovite rock makes up the upper part of the body (Springer, 1950).

(Applies to: mineralization) - According to Cerny et al. (1986, p. 503): "the Silverleaf offshoot consists largely of garnetiferous aplite with a large rounded pod of Li-, Rb-, Mn-, Nb-, Ta-, Be-, Sn-, and F-rich pegmatite. The pod consists of microcline-perthite, quartz, albite, lithian muscovite, lepidolite, spessartine, cesian beryl, petalite, spodumene, topaz, cassiterite, manganocolumbite - manganotantalite, triphylite - lithiophilite, and amblygonite - montebrasite, with rare apatite, tourmaline and sphalerite". Other minerals reported include fluorite, zinnwaldite, radioactive minerals, rare gahnite and monazite.

(Applies to: discovery and development) - The property was staked as Bear in 1924 and developed into the early 1930s, with minor production, and explored intermittently thereafter. The area was also explored for tantalum later between 1976 and 1984. Claims were dropped in 1986. Details are reported in the Manitoba Minerals Resources Library.

(Applies to: structure) Drilling in 1954 (Lithium Corporation of Canada) confirmed a tabular pegmatite body dipping 30° S, forming a gentle trough extending 168 m (550 ft) westward into a swamp (Davies, 1957), and ranging in thickness from 5 to 11 m over a drilled length of 245 m; minor Li-bearing zones were intersected (Bannatyne, 1985, p. 24). The western extension of the pegmatite was indicated in seven of the thirteen holes but was eroded in the other locations drilled.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) In 1971, Hudson Bay Exploration and Development Company Limited did a helicopter EM and radiometric survey over the area under Airborne Permit No. 93, and some anomalies were detected (Non-confidential Assessment File No. 91686).

(Applies to: production) Spodumene and lepidolite, along with other industrial minerals were produced between 1927 and 1928. These minerals were reported in stockpiles on Garden Bay (Bannatyne, 1985, p. 24).
