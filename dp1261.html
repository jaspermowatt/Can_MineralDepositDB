Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The deposit is located 0.8 km northwest of Greer Lake, l.6 km southwest of the Winnipeg River, and 9.65 km south of Provincial Road No. 315.

(Applies to: mineralization) - The showing coincides with a pegmatite which is crudely zoned, with patches of garnetiferous aplite and graphic albite plus quartz along its margins, medium- to coarse-grained albite and blocky microcline-perthite in its interior, and a segmented quartz core. Minerals include microcline-perthite, oligoclase, albite, quartz, muscovite, biotite, two types of chlorite, epidote, garnet, topaz, beryl, titanite, zircon, thorite, uraninite, magnetite, niobian rutile, fersmite, euxenite (?), columbite-tantalite, microlite, apatite, monazite, bavenite, bityite, bertrandite, chalcopyrite, pyrite, uranophane-sklodowskite, nontronite, mixed-layer montmorillonite-illite, Fe-hydroxide minerals, and an unknown Ca, Nb, Ta-bearing phase, clinozoizite, sphene, beryl, topaz and uraninite.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The showing was first staked in 1925 and intermittent production of industrial concentrates are reported. 277 kg concentrated tantalite was produced in 1930, and, later, feldspar for roofing materials and poultry grit. Details can be found in the Manitoba Mineral Resources Library.
