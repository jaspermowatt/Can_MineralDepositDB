Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The Spence No. 7, 8, 9 (W21463, 4, 5) (hereafter referred to as Spence group) are located about 45 air miles NE of Lac du Bonnet and approximately 8½ miles NE of Provincial Road No. 314.

(Applies to: property geology) The oldest rocks in the area are part of the Archean Rice Lake Group, and predominantly consists of andesite and basalt schists with sedimentary inclusions of quartzite and greywacke. This complex was later intruded by a pink to grey granite. Three pegmatite dykes intrude the quartzites and strike approximately N45°W. The dykes are exposed for a length of 1200’ along the strike with a total possible length of 3200’ ft (Non-confidential Assessment Files).

(Applies to: discovery and development) - The Spence group was first staked by A. Johnston in 1955. The same year it was progressively assigned to J.W. Resthall and H.W. Cooper, Middle-West Exploration Ltd., and C.W. Bay. M.G. Smerchanski was hired in late 1955 to map in detail (Scale 1" = 200’) a portion of the Match-Spence group of claims. The pegmatite outcrop on the Spence group was found to be radioactive and its surface stained yellow (Smerchanski, Non-confidential assessment files). Additional work was recommended but not completed and the claims were cancelled in 1958. - No staking was reported in the area until John Ziyone staked Claim Block 929 (C.B. 929) in 1969. This claim block was cancelled in 1971.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) A detailed scintillometer survey was carried out on the Pitch, Hoehne, and Blend claims which lie approximately 1½ miles NE of Spence No. 9. Several large scale anomalies were reported from assessment work in 1954-55.
