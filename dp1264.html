Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) These are two showings are located about 2 miles apart on the SW shore of Manigotagan Lake. The northern deposit is about 9 miles S of Rice Lake and 4 1/2 miles SE of the end of a branch Northern Deposit - Agassiz Option.

(Applies to: property geology) - Both showings are situated within very coarse grained pink pegmatite of Precambrian Manigotagan Gneissic Belt. - Northern Deposit: The pegmatite dyke contains unaltered fine-grained gneiss bands and ribbons. The radioactive zone is quite wide (15 + feet) and several hundred feet long. Radioactivity occurs both in pegmatite and unaltered gneiss bands (Agassiz Mines Limited; Corporation File; Manitoba Mines Branch). Another radioactive showing has been reported 1 1/2 miles NE of the Northern Deposit. - Southern Deposit: This large pink pegmatite on surface contains bands of unaltered gneiss. Drill hole logs indicate an assemblage of alternating pink pegmatite and biotite gneiss at depth (Mink No's 6 and 8; Non-confidential Assessment File; Manitoba Mines Branch).

(Applies to: mineralization) The uranium is occurs in carnotite - gummite.

(Applies to: discovery and development) This property was first staked and investigated, drilled with poor results, between 1954 and 1957. It was restaked intermittently between 1960 and 1974. A detailed account of exploration can be found in the Manitoba Minerals Resources Library.
