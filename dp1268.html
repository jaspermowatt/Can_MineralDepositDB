Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The showing is located on the N side of Shethanei Lake, about 8½ km W of the Steel River and 210 km W of Churchill

(Applies to: property geology) - The showing is sited within a Precambrian alaskite unit which contains inclusions of paragneiss. The alaskite is very coarse grained, pinkish white and high in feldspar. Individual crystals of feldspar are up to 46 cm in diameter, many with graphic intergrowths of quartz. Biotite is rare and small amounts of muscovite are present. - The contact between the alaskite and the inclusions is frequently marked by 5-10 cm where biotite is abundant. The highest radioactivity readings were found from these biotite stringers.

(Applies to: rock assays) In 1955, a sample taken from the showing where the highest Geiger counter readings had been obtained, yielded 0.01% U308 by radiometric equivalent and 0.008% U308 by a fluorimetric analysis (Unpublished Information Files; Mineral Administration, Manitoba Mineral Resources Division).
