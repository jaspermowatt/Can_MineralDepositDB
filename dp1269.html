Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The deposit is located on the S side of a small lake, roughly mid-way between, and 3 km W of Mennell Lake and Parsons Lake. It is approximately 370 km NNW of Lynn Lake.

(Applies to: discovery and development) - A thorium-uranium anomaly (Anomaly C) about 900 m long by 400 m wide was delineated with an airborne scintillometer survey over Exploration Reservation 60, and was confirmed in a follow-up ground scintillometer survey. A magnetic anomaly was found during ground work. Samples from frost-heaved boulders yielded 0.7% (14 lbs/ton) uranium in assays (Reservations 60; Non-confidential Assessment Files). In 1969, 19 diamond drill holes totalling 3 041 m tested Anomaly C. A 5 foot (1.5 m) section of the richest part (30 foot section) of DDH Y-4 assayed 9.8 lbs/ton U3O8 (Reservation 60; Non-confidential Assessment Files). Other drill holes yielded assays of up to 0.13. U3O8, but widths were less than 10 ft. (3 m). Molybdenite was found in some drill holes. Assays ranged from nil to 1.14% MoS2, the latter across 3 feet (.91 m), with many assays from 0.01% to 0.03% MoS2 across various widths. - The ground was reopened for staking in 1971, and W.B. Dunlop staked C.B. 7122 over the deposit in 1975. More exploration detail can be found in the Manitoba Mineral Resources Library.

(Applies to: property geology) Uraninite and molybdenite occurs in Precambrian sills of white, coarse-grained to pegmatitic quartz monzonite within pelitic gneiss (which forms a keel in grey gneiss). The quartz monzonite appears crushed in places. It is cut by pink pegmatite, which in turn may be related to a deep quartz monzonite (Weber et al., 1975). Copper and nickel have been reported (Yukon Antimony Corporation Files).
