Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) - The Hump showing is hosted by gneissic granodiorites that were fractured. The fractures are filled with quartz stockwork, which consists of milky quartz cut by hematite seams and by narrow veins of second generation quartz. Mineralization occurs as pitchblende, yellow uranium staining, and hematite, while locally there is green copper staining in the fractures at or near fracture intersections.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The Hump showing has been trenched, soil sampled, and radiometrically surveyed. In June, 1977, the trenches and outcrop areas were scanned with McPhar TV-1A spectrometers. Overburden areas were tested on a grid pattern with EDA Radon Gas in soils, RD 200 manometers. The radiometric survey gave values of 275, 110, 780, 1885, 2800, 825, 185, 2440, 1305 counts per minute for Uranium. Soil samples were collected at intervals of 100 feet along the picket lines used for the preceding radon gas survey. These samples provided anomalous areas of uranium and copper. This was followed by an expansions of the soil sampling area by extending the base line 1000 feet, however all of these samples were destroyed by a fire in the lab. Assayed rock samples taken from trenches gave results of 0.050% U3O8, 0.001% U3O8, 0.059% U3O8, 0.025% U3O8; 0.13% Cu, 10.67%Cu, 0.17%Cu, 0.15%Cu; and 0.04%silver, 0.24% silver. (Applies to: discovery and development) During 1978, work performed on these claims included establishing a grid for survey control, geological mapping of the outcrop area, a soil sampling program over an area approximately 1680 m by about 800 m and collected a total of 523 samples, a Ronka EM-16 survey over 75% of the grid and a preliminary bedrock sampling program using a Winkie diamond drill. Five drill holes and some trenching was done on the area. Chip samples gave results of 0.096%, 0.094%, 0.200%, 0.019%, 0.007% U3O8. Comment on AR 80928 in NORMIN.DB.
