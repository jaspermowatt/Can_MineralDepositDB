Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The FXO (Depno 1842) and UGI claims (originally staked as the DV claims) were staked in the 1954 to cover radioactive anomalies discovered in the DeVries Lake area. Pitchblende, uraninite, and allanite mineralization is commonly associated with magnetite and hematite in the groundmass of intrusive and extrusive feldspar porphyries, along joints and fractures cutting the porphyries, in amphibolite pods, and along contacts between porphyry and gneiss (Mineral Industry Report, 1975). Assays as high as 11.8% U3O8 have been attained from the FXO property. Mineralization on both claims is generally discontinuous and sporadic. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 18, 2007.

(Applies to: mineralization) Magnetite-rich hydrothermal veins containing pitchblende, uraninite and minor secondary uranium-bearing oxide mineralization are hosted in intrusion-related tension fractures. Radioactive mineralization is also concentrated in small siliceous pockets in the groundmass of quartz feldspar porphyry (FXO claims) and amphibolite pods (UGI claims). High-grade samples from the FXO claims returned assays of 4.65% U3O8 (grab), and 10.5% U3O8 and 11.8% U3O8 (trench). Mineralization on the UGI claims is more extensive, but of lower grade; one grab sample assayed 0.220 % U3O8 while the average grade was 0.03% U3O8, an assay of 0.07% U3O8 over 1.5 m was intersecting drill hole DV-2. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 18, 2007.

(Applies to: structure) Uranium-bearing mineralization is hosted in feldspar porphyry intrusion-related tension fractures and siliceous pockets, or in small amphibolite pockets in extrusive feldspar porphyry. Regional faulting appears to have no relation to uranium mineralization on either the FXO or UGI claims. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 18, 2007.
