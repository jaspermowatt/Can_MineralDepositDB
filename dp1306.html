Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The property is located along the north shore of east arm of Beaverlodge Lake, which is at the south end of much larger Hottah Lake. It is on the middle part of a topographically prominent quartzite ridge that trends northeast and is 15 km long. To the northeast are the Bingo group of claims located at the south end of Hottah Lake. The quartzite contains veinlets and irregular masses of quartz, and also a lens of conglomerate and talc-sericite schist about 40 feet wide and 150 feet long (Lang, 1952, p. 58). Pitchblende-bearing quartz veins and stringers were found in a conglomerate lens on the ridge top during 1930s over a strike length of 80 feet. The property was explored by two deep trenches and a shaft about 50 feet deep. Most of the pitchblende is removed from the site most likely for a bulk sample of 1.5 tons sent for testing at Mines Branch in Ottawa. About a mile to the southwest discontinuous lenses about a foot wide and up to 8 feet long, in quartzite, contain some pitchblende.
