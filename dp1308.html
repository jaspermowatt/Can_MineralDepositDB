Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Pitchblende veins occur in Pitch 8 to 10, and along with adjacent claims Pitch 29-30 make up the claim group held by the Indore Gold mines Ltd. One of this claims is reported to include a radioactive occurrence found by Mr. S. Campbell in 1934. The claims are located about 7 miles (11 km) north of the south end of Hottah Lake, in the Beverly Bay area. Pitchblende veins occur intermittently along both contacts of the steep diabase dyke, which trends 030° and is traceable for 183 m (Lang, 1952, p. 59-60). They comprise pitchblende with hematite and quartz in vein zones up to 25 cm wide, and samples from them returned chemical assays of up to 11 % U3O8. They were explored by several trenches, and 8 diamond drill holes, then in view of possible poor core recovery it was decided to explore the mineralization from an adit. The underground work about 50 ft below the surface exposure, included 68 feet of cross-cutting and 130 feet of drifting (Fig. 4, Lang, 1952, p. 60; Thorpe, 1970, p. ). Lenses of pitchblende up to 3 inches (7.5 cm) wide were encountered, and lower grade disseminated mineralization in the dyke also as wide. One seem of pitchblende in the drift averaged about 5 cm in width over a length of 4 m (13 ft). The underground work began in January 1951, and production rate was 50 tons of ore milled per day during 1952 to 1955. The total tonnage and grade figures are not available. The owner at the time of closer was United Uranium Corp. Ltd. (McGlynn, 1971, p. 115; Strand, 1996).
