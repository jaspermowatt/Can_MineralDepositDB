Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The El Bonanza property is located on east shore of Great Bear Lake, and is about 10 km southwest of Port Radium. It was originally staked in 1931 by G. A. LaBine and B.C. St. Paul, and was owned by a subsidiary of the original Eldorado company, called the El Bonanza Mining Corporation Limited. The early work on the property was done in 1935-'36 (Thorpe, 1966, p. 50). It included drilling and sinking a shaft inclined at 68° in direction 210° to a depth of 160 feet (~ 50 m). Two levels were developed from it. An old adit, from which an internal shaft was sunk to an unknown depth, was driven eastward from a point approximately 600 feet (183 m) from the main workings. A high grade silver vein about 3 inches (7.5 cm) wide was reportedly encountered at a depth of 30 feet (10 m) in the internal shaft. The high grade ore was mined and hand cobbed, and the ore and the rejects were sent to the Eldorado mill. This trial shipment yielded 30175 ounces of silver from 6610 pounds of ore. Minor indications of uranium on the property e.g.., pitchblende in ore dump (Feniak, 1947), resulted in takeover of the property by the crown corporation viz., Eldorado Mining and Refining Limited. during the war time in 1944. The takeover lasted until 1950 when the property was returned to the control of private parties (Lang, 1952, p. 50). During 1956-'57 a long drift was driven from the second level at depth of 160 feet (~ 49 m). The property was reactivated in March 1965 when ice extended to a depth of 80 feet. The main work done was on the first level where a drift was driven southeast of shaft for 250 feet. Ore grade material was encountered for the first 50 feet in the drift, followed by a long barren section and then another silver-bearing section (Thorpe, 1966). Estimated 300 tons of ore was added to the older stockpile bringing its total to about 1000 tons. No ore was shipped from the property during the year. The property was held by Nightwatch Resources during mid-1970s (Pasieka, 1977). In 1988 Octan Resources Inc. of Calgary carried out trenching, and magnetic, VLF-EM and horizontal loop EM surveys (Hoefer and Magrum, 1988). In addition diamond drilling of 15 holes totalling 3661 feet (1116 m) was done to test the down-dip and strike extension of the known mineralization. One of the drill hole returned assays as high as 133.4 oz/ton Ag over 8 feet to as low as 23.32 oz/ton Ag over 14.5 feet. A 1500 pound bulk sample was removed for flotation test. It assayed 352.6 oz/ton Ag, and the test showed overall 99 % recovery of silver by combination of gravity separation and flotation.
