Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Lang (1952, p. 55-56) reported that the occurrence was briefly described by Kidd (1933, p. 26) and Lord (1941, p. 55) as a fracture zone up to 1 foot wide and traceable for 50 feet in a diabase dyke cutting granite, located between Bow and Contact Lakes. It was first staked as 'Thompson Group' of claims (Kidd ibid), and was restaked as the 'Pitch 1 to 4 group' in 1948, and was acquired soon after by Indore Gold Mines Limited. The zone contains quartz stringers carrying pyrite, chalcopyrite, bornite, cobalt bloom and uranium stain. The company drilled 3 diamond drill holes totalling 205 feet, but did not find significant mineralization. Mumin el al. (2007, p. 202) recently described as the most spectacular of high-grade veins previously prospected along the Contact Lake belt. It comprises a series of veinlets, stringers and semi-massive veins of chalcopyrite, bornite and hematite along with quartz, carbonate and pyrite, with malachite and erythrite. they reported complete analyses of 5 grab samples that were consistently polymetallic with metal contents up to 31.32 weight % Cu, 6.2 g/t Au, 493 g/t Ag, 0.63 wt.% Co, 0.37 wt.% U, 0.41 wt.% Bi, and 0.20 wt.% Zn.
