Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) According to Lang et al. (1962, p. 196) the first claims were staked in 1931, and from 1936-'39 a 25-ton mill was operated intermittently by Bear Exploration and Radium Limited. Lord (1941, p. 50) reported that the total production up to the end of 1939 was 6933 pounds of U3O8 and 348250 ounces of silver extracted from 10079 tons of ore, plus some crude silver was shipped in 1934. In 1942 the claims were bought by International Uranium Mining Company Limited, which did additional exploration during 1944-'49 including radiometric and geological surveys, 15300 feet of diamond drilling, and a little underground work. Late in 1949 the company was organized as Acadia Uranium mines Limited, and drilled below the ore shoot from which previous production was obtained. The exploration stopped in 1950. An estimate in 1950s stated that the remaining ore above the second level totalled 3000 tons averaging 100 oz/t Ag and 1 % U3O8, and that a similar tonnage lies above the stoped area on the third level. The shaft and an adit are at the west of No. 1 zone which trends east for about 100 m and about 75 m farther east are No. 2 and 3 zones which are about 60 m apart at their west end. The No. 2 zone then trend east for 450 m and No. 3 zone trends for 250 m. No. 1 zone is about 1.5 m wide, and is exposed on the surface. It has been followed underground for 335 m to the east where it meets No. 3 zone that trends northeast.
