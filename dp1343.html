Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs in breccia interstices and narrow shear zones in dolomite or in dark chloritic/graphitic slates. The main zone is several hundred feet long and up to 5 feet wide. Brecciation appears to be a collapse feature, and breccia fragments are angular and of variable size. Interstices vary in width from micron size to several millimetres (Assessment Report 081318).

(Applies to: property geology) The claim property is underlain by massive and laminated dolomite, carbonaceous mudstone, graphitic slates and thin bedded dolomite of the Lower Aphebian Union Island Group. This sequence is in fault contact with Archean granites to the southwest, and on the south is overlain unconformably by arkosic sandstone and conglomerate of the Et-then Formation. The Union Island Group rocks strike northeast and dip 60 to 70 degrees southeast. At the northeast end of the property the rocks strike to the northwest where they have been drag-folded along the MacDonald Fault (Assessment Report 081318).
