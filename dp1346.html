Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) This group of claims comprises 6 surveyed claims at Glacier Bay, adjoining the Eldorado holdings to the east (Lang, 1952, p. 55). They were owned by New Athona Mines (1937) Ltd., and were prospected for uranium and silver in 1931-'33. A promising silver vein was found, but prospecting methods did not permit determination of radioactivity at that time. A shaft was sunk on the silver vein to a depth of 125 feet. A shaft shown on map by Robinson (1971, Fig. 2, p. 14) and Mursky (1973, coloured map in pocket) at the end of a small bay on the south side of Port Radium is the most likely location of this 125 feet deep shaft. Robinson's figure also shows a an adit southwest of the shaft close to shore, which was likely used for mining. Lang reported that strong radioactivity was detected at one place by a Geological Survey of Canada party in 1944.
