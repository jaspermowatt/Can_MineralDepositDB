Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Radioactive in biotite paragneiss and concordant granite pegmatite. Radioactive zone at least 579 m long and from 15 to 76 m wide.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) Airborne radiometric survey in 1967 outlined some radioactive anomalies which were in subsequent years explored by ground radiometric surveys and sampling.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Airborne radiometric surveys outlined the anomalies and followed by ground radiometrics. U and Th occur in a zone at east 1900 feet long, 50-250 feet wide with an average grade of 25 samples 1.16 lbs/ton U3O8 and 0.54 lbs/ton ThO2. Highest grade sample was 6.80 lb/ton U3O8 and 1.08 lb/ton ThO2.

(Applies to: mineralization) Main radioactive mineral is uraninite, many outcrops are stained yellow with secondary U minerals.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) 25 selected samples from the anomalous zone, where there was yellow-green secondary uranium minerals staining, averaged 0.058% U3O8 and 0.027% ThO2.

(Applies to: mineralization) Radioactivity in fresh pegmatite caused by uraninite. Secondary yellow-green uranium minerals stain 40% of the showing.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) An average grade of 25 samples 1.16 lbs/ton U3O8 and 0.54 lbs/ton ThO2. Highest grade sample was 6.80 lb/ton U3O8 and 1.08 lb/ton ThO2.
