Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The showing was discovered in early 1950s and sampling and trenching were done. The best assay, obtained from Trench 1 assayed 0.39 % U3O8. In 1956 Star Uranium Ltd. acquired the property and completed 2001 ft of diamond drilling in 9 holes.

(Applies to: mineralization) Anomalous radioactivity associated with migmatite. The main radioactive zone traced almost continuously for 1800 ft and conforms closely to the strike. Sampling shows that the radioactivity is associated with the granitic bands, and higher percentage of U3O8 was found in the proximity of the narrow quartz veins.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) Of the 9 diamond holes drilled, except 2 intersected radioactivity of varying widths and intensity, however values were generally sub-ore. The best values were 0.23 ft over 1 ft, 0.14 %/1 ft and 0.10%/2 ft. Drilling indicated that the main zone continued to depth showing remarkable consistency.

(Applies to: rock assays) 18 trenches were blasted and 82 samples were assayed and mostly gave sub-ore assays.

(Applies to: rock descriptions) Highly altered rocks of Tazin Group - schists, phyllites with granitic bands - of Archean age occurs on the property. This forms the basement for the Paleoproterozoic Nonacho basin supracrustal rocks in the area.

(Applies to: location and access) The location of the showing is taken approximately at the boundary of Rosa 3 and Rosa 4 claims (Ref ID5000002).
