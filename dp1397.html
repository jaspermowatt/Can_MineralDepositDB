Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The Pyramid showing is defined by a 400 m long, northeast trending zone with surface uranium and copper mineralization located 100 m south of the southern tip of MacInnis Lake, in the center of the KULT14 mineral claim. Mineralization occurs in 2 X 50 cm wide, 40 m long bands, 25 m apart, in veins, fractures and disseminations in a northeast trending mylonite zone. The zone is on the west side of a north trending amphibolite band and surrounded by granitic gneiss. The mylonite is composed of 40 percent quartz, 25 percent sericite, 20 percent fine disseminated chlorite and 9 percent hematite, accessory are calcite, pyrite and Ti-oxide. The amphibolite is up to 200 m wide and varies in character from a weakly foliated gabbroic rock to a highly chloritized sheared mafic rock and is intruded by a quartz-rich, potassic granite. Foliation in the amphibolite trends north and commonly dips vertically or steeply to the west. Pyrite and minor chalcopyrite occur disseminated throughout the amphibolite.
