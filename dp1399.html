Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) This prospect was discovered in 1954 and was optioned to Iso Uranium Mines Ltd., who did geological mapping, prospecting and blasted 10 trenches on two radioactive shear zones 60-70 m apart. Shell Canada acquired the property in 1974 from Consolidated Shunsby Mines Ltd. and 1 hole drilled with X-ray drill but no radioactivity in the drill core was found.

(Applies to: mineralization) Pitchblende mineralization associated with calcite and hematite in sheared and fractured basal conglomerate of Paleoproterozoic Nonacho Group. There are two, roughly parallel mineralized zones about 200 ft apart, which strike about north-south. The zones are exposed for 300 and 400 ft, follow narrow shear zones, and have mineralized widths up to 3 ft. Scattered euhedral pyrite occurs within the arkosic matrix and carry Au values. PNC Exploration Co's in 1983 found an anomalous zone of 100m x 100 m in the oligomictic conglomerate east of the mylonite zone which occurs along the contact between basement complex and Nonacho Group.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) PNC Exploration Co. did mapping, prospecting and drilling from 1983-85. Fourteen holes totalling 1889.3 m were drilled to confirm the downward extension of the surface mineralization. Four drill holes - TK82-18, 19, 20 and 34 intersected discontinuous and weak mineralization, which consists of radioactive fractures associated with hematite, chlorite and calcite in veins or breccia. Four holes north of the showing (TK-21 to 23 and 35) to confirm northward extension of the anomalous zone, 3 encountered weak anomalies.
