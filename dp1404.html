Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Giant Yellowknife staked in 1954 SOL - 1-70 claims on this occurrence and trenching was done. In 1955 the work on the property included geological and geophysical surveys, 9 X-ray diamond drill holes totalling about 1000 ft. The vein quartz and associated hematite was encountered in drill holes but the core showed no evidence of radioactive minerals.

(Applies to: mineralization) The claims are underlain by arkoses, conglomerates, greywacke and quartzites of Lower Proterozoic Nonacho Group sediments. A fracture dipping 60-80 degrees north cuts the steeply dipping greywacke with minor arkose and is associated with much shearing. The strongest radioactivity is in cherty quartzite. McGlynn (Ref ID 889) reported that radioactivity occurs with shears in metamorphosed greywacke (biotite schist) close to the granitized sediments of the basement rocks .
