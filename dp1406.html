Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Geological mapping, ground radiometric and magnetometer surveys followed by sampling, trenching and diamond drilling (16 DH totalling 1900 m) were done by Canadian Superior Exploration Ltd. in 1969-70 to prospect the anomalies found in glacial boulder drift on the east side of the Jo lake and west side in between Jo Lake and Hjalmar Lake. The GSC conducted a lake sediment survey in 1975, and Canadian Occidental Petroleum Ltd. conducted airborne radiometric survey in 1976. In 1978 Kelvin Energy Ltd. optioned Kay 1-10 claims and staked WEB 1-5 and KEE1-27 and conducted geochemical and geophysical surveys.

(Applies to: mineralization) Radiometric anomalies associated with boulders of glacial drift were found at many places on the east side of Jo Lake. Sampling followed by trenching and diamond drilling were done to prospect the U mineralization. An area of about 400 m x 150 m on east side of Jo Lake was outlined where anomalous syenite was encountered in 6 drill holes and the bedrock source of the boulders was determined. The assays of drill core averaged only 0.02% U3O8 over small widths, hence the prospect was considered non-economic.

(Applies to: rock assays) Four 50 lb bulk sample of boulders close to Jo Lake shoreline were assayed - 0.047%, 0.238%, 0.071% and 0.073% U3O8. The trench samples ranged 1.20 % to 0.02% U3O8 across width of 2" - 24".
