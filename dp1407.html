Qualified comments: (Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) In 1966 6 diamond drill holes totalling 1021 ft were completed on ONA group at Nonacho Lake. Capillano exploration ltd. (restaked as NON Group) drilled 6 holes totalling 1264 ft but no assays available. Kikuchi (Ref ID 3513) reports Drill hole 69-10 was the best with 9 enriched intersections from 27 to 122 ft ranging from 2 ft to 5 ft. and the assays were given in mR units.

(Applies to: discovery and development) This property was originally staked and worked by Eldorado Mining and refining Ltd. in 1955 as NONA group. Mastodon-Highland bell Mines ltd. restaked the property in 1966 as Ona claim group. In 1968 Capillano Explorations Ltd. acquired the property and staked as NONA 1-36 claims and drilled 6 holes (1264 ft).

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) Drill hole 3 (Mastodon-Highland Bell Mines Ltd.) intersected mineralization between 2-102 ft with assays ranging 0.022 to 0.207 % U3O8. Hole No. 1 about 400 ft N-NE of DH 3, returned assays ranging from 0.016 to 0.088 % U3O8 between 20-28 ft and 82-102 ft.

(Applies to: mineralization) Pitchblende occurs in fine fractures in greywacke in an area 1000 ft x 200 ft over a length of 125 ft in a 2-3 ft thick bed. Arkose was mentioned as intersected in drill holes cut by narrow quartz and carbonate stringers and is brecciated in few places. Hematite occurs in some fractures and shear zones cutting the Nonacho sediments. Drill hole intersections of uraniferous veins containing, quartz, Kspar, chlorite and pyrite in sandstone / greywacke.
