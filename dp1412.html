Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) 1) In 1950 prospectors found this radioactive occurrence and staked GM 1-12 claims on 3 zones. 2) 1952- American Yellowknife Mines Ltd. concentrated their efforts on the most promising Zone 1 and did mapping, sampling and 16 small trenches. 3) Eldorado Mining and Refineries staked AG and DONPAR claims 4) In 1967 the ground was staked by Territorial Expeditors as CC claims and did some trenching, drilling and sampling. Thus this property was staked every time there was a uranium rush but never enough work done to evaluate it. 5) Kelmet Resources and Comaplex Minerals Corp. staked the same ground as LABELLE claims in 1988 to protect this old U-Ni-Au-Ag showing and sampled for evaluating Au, Ag, Pt, Pd

(Applies to: mineralization) U-Ni-Co-Au-Ag showing occurs in a series of fractures in quartz diorite to quartz monzonite laccoliths of Caribou group of laccoliths, which is surrounded by Stark Megabreccia. Territorial Expeditors found two zones - Zone A is more promising then Zone B, which is 870 m to the south and weakly radioactive.

(Applies to: rock assays) The Trench sample by Territorial Expeditors assayed up to Au 0.16 oz/t, Ag 3.6 oz/t, U3O8 7.00 % (equivalent), Co 12.19 %, Ni 5.83 %. Some significant assays by Kelmet Resources / Comaplex Minerals Ltd. are: Au 10 ppb, Ag 11.o ppm, Pt < 30 ppb, Pd 900 ppb, Radioactivity Total 33400 cps.
