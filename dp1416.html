Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) Zones A, B, C, D and E occur almost along east - west direction north of Regina Bay of Stark Lake, east arm of Great Slave Lake. With respect to the Zone C, Zone A is 850 m to the east; Zone B is 375 m to the east; Zone D is 200 m to the west; and Zone E is 680 m to the west.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) On "C" vein 15 drill holes totalling 3300 ft were put in 1950, the uraniferous intersections sampled ranged from few to 28 inches widths and the assay values ranged from 2.99 % to 0.01 % U3O8, averaged 0.63 % U3O8. The deepest intersection was 170 ft below the surface.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The Rex Group of 24 claims were staked in 1949. Surface and underground (1949-52) exploration included trenching, bulk sampling, 15 diamond drill holes, a 100 ft cross cut adit on Vein C and a 453 ft drift mostly along the vein. Hudson Bay Oil and Gas Co. Ltd. acquired the Rex Property in 1969 and conducted a radiometric survey.

(Applies to: mineralization) There are 5 main showings, A, B, C, E and Stevens veins all with similar mineralogy in quartz diorite or monzonite laccoliths. The mineralization consists of actinolite, tremolite veins in acicular growths growing perpendicular to the vein walls. Midway from the walls apatite, and fine grained uraninite occur. Monazite is present in trace amounts. In addition to the veins there are narrow joint fillings 1/2 to 1/16 inched wide. The C vein is the best on the property, 570 ft long, and width varies from 54-18 inches. The Stevens vein is 110 ft long and has a maximum width of 6 ft. Veins A and B are more irregular. B vein was traced at intervals for 800 ft, and Vein was traced intermittently for 800 ft also with average width of 1 ft for 200 ft.

(Applies to: regional geology) The Rex or Ridley Property veins are hosted by Regina Bay laccolith, which is one of the 20 cogenetic laccolithic intrusions of dioritic-monzonitic composition that extends along the 225 km length of the east arm of Great Slave Lake. These intrusions were emplaced along the contact of Pethei Group limestone and the overlying megabreccias of the Stark Formation that belong to the Aphebian Great Slave Lake Supergroup.
