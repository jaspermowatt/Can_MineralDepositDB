Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Zone 5 trends NE but radioactivity is discontinuous and consists of a series of lenses which at most are 30 metres in length. Secondary and primary uranium minerals (Vestor identified uranothorite) occur in fractures in the grit, many which strike at 72 degrees. The host rock is a cross bedded pebbly (quartz pebble) grit which has been hematized and partially leached (AR 082915). Irregular quartz veinlets, up to 2 cm wide, and associated carbonate are abundant in the area. Very fine pyrite specks (less than 1%) were observed in some of the trenches.

(Applies to: discovery and development) - Zone 5 as described by various operators is the largest uranium occurrence in the area. The zones are exposed over a 200 metre strike length and widths up to 20 metres wide have been recorded. During the winter of 1971 Vestor Explorations drilled a total of 16 holes totalling 4794 feet (AR 060835). - After Kelmett Resources took over the properties which host the showings, the emphasis shifted to precious metal exploration. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 21, 2007

(Applies to: discovery and development) - During 1971 three holes were drilled on Zone 1 for a total of 828 feet. Associations of strong fracturing, hematization, and black pyrite mineralization were noted to correspond with strongly radioactive zones in all the holes (AR 060833). During the 1988 season Kelmett Resources, which had subsequently staked the area containing Zone 1, carried out detailed prospecting and trench re-sampling (AR 082915). - A 550 pound bulk sample was taken by Vestor Explorations presumably during the 1970 program. This sample returned 0.15 % U3O8 (AR 019669). Description from NORMIN.DB, June 21, 2007

(Applies to: property geology) Zone 1 lies very close to the lakeshore and consists of a series of radioactive patches over a strike length of 150 metres. The showing occurs immediately north of a bostonite dyke, in a diatreme breccia which is composed of large blocks of Sosan Group Hornby grit and blocks of very fine grained dark greenish grey mudstone. The blocks are often over 30 metres in length. The uranium occurs within the sandstone (grit) blocks themselves. The sandstone or grit is variable in color - in places it is very red and hematized but more often it is very pale and has a bleached appearance. The effects of the bleaching are gradational. Locally, the sandstone blocks are riddled with drusy quartz veins, many of which are aligned at 120 degrees. The veins themselves are up to 1 cm wide and are often irregular.
