Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The silver-rich vein was discovered in 1932 by Don MacLaren for A. X. Syndicate, and was staked as Elite claims. White Eagle Silver Mines was formed in 1933 to take over the Elie and 6 other claims from the syndicate. The company carried out stripping and test pitting on 5 principal veins. Later the ground was dropped and was restaked by A.V. Giauque and Associates in 1945-'46 as F and H groups. It was dropped again and restaked in 1960 as ITLD claims by J.M. Harriman, Caeser Silver Mines was formed in 1967 to take over the these claims and subsequently additional claims were also staked in the area. During 1968 some trenching and drilling were carried out, and one shallow drill hole intersected 2458.3 oz Ag /t over 3 inches. and 99.5 oz/t Ag over the next 6 feet across the main vein. An adit and a raise driven on the vein indicated about 2000 tons of high grade silver recoverable by open pit. In 1969 Norex Uranium Ltd. optioned the property and conducted additional sampling and drilling. Open pit mining commenced in 1970 Some 5181 tons of ore were mined an d 700 tons of which were milled in test mill and about 20 tons of concentrate bagged. During that the company changed its name to Norex Resources Ltd. In 1971 a 1500-foot decline was started to intersect the main Graham vein at a depth. Collar of the adit is 200 feet vertically below and nearly 1000 feet horizontally away from the open pit. Drilling of 20 holes that year included deepest intersection at 252 feet below the pit with an average grade of 1.6 % Bi and 3 oz/t Ag over 10 feet, and a further 10-foot interval graded 144oz/t of Ag and 0.16 % Bi. Also during 1971 500 tons of low grade material were run through the test mill and 975 tons of mill ore grading 59.26 oz/t Agwere processed at Echo Bay mill from which 57249 oz of Ag were recovered in jig and flotation concentrate. In addition 2500 tons of ore containing about 250000 oz Ag was mined and stockpiled at the site. In 1972 Terra Mining and Exploration Ltd. acquired 50 % interest in the Norex property. The stockpiled ore was hauled to the Terra mill on ice road. All weather road was completed in 1974 and the decline was advanced about 300 feet. The mine was operated again during 1977 to 1983. Over the mine's history the total ore mined amounted to 46914 tons (42559 tonnes) from which 2175943 oz of Ag were recovered (Strand, 1996). It may be noted however that Webb (2003, p. 13) quoted figures from Northern Miner Handbook of Mines (1984-85) for the Norex mine and Smallwood (located about 3 km to the SE) as follows: 23978 tons of ore mined from which 1791341 oz of Ag produced from Norex mine, and 8714 tons of ore mined at Smallwood from which yielded 124962 oz of Ag..
