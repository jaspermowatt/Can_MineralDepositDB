Qualified comments: (Applies to: rock descriptions) According to Cox et al (2010), the nepheline sodalite syenite that hosts the ore consists of a layered series of increasingly peralkaline rocks with depth.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The Thor Lake area was first map by the Geological Survey of Canada (GSC) in 1937 and 1938. The first claims were staked for uranium in 1970. In 1971 an airborne radiometric survey commissioned by the GSC outlined a radiometric anomaly that was attributed to elevated thorium levels rather than uranium. Between 1976 to present, after the discovery of niobium and tantalum, intermittent exploration was focussed on niobium, tantalum, and rare earth elements. The most recent phase of exploration (2005 to present) is being undertaken by Avalon Rare Metals, leading up to a pre-feasibility study on the project (Cox et al., 2010) and further to a feasibility study (Ciuculesco et al., 2013).
