Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) - The Sun 1 - 16 claims were staked in 1954 to cover a radioactive anomaly detected by airborne survey. 14,146 feet of surface diamond drilling in 38 holes was completed by June 1, 1955, at which time the decision was made to investigate underground. A two-compartment shaft was sunk to a depth of 274 feet, and two levels established at 125 foot intervals. A total of 2,540 feet of lateral work, 132 feet of raising and 1,456 feet of underground diamond drilling had been completed when the power plant and hoist house were destroyed by fire on July 10, 1956. - Good drill results were obtained below the bottom level on the Main zone about 350 feet below the surface and 50 feet below the second level. Development work in 1957 opened 2 ore shoots in the number 1 zone on the lower level for a combined length of 100 feet and an average width of 6.45 feet grading 0.207 percent uranium oxide. An ore shoot on the lower level of the number 1 zone was opened over a length of 127 feet with an average width of 7.28 feet grading 0.212 percent uranium oxide (Crestland assessment report 1971). - In 1969, a 100 foot long trench was excavated about 450 feet south of the main zone. Sections from this trench averaged 0.448 pounds of uranium oxide across 9.6 feet and 0.453 pounds uranium oxide across 12 feet. - A small tonnage of ore grading better than four pounds per ton had been indicated. A proposal was made to operate the mine on a small scale during the summer months and truck the ore to the mill at Rayrock. The high unit costs of production and the uncertainties of the uranium market at that time brought the decision not to proceed. - In 1969, detailed scintillometer surveys were conducted over the main showing while prospecting located yellow staining, indicating uranium mineralization, to the north. The additional Sun claims were staked to the north of the original block. - In 1974, the Rose claims were staked. Reconnaissance exploration found that the yellow staining was widespread. A few test pits disclosed low levels of uranium mineralization. - In 1976, Rayrock Mines Limited optioned the property and conducted a surface exploration program over the extension of the Chico Fault. - Underground development - 9000 tons at 0.2% U3O8. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 19, 2007.

(Applies to: property geology) - The SUN showings are underlain by Precambrian rocks consisting of andesite, granites and a quartz stockwork. The stockwork is the principal host for radioactive mineralization. - The regional controlling structural feature is the Chico Fault which permitted invasion and digestion of the original rocks by the quartz stockwork. The stockwork, bounded on one side by the fault, is two to four hundred feet wide, finely fractured and mineralized by pitchblende in the form of veinlets, blebs and sheets. Veinlets are irregular and discontinuous but fractures can be followed. Fine fracture patterns are usually surrounded by red, altered, brecciated zones mineralized with pitchblende forming patches or lenses of ore. In the andesite, on the southeast side of the stockwork, pitchblende occurrences are more regular and are usually accompanied by fine pyrite mineralization. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 19, 2007.

(Applies to: location and access) The SUN Main showing is approximately 10 kilometres north of Marian Lake. Access is by plane from Yellowknife to Marian Lake. An all weather road to Rayrock Mine passes within kilometres of the showings.
