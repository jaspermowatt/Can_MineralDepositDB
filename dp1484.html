Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The showing is related to a giant quartz vein that is roughly 58 meters wide across its biggest exposure and trends NE at an azimuth of 060 degrees. The vein intrudes granitized quartzites of the Snare Group and a portion of hornfelsed monzonite that is intensely chlorite and biotite altered. The vein is associated with quartz-hematite breccias, sulphide mineralization including pyrite and chalcopyrite, and variable amounts of visible uranium oxides. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 19, 2007

(Applies to: discovery and development) - 12 diamond drill holes to total 3963 feet were sunk. Eighteen samples showed values ranging from 0.011 to 4.56% U3O8, determined by radiometric tests and five samples showed 0.55 to 21.5% U3O8, determined by chemical analysis. - In 1996, high grade grab and chip samples of pre-existing trenches were submitted for analysis by GMD Resource Corp., however, these samples were not tested for uranium. A 3.75 meter consecutive chip sample of a trench yielded an average of 0.0274% Cr, and 0.0184% Zn; a second 2 meter wide section of mineralized quartz vein returned length weighted averages of 0.0254% Cr and 0.0242% Zn over 4 m., with values as high as 0.102% Cr, 0.339% Zn and 0.473% Cu for grab samples.
