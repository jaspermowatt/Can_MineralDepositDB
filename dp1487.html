Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs in a fracture zone affecting the gneissic granodiorite that strikes N35E and dips almost vertically. The fracture zone is about 70 feet long and up to 5 feet wide, and consists of principal fractures with numerous short cross fractures that give a braided effect. Rock between the fractures is crushed and stained with hematite. Quartz veins occur in some fractures. Pitchblende forms in fractures and more commonly at fracture intersections.
