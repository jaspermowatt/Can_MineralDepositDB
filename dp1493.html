Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The occurrence is located on the W side of the SW arm of Kasmere Lake, opposite the 'narrows' of Bryson Lake and approximately 305 km N of Lynn Lake.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) The Kasmere Lake-Snyder Lake corridor is considered the area with the greatest potential for economic uranium deposits.

(Applies to: mineralization) Uranium occurs in Precambrian greyish-white pegmatite with local hematite staining. Yellow gummite stains are found parallel to the length of the zone, which is 240 m long and 120 m wide.

(Applies to: discovery and development) - Exploration Reservation 36, Denison Mines Limited, covered this area between 1967 and 1970. Airborne and ground electromagnetic and radiometric surveys identified an electromagnetic anomaly with magnetic correspondence and a radiometric anomaly. The area was trenched for over 30 m. Assays of chip samples from the trench proved disappointing, with trace U3O8 and nil ThO2. The best grab sample yielded 0.03% U2O8 (Reservations 36-40; Non-confidential Assessment Files). The ground was reopened for staking in 1971.
