Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) This prospect occurs in a NW-trending outlier of Paleoproterozoic Fairchild Group cut by a 800 by 1800 m heterolithic Wernecke breccia body with irregular margins and gradational contacts with the wallrock. . The breccia pipe hosts two distinct types of mineralization. The earliest phase consists of massive magnetite-hematite-jasper-quartz with minor disseminated chalcopyrite in the matrix, and occurs in an irregular zone about 30 m across in the northern part of the pipe. The second phase includes coarsely crystalline brannerite with quartz and pink K feldspar, and occurs at the east edge of the pipe's core.
