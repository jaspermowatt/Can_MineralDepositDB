Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) According to the Ontario Mineral Deposit Inventory: "A sequence of calcareous metasediments on the W margin of the Hond Lake Granite hosts the deposit. Mineralization [ Mo ] occurs in pyroxenite , mostly at the gneiss / granite contact. Uraninite occurs in pegmatite where it intersects molybdenum - rich metapyroxenite skarn. [ 0.06 - 0.42 % U3O8 , 1955]."

(Applies to: discovery and development) According to the Ontario Mineral Deposit Inventory: "Initial discovery in 1914 on lot 27. 1917 - 81 tons of ore [7,800 lbs. molybdenite, 472 lbs flake ]. 1923 - 1937 - Phoenix Molybdenite Corp. sunk a 2 - compartment shaft to 205 feet and installed a 150 ton mill. In 1931 12 tons of ore was shipped for testing. 1934 -1937 produced 8,579 tons of ore [ 15-22 tons of concentrate at 80-85% MoS2 ]. 1938 -1940 - Zenith Molybdenite Corp. carried out development work. 1942 - 1943 - Wartime Metals Corp. produced 400 tons of ore [ 2,274 lbs of molybdenite ]. In 1955 Goldyke Mines Ltd. drilled 11 holes [ 336 feet ] on the 175 foot level in search of uranium. Workings consist of very numerous and widespread pits and trenches, including one pit 100 feet by 16 feet."
