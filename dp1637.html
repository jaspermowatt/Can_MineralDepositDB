Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Elevated Th-U in alkaline syenite (bostonite ?) bodies, with radioactivity also concentrated along fractures.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) This showing plots within the outcrop area of the Christopher Island Formation, and coincides approximately (maybe a bit south) with a swarm of bostonite dykes mapped by Pan Ocean Oil (Mazur, 1980). These intrusions contain elevated uranium and thorium, and according to David Quirte (pers.com.) are more of a thorium source that a uranium source. These dykes and irregular bodies of the same cut the Archean basement gneisses, the basal Baker Lake sedimentary rocks and the Christopher Island trachytes and volcaniclastic rocks. Irregular, more plug-like syenitic equivalents of the bostonite (Martell Syenite) cut Christopher Island Formation across the central part of NTS 65O.

(Applies to: mineralization) Both Prasad (1981) and Mazur (1980) report subequal concentrations of uranium and thorium.
