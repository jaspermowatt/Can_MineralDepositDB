Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) - Uranium oxides were reported by Russell (1957) in 2 showings, Showings A and B. Showing A occurs on the northwest shore of the Northeast Bay of Herblet Lake. Yellow stains coincide with strong signals when a Geiger counter was held near the outcrop. Showing B corresponds to yellow staining on the north shore of the Southeast Bay of Herblet Lake, north-northeast of Whitefish Bay. A third uranium showing occurs on the west shore of a lake about 1 km east of northern Wolverton Lake. Prospectors investigating a rutile showing found the uranium showing in 1948 from Geiger Counter readings. Later in 1948, Snow Lake Gold Mines Limited diamond drilled 8 short holes totalling 225 m on J.N. 1. The claim was cancelled in 1951. The area was restaked in 1981 as CB 12387 for Hudson Bay Exploration and Development Company Limited, and worked in 1982, 1985, 1987 and 1989. The claim was cancelled in July 1990. Details can be found in the Manitoba Mineral Resources Library.

(Applies to: mineralization) Showing A consists of radioactive stains in granite near its contact with arkosic remnants, and Showing B merely coincides with yellow staining (Russell, 1957, p. 29). J.N.1 or JN-1 was drilled, and the drill holes were reported to contain pitchblende and brown to black and greenish to yellow staining in felsic rocks. Pyrite, tourmaline, hornblende, and feldspar were found throughout the holes (Non-confidential Assessment File No. 90105, Manitoba Mines Branch).
