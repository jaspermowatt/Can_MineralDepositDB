Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) - A yellow to ochre secondary uranium mineral occurs in graphic pink Precambrian pegmatite near a contact with Archean greywacke. The radioactive mineral was found on surface and in the uppermost sections of two drill holes. Resinous yellow mica with a slight greenish tint was reported in one drill log. Pyrrhotite, pyrite, and graphite disseminated in the greywacke and in quartz-feldspar schist were also identified in drilling (Non-confidential Assessment File No. 90093). - The zone is over 305 m (1000 ft) long, reaches a maximum width of 6 m (20 ft). Uraninite, monazite, and molybdenite were reported (Manitoba Mines Branch, Unpublished Information File).

(Applies to: discovery and development) - Gamma 2 and 3 were staked in 1952, and partially surveyed by EA-130 ratemeter. Selected samples from two areas near Dion Lake returned wet chemical assays of 2.64% U3O8 and 5.75% U3O8. Three channel samples, taken further west, gave equivalent assays of 0.86% U3O8 over 1.2 m (4 ft), 0.86% U3O8 across 1.8 m (6 ft), and 1.70% U3O8 over 3.4 m (11 ft). The area was trenched, and twenty-eight grab samples returned grades of 0.15%-0.20% U3O8 across 3 - 4.6 m (10 - 15 ft) widths (Northern Miner, December 11, 1952). - Cypress Exploration Corporation Ltd. optioned the property in December of 1952. They drilled 12 holes totalling 789 m (2587.3 ft). The best assay was 0.19% U3O8 across 1.2 m (4 ft) of core. The drilling showed that the surface showing only extended to 9 m (30 ft) (Northern Miner, February 19, 1953 in Manitoba Mines Branch Minerals resources Library). The option was abandoned in 1957, although an airborne electromagnetic survey was done by Canadian Nickel Company Limited in 1957. The property was restaked intermittently between until 1965, when Hudson Bay Exploration and Development carried out airborne electromagnetic and radiometric surveys; it was trenched in 1966-1967. In 1968 further magnetic and electromagnetic surveys found no anomalies. The ground was dropped, and then restaked intermittently until at least 1990.
