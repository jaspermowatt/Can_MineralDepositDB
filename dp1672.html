Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The showings are sited in multi-stage fractures proximal to the Hoidas-Nisikkatch Fault, a fault system likely a splay of the prominent Black Bay Fault system. Early descriptions of the Hoidas Lake showings referred to the mineralization as skarn-type, whereas current interpretations favour its origins as hydrothermal with a late-stage magmatic hydrothermal source, possibly alkaline.

(Applies to: mineralization) The mineralization is hosted predominantly in the minerals apatite and allanite. It is also hosted by chevkinite, monazite, and some of the carbonate minerals. Diopside, hyalophane, quartz, biotite, epidote, carbonate, zircon, scapolite, and amphibole are gangue minerals. Calcite, barite, and feldspar occur in the inner zones of some veins. The veins have been classified into dark green pyroxene-rich veins and reddish to brown apatite-rich veins. Concentrations of cerium and neodymium occur in the former, and concentrations of lanthanum, cerium, praseodymium, neodymium, samarium, gadolinium, and dysprosium are associated with the latter.
