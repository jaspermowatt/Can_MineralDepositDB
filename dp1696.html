Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) Radioactive fractures in mafic gneiss and mixed mafic-granitoid gneiss near a late northwest-trending cataclastic zone. Isolated remnants of the conglomerate which underlies the Christopher Island Formation are found within and near the cataclastic zone on its southwest side. Foliations and an apparent isoclinal fold hinge in the mafic and felsic gneisses, biotite trachyte and microsyenite southwest of the cataclastic zone strike east-southeast. Foliations northeast of the cataclastic zone strike east-northeast and the basement geology apparently contains fewer intrusions than the basement south of it.

(Applies to: mineralization) The main showing (named the Lake by Pan Ocean Oil Ltd.) occurs in intensely fractured mafic and felsic gneiss (migmatitic or agmatitic). This description was extracted from Mazur (1980). The mineralization is concentrated in thin (up to 5 cm) fractures in the folded mafic gneiss adjacent to biotite trachyte that was thought by Mazur (1980) to pass or grade into the more felsic microsyenite (bostonite) intrusion to the east. The main mineralized trend at ~ 140 is pervasively chloritized and hematized, and the U-Co-Ni-Pb-Ag mineralization is localized as massive pods and disseminations sporadically at intersections of cross fractures. This fracture contains local native silver associated with chlorite veinlets. Another mineralized fracture is brecciated and filled with chlorite and quartz. Other carbonate-hematite fractures striking approximately east-west are neither mineralized nor radioactive. Other mineralized fracture sets occur on the property.

(Applies to: mineralization) The syenite and microsyenites (bostonite) at this locality (and elsewhere in the general area) show elevated background radioactivity. One such grab sample assayed 0.055% U3O8 and 58.16 oz/ton silver (Mazur, 1980), although visible silver was lacking.

(Applies to: mineralization) According to Mazur (1980), the silver occurs in three settings at this showing: (1) associated with finely disseminated galena in chloritic veinlets and pyritic stringers; (2) thin veinlets of massive galena-sphalerite with associated silver values; and (3) associated with finely disseminated galena in border phase bostonite. It is noteworthy that zinc mineralization is apparently rare or minor at this showing.
