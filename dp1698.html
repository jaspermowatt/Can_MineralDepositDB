Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) -The radioactivity is in unsheared, fractured, pebbly arkose. The fractures are filled with breccia with a matrix of iron oxide and a white, clay-like mineral. The uranium bearing mineral is euhedral thucholite. Pyrite and minor CaCO3 form circular concretions. - South of the Mushroom trench, there is a radioactive zone 48 m in length and 1 m wide. A trench was excavated at 400N + 150W which exposed a quartz vein striking 130 degrees. The surrounding rock is strongly fractured and radioactive. Fractures are sealed with gangue consisting of quartz, plagioclase, chlorite, illite, dolomite, calcite and secondary green and yellow uranium minerals. description from NORMIN.DB, June 22, 2007.
