Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) - The Steve U-Th showing is located on the INOMI-North grid, 1.5 km west of Thekulthili Lake, near the northeast corner of a small lake informally named East-West Lake by SMDC personnel. - The INOMI grid area is underlain by pebbly arkose and polymictic conglomerate. The clasts are composed of granitic and felsic gneiss (45 to 75 per cent), sedimentary fragments (45 to 75 per cent) and vein quartz (10 to 30 per cent). Pebbles are round to angular and range from 1 to 20 cm in size. Arkose and lithic sandstone beds are interbedded with the conglomerate and vary in thickness from several cm to several metres, laterally the beds extend from a few decimetres to hundreds of metres. The contact between arkoses and conglomerate is faulted (45/90 degrees) and filled with quartz. The general trend is northeasterly with dips ranging from horizontal to steep. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 22, 2007.

(Applies to: mineralization) The radioactivity is located in fractured and folded arkose in a 10 cm wide, 6 m long, at 55 degree trending zone transgressing the bedding of the arkose. The folds are broad, open and trend north-northeast. Pitchblende is disseminated in the host rock, secondary uranium mineralization, calcite and specularite are as coating on fracture planes. Fractures are short and radioactivity is spotty, the orientation of the fractures was not measured because most rocks have moved during trenching. The eastern margin of the mineralized zone is marked by red hematite staining, to the west the mineralization decreases gradually in intensity. Gandhi (1980) determined a mineralization age of 1,617 Ma at a sample from the Steve showing. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 22, 2007.
