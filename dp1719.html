Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The Collins Bay D, E and F are small zones between the Collins Bay A and B zones that are 2.5 km apart, the A zone being northeast of the B zone. All five zones are located along the Collins Bay fault zone, a major thrust trending north-northeast with an easterly dip. The D zone is the largest of the three small zones, is located 320 m north of the northern tip of the B zone. It was discovered in 1979 by Gulf Minerals Canada Limited during a systematic drilling program to explore the Collins Bay thrust between the A and B zones. The drilling was conducted from the ice near the shore the bay, and resulted in the discovery of the D zone. Follow-up reverse circulation drilling in 1979 and 1980 was done to outline the deposit. The deposit was defined by a total of 117 diamond drill holes and 46 reverse circulation drill holes. It was mined during the winter of 1995-'96 from an open pit and was exhausted in 6 months. The mining was carried out by Cameco Corporation, which was formed in 1988 by the merger of Eldorado Nuclear Ltd. and Saskatchewan Mining and Development Corp., and acquired the property. The E and F zones are located 120 m and 670 m north of the northern tip of the B zone, respectively. They are smaller and lower in grade than the D zone. Their resources are not defined.

(Applies to: mineralization) The D zone is morphologically, structurally and geochemically similar to the Collins Bay A zone and the high grade north end of the B zone. It occurs in highly altered basal Athabasca Group sandstone on the footwall side of the Collins Bay reverse fault, where the basement rock is granite. The deposit is 700 m long, ranges from 8 to 40 m in width and 10 to 46 m in thickness. It has a high grade core about 100 m long near its northeastern end. Uranium minerals and Ni-arsenides occur together in the deposit as they do in the A and B zones, with the exception of the southwestern tail of the deposit which is relatively poor in nickel arsenides. The ore has high content of lead, and anomalously high contents of silver, cobalt, molybdenum and vanadium, but no significant amount of gold. The Collins Bay E and F zones in its vicinity also occur in similar geological environment. The E zone is 60 m long, and the F zone is less than 30 m long. The five Collins Bay zones demonstrated the consistency of the favourable conductive horizons and structure that served as a basis in further exploration for the unconformity-associated deposits.
