Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The Burnell Pond uranium occurrence lies about 1.5 km west of the former Susie mine (082ESW090) and 5.5 km northwest of Oliver, British Columbia. This occurrence lies near the northwest end of a 2 km northwest trending area of erratic uranium and thorium occurrences.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The property was examined and evaluated by D.G.Leighton for British Newfoundland Exploration Ltd. in 1979. A total of 19 auger holes were drilled into unconsolidated sediments.

(Applies to: regional geology) The area is principally underlain by medium grained intrusive rocks that form the Jurassic Oliver complex. To the immediate south, the complex cuts Carboniferous to Permian Kobau Group metasedimentary rocks. On its northern margin, the intrusive mass is in contact with Eocene volcanics and sediments of the Penticton Group. The Kettle River Formation, consisting of conglomerate, arkose and rhyolite tuff, is overlain by the Springbrook and Marron formations.

(Applies to: genetic model) The Burnell Pond is a fluviatile-type young uranium deposit occurring in a swamp where groundwater flow and organic sequestration are probably the dominant depositional controls (IAEA TECDOC 322).

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) The Burnell Pond zone covers 12,000 square metres with an average thickness of 5.5 metres at an average depth of 1 metre. In 1979, auger drilling intersected 0.033 % uranium over 4 metres in one hole and 0.026 % uranium in another (Assessment Report 7398). A 0.107 % uranium value occurs over 0.5 metre within the uraniferous layer (Culbert, 1979).
