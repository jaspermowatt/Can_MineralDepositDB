Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The property was examined and evaluated by D.G. Leighton for British Newfoundland Exploration Ltd. from 1977 to 1979. A total of 98 auger holes were drilled into unconsolidated sediments.

(Applies to: location and access) The South Wow Lake uranium occurrence lies about 3.75 km northwest of Oliver, B.C. and 1 km north of the former standard mine (082ESW091).

(Applies to: regional geology) - The area is principally underlain by medium grained intrusive rocks that form the Jurassic Oliver plutonic complex. To the south the complex cuts Carboniferous to Permian Kobau Group metasedimentary rocks. On its northern margin, the intrusive mass is in contact with Eocene volcanics and sediments of the Penticton Group. The Kettle River Formation, consisting of conglomerate, arkose and rhyolite tuff, is overlain by the Springbrook and Marron formations. - Bedrock types to the south of South Wow Lake include laminated quartz schist or dirty, massive and laminated quartzite and minor limestone of the Kobau Group. In the South Wow Lake area, the Oliver plutonic complex is composed almost entirely of biotite-hornblende quartz monzonite. The southern contact with the Kobau Group is approximately 2.5 km to the south of the Wow Lakes.. Three distinct phases have been identified. From the youngest to oldest these are: a monzonite to the south and biotite-hornblende quartz monzonite to the north of the core. Hornblende diorite occurs in several small areas to the north. Border phases and dykes related to the Oliver plutonic complex include lamprophyre, augite-plagioclase porphyritic andesite, micro-quartz diorite, albite porphyritic dacite, diabase, fine-grained quartz-monzonite and aplite.

(Applies to: structure) The Wow Lakes lie along a north-northeast trending linear which separate muscovite-garnet quartz monzonite to the east from porphyritic biotite quartz monzonite to the west.

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) The 98 auger holes drilled in the property has outlined a 17,500 square metre deposit approximately 1 metre thick (Culbert, 1979). The average uranium concentration was 0.036 %, with a maximum value of 0.080 % uranium over a 1/2 metre section (Culbert, 1979).

(Applies to: genetic model) The South Wow Lakes occurrence is a postglacial, lacustrine, closed basin type of deposit which is forming within a few metres of the surface by enrichment of uranium and other elements by evaporative pumping. The uranium is concentrated in clays as a salt from saline oxidizing groundwater in an arid environment in the deposit. The underlying rocks are sources of labile uranium with possible contributions from mineralized fault zones (BC MINFILE).

(Applies to: mineralization) No uranium minerals have been recognized in the Quaternary surficial material. Bedrock uranium mineralization consists of pegmatite accumulations, uraniferous limestone, uranium-pyrrhotite and fracture-hosted uranium (Assessment Report 7398). The latter are common in the Wow Lakes area.
