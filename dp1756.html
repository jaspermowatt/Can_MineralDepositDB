Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The PRAIRIE FLATS deposit occupies the lower reaches of the Prairie Creek valley. It is located within the Summerland municipal boundaries, immediately southwest of the town site. The area was once a marsh but has since been drained for agricultural use and residential development. The PRAIRIE SOUTH EDGE area is included with the PRAIRIE FLATS occurrence, but the uranium contained in the PRAIRIE SOUTH EDGE is not included in the PRAIRIE FLATS deposit because of its low grade.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The PRAIRIE FLATS deposit was discovered in 1979 by D.G. Leighton & Associates Ltd. during a regional uranium reconnaissance program. Work prior to the uranium moratorium in 1980 consisted of systematic soil auguring and bench extraction tests.

(Applies to: regional geology) Overlap assemblage on plutonic rocks of the intermontane tectonic belt on the Thompson Plateau. The area is underlain by granodiorite of the Jurassic Okanagan Intrusions, which is unconformably overlain by a succession of Eocene epiclastic sediments, pyroclastic rocks and alkaline lavas of the Penticton Group White Lake and Marron Formations.

(Applies to: property geology) Surficial occurrence in postglacial unconsolidated material.

(Applies to: reserve/resource assays) Tonnage is calculated from average thickness of 1.7 metres over 37.0 hectares with an average density of 1000 kg per cubic metre. Uranium grades at 0.0334 %. Quantity is estimated at 629,000 tonnes.

(Applies to: genetic model) This is a postglacial fluviatile type of deposit where uranium occurs in a collector basin composed of organic-rich valley fill deposited by Prairie Creek. Upwelling of groundwater into organic-rich soils, topographic control and concentration of uranium by evaporitic discharge and ion absorption-reduction are the principal depositional controls.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) A contour map of the deposit showing variations in uranium grade identifies several areas with greater than 0.9 kg U3O8 per square metre (Culbert, 1979). Cross-sections of the deposit, based on 28 auger holes, show high grade layers with greater than 0.1% uranium (Culbert and Leighton, 1988). Molybdenum enrichment (up to 0.09 per cent) is also present (Culbert, 1979).
