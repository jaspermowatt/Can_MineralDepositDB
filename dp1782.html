Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Strongly anomalous radioactivity and geochemical responses were discovered at the Bear prospect in 1977 by BP Minerals Canada Ltd.-Union Carbide Exploration Ltd. Joint Venture. The anomalies occur in overburden on floor of a steep-sided, 200 m wide, north-trending Bear Valley, which follows a brittle fault/shear zone for 15 km to the south. Surface mapping and prospecting revealed NW-trending radioactive fractures on the east side of the valley up slope from the anomalies. The fractures contain pyrite, and limonitic samples from them contained 80 to 180 ppm U, and one sample assayed 2118 ppm U. The fractures are related to the NW-trending Mouse River Fault. Drill testing of the anomalies was done in 1978, but their bedrock source was not located. In 1982 the Bear Valley properties were farmed out to Anaconda Canada Exploration Ltd., and a track-etch survey was carried out that defined targets. These were drill tested in 1983 by 5 holes totalling 416 m, but no significant uranium values were intersected. The drilling intersected schists and gneisses of Epworth Group and the peraluminous Hepburn intrusion. These rocks are faulted against the Akaitcho Group to the west, which comprises low grade metavolcanics and metasediments. It is overlain by the Hornby bay Group sandstone that outcrops on the west side of the valley.
