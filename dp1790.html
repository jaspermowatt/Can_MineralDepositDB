Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) - Mineralization consists of pitchblende and secondary uranium minerals as fracture fillings and disseminations in quartz rich mylonite. Copper minerals are also abundant with mainly chalcopyrite and bornite with some chalcocite and covellite. Pitchblende is massive and botryoidal in trench B. Vein type mineralization consisting of sulphides and calcite was found in granodiorite. - Uranium and copper mineralization occurs in 1-2 cm veinlets, fracture-fillings and disseminations in the mylonite zone, in a narrow silicified zone at the contact of basement with the granodiorite dyke, in calcite, quartz and hematite veinlets within the granodiorite, as spots in the basement rocks and at one place in what appears to be a sheared conglomerate in the gneissic granite.
