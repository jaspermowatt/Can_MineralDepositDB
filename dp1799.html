Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) BP Minerals Canada Ltd. started exploration for uranium in the Hornby Bay Basin during 1974-'76. Several radioactive occurrences were located in the Coppermine River Big Bend area. This led to acquisition of extensive claims and permits in that region during 1976, in equal partnership with Union Carbide Exploration Ltd. The focus of exploration here was the sub-Hornby Bay Group unconformity. Exploration during 1977-'81 included airborne geophysical surveys, ground follow-up surveys, and diamond drilling of selected targets. The drilling amounted to 10664 m, of which 84279 m were drilled during 1979-'80. Exploration expenditures by the joint venture totalled $ 7 million. In 1982 a group of contiguous claims in the north-trending Bear Valley (B1-9, U1-7, U-14-17 and U53), totalling about 350 sq km, were farmed out to Anaconda Canada Exploration Ltd. In that year the company carried out a large soil geochemical survey, including overburden drilling. The 1983 program drill tested the showings located prior to 1982, and also the geochemical and geophysical anomalies in their vicinity. A total of 3530 m were drilled in 46 holes at 8 prospects, including Tabb A and B showings. Expenditures by the company during the two years totalled $ 1.735 million. The results were not encouraging. The depressed price of uranium further discouraged exploration activity. In 1996 the Bear Valley claims were acquired by the newly formed Hornby Bay Explorations Ltd., which was taken over by UNOR Inc. in 1998. A major exploration initiative was launched by UNOR in 2004 with acquisition of additional properties in the region, and also included the Bear Valley Project in joint venture with Adriana Resources Inc. (UNAD JV). During 2005-'06 the Tabb showings were examined. Assays on 12 samples ranged from 0.06 to 4.0 % U.
