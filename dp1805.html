Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The showing discovered in 1958 and staked as WW Claims for Murky Metals Mines Ltd. and Basement Mines Ltd. was explored by 5 trenches and 6 short x-ray drillholes totalling 227 m.

(Applies to: mineralization) The heavy minerals concentration as lenses occur in Nonacho arkose which dips gently to the north. The unconformity between Nonacho sediments and gneissic granite is about 150 m to the south. The detrital lenses rich in heavy minerals are up to a few centimetres thick and are traceable for a few metres along the strike and dip. The detrital lenses consist of alternating thin layers of grey heavy minerals and lighter coloured quartz and feldspar rich layers up to 5 m in thickness. Minerals in the layers are cassiterite, pitchblende with magnetite/hematite, monazite, zircon, tourmaline, uranothorite and native gold. Magnetite, now largely altered to hematite, constitutes almost 98 % of the heavy minerals.

(Applies to: stratigraphy) The basal Nonacho Group arkose rests on gneissic granite unconformably. This is followed by quartz-pebble conglomerate with thin arkose beds, which in turn is overlain by pebbly arkose with beds and lenses of conglomerate and lenses of heavy minerals.
