Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) During 1977, an airborne gamma ray spectrometric survey, carried out in the area under the Uranium Reconnaissance Program (U.R.P.) by the Geological Survey of Canada, delineated 2 major anomalies one in the east (Locality B, C and D) and one in the west (Locality E). Dejour Mines staked the claims over the radiometric anomaly, found an extensive fault zone in the basement 100 m north of the unconformity containing 60 cm wide quartz-hematite vein. the vein was traced to 100 m and assayed 1.6 lb/ton U3O8. Drilling was later carried out (Northern Miner v. 65, no. 47, Jan 31, 1980, p. 4). This vein coincides with the site of the highest level of radioactivity in the eastern anomaly.

(Applies to: mineralization) Zones of enrichment within granitoid bodies in the basement underlying the Fury and Hecla Group supracrustal sediments. Geochemical considerations indicate that epigenetic processes controlled the mineralization rather than syngenetic processes.
