Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) A small zone of uranium mineralization adjacent to a quartz vein occur in Fury and Hecla Formation sandstone of Eqalulik Group of Helikian age. The uraniferous sandstone occurs close to the faulted unconformity that brings it in contact with granite. The mineralization occurs over a 10 x 7 foot area and averages 30,000 cpm radioactivity. The mineralization was thought to represent the truncated erosional remnant of once more extensive occurrence. This anomaly was considered too small to be of economic significance.
