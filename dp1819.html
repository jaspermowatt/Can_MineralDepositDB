Qualified comments: (Applies to: regional geology) According to the Newfoundland and Labrador Mineral Occurrence Database (MODS): "The Wilson-Winokapau lakes area lies within a mid to late Precambrian mixed gneiss, granitoid and gabbroid crystalline terrane, which belongs to the northern Grenville Province in central Labrador. Rocks within the map area are part of a widespread regional, in part thrust fault bounded, salient block termed the Wilson Lake allochthon. The allochthon consists of granulite-facies quartzofeldspathic paragneiss, charnokitic rocks and pyroxene-bearing mafic intrusive bodies." Most of the high grade metamorphism is thought to be related to the 1700-1600 Ma Labradorean Orogeny. However, the area is also imprinted by the ~ 1000 Ma Grenvillian Orogeny, the effects of which are difficult to identify.
