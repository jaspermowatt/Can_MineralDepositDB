Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1976 S.E.R.U. Nucléaire (Canada) Limitée carried out a helicopter-borne radiometric survey of the SBI and JT claim groups near the southeast and northwest ends of Bathurst Lake, respectively, and followed it up with ground checking. The survey detected the Anomaly 4 on the SBI claim, located 6.5 km southeast of the southern end of Bathurst Lake. The ground check with SPP2 scintillometer revealed certain siltstone beds with high total counts of 3000 counts per second in dolomitic siltstone compared with background of 125 cps in the general area. Analysis of a sample of the radioactive bed showed 181 ppm U and 833 ppm Th. High Th/U ratio reflects presence of thoriferous detrital mineral, most probably monazite. The occurrence is of little significance in terms of uranium, but it could indicate possible richer concentrations that may contain significant amounts of thorium and rare earths in the Quadyuk Formation and also in underlying Mara Formation with anomalously radioactive siltstones (Roscoe, 1984, p. 7).
