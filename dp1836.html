Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1976 S.E.R.U. Nucléaire (Canada) Limitée carried out a helicopter-borne radiometric survey of the JT claim group on the west side of the northwest end of Bathurst Lake and followed it up with ground checking. The survey detected two anomalies numbered 5 and 7, in the northwest and northeast corner of the JT claim group, respectively. Anomaly 5 is the main one that extends over 1500 m in north-northwest direction. Ground check showed anomalous readings in the range of 500 to 2000 counts per second on SPP2 scintillometer, locally reaching 5000 cps (equivalent to 8600 ppm U) with negligible thorium content. Anomaly 7 is located 2900 m from Anomaly 5 in direction 098° and about 1 km from Bathurst Lake to the east. It is a weak anomaly in feldspathic quartzite of Brown Sound Formation, with maximum total 900 cps. A sample from that locality assayed 130 ppm U and 140 ppm Th. The data are indicative of detrital concentration of zircon and/or monazite. Relatively higher thorium over uranium content occurs at Anomaly 4 in siltstones to the south in SBI claims, which were surveyed and checked at the same time as the JT claims.
