Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Primary metal concentration is contained within the Sue-Dianne Breccia complex, which is an elliptical feature 600 by 500m and extending to a depth of 350m. The breccia consists of angular to rounded, microcline and iron oxide altered ignimbrite fragments in a matrix of specular hematite/magnetite, altered rock flour, chlorite, epidote, fluorite, garnet and copper sulphides. Copper minerals are dominated by bornite near surface and chalcopyrite in the bulk of the deposit and occur as veins, fracture fillings and disseminations in the breccia matrix. Description abridged from NORMIN.DB, June 19, 2007.

(Applies to: property geology) The Sue showing consists of Olympic Dam-type mineralization containing copper, uranium, gold, silver and magnetite/hematite within a pipe-like breccia zone in a thick pile of rhyodacite ignimbrite. Ore minerals occurring on the showing include chalcopyrite, bornite, chalcocite and pitchblende. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 19, 2007.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Discovered in 1974 by regional, 5 km line spacing airborne spectrometric survey. Drill-indicated resources as of 2000: 17.3 Mt averaging 0.72 % Cu and 2.7 g/t Ag with minor U, Mo, Bi, Co, and REE
