Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The rhyolitic porphyry is the host for uranium mineralization. The mineralization occurs with fine-grained carbonate, hematite, rutile, quartz and other silicate material in reddened micro fractures 'crackle zones' which are seldom more than a few millimetres wide but very dense within the unit. Veinlets of pitchblende and coffinite are present but minor, uranium is primarily adsorbed by rutile. An area approximately 5 m by 130 m long gave an average radioactive reading of 5000 counts per second. Assays from the showing ranged from 22.7 to 1590 ppm U3O8. Miller (1982) concludes epigenetic mineralization with similar ages to the Echo Bay deposits. Description from NORMIN.DB, June 29, 2007
