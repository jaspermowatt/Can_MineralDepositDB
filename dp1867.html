Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) U and Th was first reported as being in joints and fractures in quartz latite flows, but the last assessment report filed by Shell Canada Ltd. (Best et al., 1977) showed that the two trenches with pitchblende in fractures were located in the outcrop area occupied on surface by Martell porphyritic to fine-grained syenite. The Martell syenite is reported as being at least locally hypabyssal in this area, and possibly grading into Christopher Island Formation.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Shell Canada reported on three claim groups in the area including the Bak Claims (Best et al., 1977). Two trenched in bedrock on the Bak Claims were used to approximately located this showing, really an anomaly. There are many radioactive fractures in this area; those in the Christopher Island Formation are reported as extending for only short distances.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) The Bak showing was originally mislocated in Archean rocks in NTSO01 - a direct transcription from the cover of MacLeod and Brander (1976) by Laporte, 1978. The error was never corrected, and made its way into the URAG database at the GSC and thence into the NUMIN database. Easting: 528999.6 and Northing: 7017743.5 - but not that accurate and between the 2 trenches.
