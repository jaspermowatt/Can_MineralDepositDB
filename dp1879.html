Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) This showing of mainly beryllium is located about 0.8 km E of Greer Lake, 22.5 km E and 3.2 km N of Point du Bois and Provincial Road No. 313.

(Applies to: property geology) - The Top of the World showing occurs in a pegmatite dyke which intrudes Archean grey quartz diorite and granodiorite of the Great Falls Pluton. The dyke is zoned parallel to its walls, with zones rich in beryl and others with less beryl and more lepidolite. In addition to the beryllium and lithium, columbite-tantalite, monazite, cesium, cassiterite, bismuth, cadmium, and uranium are also reported on the nearby Soon group of 28 claims. Gold, silver, copper and zinc have also been reported. This claim became part of the Soon group. - Bannatyne (1985, p. 30) reported two parallel pegmatites outcropping west of a large pegmatite that has been traced over a length of 610 m, the most westerly of which is zoned, strikes north, dips 80° to vertical, and is 6 m wide. The latter was thought to be the Top of the World pegmatite and is exposed in a small pit at the top of a cliff. This pegmatite consists of fine-grained and glassy quartz, pink feldspar, pink albite, pink cleavelandite, muscovite, some quartz-feldspar intergrowths. Six zones were described. In the 0.6 m intermediate zone, patches of lilac lithium mica forming up to 30% of the zone over a small area were reported (Bannatyne, 1985).

(Applies to: discovery and development) This claim was originally staked in 1925, and has had a complex history until at least 1974. Details are reported in the Manitoba Mineral Resources Library.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) A hole was drilled at a bearing of 168o, an inclination of 50°, to a depth of 57 m (188 ft). Pyrite (up to 90%) was reported in the core from drill depths of 19 to 41 m (61.6-135.5 ft) (Non-confidential Assessment File No. 92360).
