Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) From Masson and Gordon (1981): The mine was first opened in 1912 by J. Legree of Renfrew and it was sold to W.J. Spain of New York in 1915. In 1918 the ownership was taken over by Steel Alloys corporation. In the period from 1915 to 1919, the mine produced about 8 050 pounds of pure molybdenite and another 600 pounds of concentrate of unknown grade. In 1939, renewed interest in more stripping, trenching and diamond drilling which disclosed the new 'ore body' in the vicinity. In 1956-66, New Far Northern Exploration Limited sampled and drilled the deposit, but no significant mineralization was found.

(Applies to: reserve/resource assays) 25,000 tons of ore reserves grading 1.67 per cent MoS2 based on a report prepared by Velocity Surveys Ltd. (Sept. 13, 1968).

(Applies to: mineralization) From Carter et al. 1980: Mineralization consists of large, erratic distributed flakes of molybdenite associated with pyrite and pyrrhotite, disseminated within a stratabound lens measuring 61 by 16.8 m, with an average thickness of 1.5m.
