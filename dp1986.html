Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Showing A is exposed in outcrops, talus and frost heaves for a distance of 600 m. Three bedrock exposures of mineralization are located on the metric grid cut in 1977 at 0+50W to 0+00W, 0+15N to 0+25N, 1+50E to 2+50E, 0+25S to 0+50N, and 5+00E, 0+50N. Chip samples at the first location yielded 0.024 % U3O8. Five mineralized beds at the second location gave 0.013%/3.5 m, 0.003 %/1.4 m, 0.038 %/ 5.5 m, 0.011 %/5 m, and 0.013 % in a collection of random grab samples. Higher grades were obtained in grab samples at the first location; one of talus at the first locality assayed 0.153% U3O8, and another of uranophane-stained 1.014 %. Frost heaves at several localities on the grid assayed less than 0.2 %. U3O8. One test drill hole of 1977 (# 77-3) on Showing A intersected two horizons, which have true thickness of 3.9 and 1.8 m, with assays of 0.041 and 0.183 % U3O8, respectively.
