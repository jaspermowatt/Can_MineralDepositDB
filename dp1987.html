Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The showing was discovered in 1976 by Westmin crews at north shore of eastern part of Showing Lake. It was mapped in detail and assay results on chip samples across radioactive beds were reported (Schindler, 1977). Intensive surface exploration on the showing in area around it was done in 1977. It included geological mapping of the showing, regional geology of NTS 66 H 6, detailed radiometric survey, follow-up of 1976 reconnaissance geochemical survey with detailed lake, stream sediment and water surveys for a U, Ra, Cu, Ni, Co, Mo, As and Ag (Nutter, 1979; Knox, 1977, 1980). Other work in the NTS sheet included soil geochemistry, prospecting, packsack drilling, underwater scintillometry, VLF-EM and Proton magnetometer surveys.

(Applies to: mineralization) Radioactivity is detected on numerous outcrops of interbedded sandstone and siltstone beds of the Showing Lake Formation in an area 1600 x 1300 m, situated on the north shore of the eastern part of Showing Lake. Axis of a broad syncline trending 080° is located in the middle of Showing Lake, which is about 3 km long in that direction. At the showing however the beds dip 80° to the north on the north limb of a subsidiary anticline, and their strike varies from 065° to 080° averaging about 070° (Schindler, 1977; Knox, 1980). Near the lake shore the bedrock comprises micaceous siltstone and fine grained sandstone, interbedded on scale of 0.5 to 3.0 cm. To the north (up section) the beds are relatively coarser grained dominated by fine to very fine grained sandstone. The beds are more massive and the bedding is difficult to recognize. Nonetheless the mineralized beds are of fine grained, well sorted sandstone 30 cm to 2 m thick. There are 3 principal radioactive stratiform zones, distinguishable from unmineralized rocks by their red hematitic colouration and slightly coarser grain size. They occur within a stratigraphic zone less than 200 m thick. The northern one is traceable for 1400 m along strike. The middle one is traceable for a kilometre. It is the most radioactive one of the three zones and shows readings up to 33 times the background radioactivity on the McPhar Spectra 44 Gamma Ray Spectrometer. A sample from its east end assayed 0.22 % U3O8. Chemical assays of continuous chip samples across bedding from four localities gave average of the higher grade assays as approximately 0.50 lbs/short ton (Schindler, 1977). Thorium content is negligible (close to or below detection limit). An opaque Ti02 polymorph was found in mineralogical study (Knox, 1980, p. 136), which has the same needle like crystal habit as ilmenite. The study also showed that in this type of occurrences pitchblende grains in interstices between pitted sand grains are overgrown by later silica cement, in contrast with other occurrences in the Amer fold belt that have porous calcareous cement. Stratigraphically the lowest southern zone is estimated to be about 300 m above the base of the Showing Lake Formation. Regionally this upper part of the Showing Lake Formation hosts important siltstone-hosted occurrences viz., 76-1, 76-5, 76-8, 77-3 and 77-5. The lower part of the formation, about 15 to 30 m above the base of the formation, hosts showings 76-4, 76-7, 77-1 and 77-2. This distribution is also observed in the area to the east explored by Aquitaine-Cominco Joint Venture where the two radioactive zones are stratigraphically about 175 m apart (Blackwell, 1978).
