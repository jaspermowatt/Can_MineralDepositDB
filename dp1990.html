(Applies to: mineralization) This occurrence is at the northernmost tip of a lake 8 km long in N-S direction and up to 3 km wide, referred to as Yoshemite Sam Lake. It occurs in an outcrop about 100 x 50 m of Showing Lake Formation. According to Knox (1980, p.120-122) the outcrop comprises black micaceous siltstones and interbedded fine to medium grained sandstone. The ratio of sandstone to siltstone is about 2:1. The outcrops are mapped as the lower part of Showing Lake Formation (Young, 1979), on the north limb of a syncline trending east-northeast, and a trench is noted on the map. The beds strike about 075° and dip 80° to the south. Northern half of the outcrop has 5 radioactive zones that range in true thickness from 40 to 70 cm, and are separated by 70 to 120 cm of unmineralized sediments. They are defined by 1000 cps contour from readings taken by SRAT-SPP2 scintillometer. Stratigraphically lower two zones are relatively more radioactive than the others. Radioactivity correlates directly with abundance of magnetite in the rock. Pitchblende is identified by electron microprobe. It occurs as interstitial grains a few microns in diameter. Zone 1 is stratigraphically the lowest one and also the northernmost zone. It is 70 cm thick micaceous to clean, fine grained sandstone, and is overlain and underlain by siltstones. The highest assay from it, 0.14 % U3O8, came from a sample of relatively clean reddish fine grained calcareous sandstone bed 18 cm thick. Zone 2 is 80 cm thick and comprises medium grained purple radioactive calcareous sandstone beds 1 to 3 cm thick, which are surrounded by black micaceous siltstones. Zone 3 is 60 cm thick and comprises fine grained micaceous sandstone, similar to those in Zone 1 although it lacks red sandstone. The zone is slightly calcareous. Zone 4 is the least radioactive zone, about half as strong as the Zones 1, 2 and 3. It is 40 cm thick and comprises non-calcareous, well defined micaceous medium grained red sandstone. It is overlain by massive, coarser grained, well indurated arkosic sandstone. Zone 5 is 70 cm thick, and is comparable in radioactivity with Zone 4. It is composed of red, very fine grained sandstone. The sediments between the 5 zones are fine grained micaceous siltstones and medium grained silica-cemented arkosic sandstones comparable with those that overlie Zone 4. The remainder of the outcrop stratigraphically above the radioactive zones comprises black micaceous siltstones and light brown to grey, fine grained, occasionally calcareous, sandstones. In general, these rocks are interbedded on a scale of 10 to 20 cm. Weathering and lichen cover however obscure their relationships.
