Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Radioactive anomaly indicated by airborne radiometric survey in 1967. Ground follow up work was by Kalbluna Syndicate in 1969. In 1975 Imperial Oil Ltd did helicopter borne radiometric surveys and ground exploration. In 1978, a small diamond drilling program and ground surveys by Esso Minerals Ltd indicated that the zone was small and did not continue at depth.

(Applies to: mineralization) High concentration of uranium in a reddish pegmatite lens. Yellow staining of secondary uranium minerals on outcrops - a result of the weathering of uraninite.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) Nine drill holes ( 4 failed to cut any significant radioactivity) totalling 1050 feet by Esso Minerals in 1978 cut 1 to 2 lb/Ton U3O8 over widths ranging from 5 to 12.5 feet. Drill results indicate that the mineralization bottoms out at about 25 feet.
