Qualified comments: (Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) Five 'hot spot' samples located by scintillometer gave two ore grade values 7.05 and 2.81 lbs U3O8/ton. The rest 19 samples from the grid gave values between range 0.14 and 0.43 lbs/ton U3O8 (20 ppm U).

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) This showing gave a sharp 5 times background uranium peak on airborne radiometric survey. The low U values with a sharp anomaly peak was attributed to the flight path that must have been directly over the strong radioactive hot spot.

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) The low U values and small tonnage potential makes this occurrence not promising.

(Applies to: rock descriptions) A lens of white to pink coarse-grained to pegmatitic granite intrusive into strongly deformed marbles and biotite paragneisses of Penrhyn Group.

(Applies to: mineralization) The area with significant radioactivity is 90 m x 50 m.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Aquitane Co. of Canada in 1970-72 conducted airborne radiometrics and ground EM and mag surveys for uranium and base metals potential of the area. In 1977 Geological Survey of Canada's lake water and sediment survey, covering 29,800 sq km area in southern Melville Peninsula (GSC Open File reports 521, 522) located many anomalous lakes which guided the selection of the airborne radiometric survey area by Cominco and E & B Exploration Ltd.
