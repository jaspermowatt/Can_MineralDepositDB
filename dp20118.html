Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Geological mapping by the Geological Survey of Canada in 1950s and airborne radiometric survey of the Fury and Hecla Group in the northwestern part of Baffin Island and the northern portion of Melville Peninsula were done in 1976. In 1979 Dejour Mines Ltd. staked claims, DERRY, DF, BK and RT, to prospect for the unconformity type uranium deposits similar to the Athabasca deposits (Saskatchewan) or Baker Lake (NWT). The airborne radioactive anomalies were prospected. Nine trenches, 3 drill holes totalling 982.5 feet and channel sampling were carried out in 1979 and 1980.

(Applies to: mineralization) Of the 23 radiometric anomalies in this occurrence hosted by Aphebian age granitoid, most are small isolated patches with no continuity. However one vein extends to at least 400 feet and varies 9 inches to 4 feet wide. The western half is 9 inches -1 foot wide while eastern half is up to 4 feet wide. The vein is exposed by 9 trenches. It is characterized by development of coarse comb quartz, specularite, hematite and graphite. Adjacent to the quartz vein, strongly hematized breccia is developed in granite.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) The average grade of the channel samples of the main radioactive vein for the western half is approximately 0.090 % U3O8 over an average width of 3 feet, while the eastern half has an average grade is approximately 0.090 % U3O8 over an average width 3 feet.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) Three drill holes were put in 1980. DF 80-1 to depth 263 feet encountered a 5 foot section that assayed 0.022 % U3O8 including the specularite rich zone, while the specularite zone alone assayed 0.075 % U3O8.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) DF 80-2 end at 463 feet had an intersection at 256-257 feet that assayed 0.030 % U3O8.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) DF 80-3 to the depth of 256.5 feet had 3 intersections 1) 0.006 %U3O8 over 5 feet, 2) 0.010 % U3O8 over 5 feet and 3) 0.016 % U3O8 over 6 feet.
