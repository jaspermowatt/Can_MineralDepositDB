Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The showing consists of two granite outcrops 250 feet apart. The radioactive anomaly is associated with iron-staining and specularite in quartz stockwork hosted by epidotized and sheared gneissic granite. The presence of quartz stockwork close to the interpreted unconformity indicates that the unconformity is probably faulted at this location.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Three trenches were dug and channel sampling carried out in 1979. In 1980, detailed geological mapping, radon gas, spectrometer and magnetometer surveys and 3 drill holes totalling 582 feet were completed. Results were disappointing.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Geological mapping by the Geological Survey of Canada in 1950s and airborne radiometric survey of the Fury and Hecla Group in the northwestern part of Baffin island and the northern portion of Melville Peninsula was done in 1976. In 1979 Dejour Mines Ltd. staked claims, DERRY, DF, BK and RT, to prospect for the unconformity type uranium deposits similar to the Athabasca deposits (Saskatchewan) or Baker Lake (NWT). The airborne radioactive anomalies were prospected. The 5 U showings are adjacent to the faults between the late granites and redbeds of the basal formation of Fury and Hecla group.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) Channel samples 1 feet in length on both walls of the East trench returned low assays, while 5 samples from excavated material assayed between 0.025 to 0.853 % U3O8.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) BK-80-6 total 196 feet- intersected 90-95.7 feet 0.075% U3O8 including 1.7 feet of 0.10 % U3O8. The best intersection at base 0.035 % U3O8 over 1 feet

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) BK-80-7 - No U anomaly detected.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) BK-80-8 - Total 227 feet - Between 73-78 feet 0.03 % U3O8, best assay was 0.081 % U3O8 over 1 foot; Between 84-94 feet average assay 0.062 % U3O8. An unusual feature of this hole was that the bore-hole logging indicated the strongest radiometric anomaly, but the assays did not reflect that.
