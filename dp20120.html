Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The radioactivity occurs with strongly iron-stained dark-red to purple quartzite about 70 ft from the unconformity that exists between the Fury and Hecla group of rocks and the Aphebian granites and gneisses. The unconformity is also faulted at many places. About 1500 ft west a 4 ft wide barite vein occurs in the fault zone. Uranium occurs in patches on weathered joint surfaces. Four of 6 samples had anomalous uranium content.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Geological mapping by the Geological Survey of Canada in 1950s and airborne radiometric survey of the Fury and Hecla Group in the northwestern part of Baffin island and the northern portion of Melville Peninsula were done in 1976. In 1979 Dejour Mines Ltd. staked claims, DERRY, DF, BK and RT, to prospect for the unconformity type uranium deposits similar to the Athabasca deposits (Saskatchewan) or Baker Lake (NWT). The airborne radioactive anomalies were prospected. The 5 U showings are adjacent to the faults between the late granites and redbeds of the basal formation of Fury and Hecla group.
