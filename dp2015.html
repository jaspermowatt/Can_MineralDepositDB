Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Mineralization at this property was discovered by Uranerz Exploration and Mining in 1980. It is named after prospector Allan Andrew Matoush, a native Indian prospector, who died in a helicopter crash on the project (Gatzweiler, 1987). The company carried out airborne magnetic, EM and radiometric surveys, geological mapping, sampling, ground geophysics, and exploration drilling over 4 years. Then the claims were allowed to lapse. In 2002 the main Matoush property was staked by Ditem Exploration Inc. during the kimberlite staking rush. Having found no kimberlite targets, Ditem optioned the property to Strateco Resources in 2005. Subsequently Strateco acquired 100% ownership of the property and started work on it in 2006. Adjacent Eclat property on the south side is subject to an agreement made with Vija Ventures in 2005. Matoush Extension property on the north side (NTS 23D/04) was staked by Strateco Resources. Additional ground, mainly on the east and west side of the staked ground, is acquired with a joint venture agreement with Pacific Bay in 2008. In all the land package is 31 km in north-south direction and about 10 km in east-west direction, and is centred on the main mineralized zone which is along a regional north-trending fault and associated mafic dyke. It lies located at the west margin of the Otish mountain Park. Exploration since 2006 included substantial airborne geophysics, down hole geophysics, and a ITEM-III deep probing EM survey. Prospecting uncovered a number of radioactive boulders dispersed in trains in south-southwest direction. Diamond drilling was undertaken during January -June 2008, and a total of 33,794 m were drilled in 63 holes. Resource estimate compliant with NI 43-101 was released in December 2008. An underground exploration program is proposed for mid-2009.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs in the Indicator Formation along a basement-rooted north-trending fault dipping steeply to the east. It has little vertical displacement and is occupied by a gabbro dyke up to 6 m wide. An alteration halo as much as 50 m wide is developed around the structure, and comprises the inner tourmaline zone, followed by a zone of Mg-chlorite and Cr-V green mica and the outer zone of hematite and limonite. Metallic mineralization is mainly in the coarse, immature arkosic to sub-arkosic sandstone of the Active Channel Facies, which has paleoaquifer properties, as opposed to the relatively fine-grained, mature arkosic sandstone of the Channel Bar Facies. However, where the Channel Bar Facies displays increased porosity viz., its silica cement appears to be lacking, it is also is mineralized. The fault and its subsidiary fractures appear to have played a major role in transporting the mineralizing fluid to the multiple mineralized lenses that have been outlined by drilling. A dilational bend in the fault near the main zone of mineralization may suggests dextral displacement during mineralization. Mineralization is also found in one hole that penetrated the sub-Otish Group unconformity where it is faulted. The proximity of mineralization to the fault has led to its earlier classification as vein-type, but most of the ore is in sandstone, hence it is regarded here as sandstone-type controlled by fault, and because of the open fracture system, uranium is likely to have been redistributed over time. The metal may have been sourced over long distances, aided by the redbed environment of the Peribonca Formation that overlies the Indicator Formation. The associated elements like Ni, Co, As and Cr were however likely derived from the gabbro dyke. The relationship of this gabbro dyke to the Otish gabbro sills and dykes is not certain. A precise U-Pb age on baddeleyite from a sill is 2169 Ma (Hamilton and Buchan, 2007). This puts Otish Group in the same time bracket as the Huronian Supergroup, which are comparable in terms of their upper units as redbeds and lower units deposited in reducing environments prior to the oxyatmoversion (Roscoe, 1968). The U-Pb isotopic age of 1359 ± 28 Ma on pitchblende and coffinite concentrates (Hohndorf et al., 1987) is much younger.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) While the Matoush property has been the focus of most of the drilling, Strateco Resources have staked claims over the Eclat property to the south and the Matoush Extension to the north, covering the area around the trace of the Matoush Fault.
