Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) During 1974 to 1984 Uranerz carried out intensive uranium exploration in the western part of the Otish basin. The Camie River prospect is the main one among several occurrences discovered at the south margin of the basin. The initial discovery was a small occurrence in fracture zone in the gritty feldspathic sandstone of Indicator Formation. The fracture zone is 200 m long and a few centimetres wide, and trends east along the basin margin. It is 1 km east of the main zone at Camie River discovered later. Mineralization in the fracture comprised pitchblende and coffinite and is geochemically enriched in Mo, Nb, Ti, Cr, Ba and Pb. A north-northeast trending train of radioactive boulders, namely Monday boulder train, comprises 200 arkosic boulders and is located about 1.5 km west of Camie River on the same fracture zone. It is only a few hundred metres west of the main zone, which has no surface expression. Discovery of the main zone resulted from geophysical survey that located conductor in the basement, and from interpretation of high helium values in ground water from older drill holes which were drilled by earlier explorers in search for pyritic quartz pebble conglomerate-type deposit. Drilling to test the conductor located graphitic schist and the main deposit at the unconformity about 300 m below the surface. The main deposit is situated at the present south margin of the basin. The basement here has an east-trending vertical greenstone belt with intermediate flows, intermediate to felsic tuffs, cherty iron formation, graphitic schists and horizons of massive sulphides. The main concentration of uranium occurs at the unconformity along a steep east-trending fault on which the south side is thrust up. A subsidiary parallel fault occurs nearby to the south. The total vertical displacement on them is in the order of 200 m. The main concentration of uranium is at the unconformity on north side of the fault where a flat-lying cigar-shaped zone is developed. It has a strike length of 550 m. It extends 20 m into the basement and 50 m into the basal conglomerate-sandstone of the Indicator Formation. There is also a perched zone about a hundred metres above along the fault. The graphitic schist is at the surface just south of the southern reverse fault. It is radioactive and referred to as Coon Showing.
