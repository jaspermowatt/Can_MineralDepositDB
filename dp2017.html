Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Initial reconnaissance conducted during 1979-'80 over 1200 km sq area in the eastern Otis basin by Séru Nucléaire Canada Ltée. (predecessor of Cogema Canada Ltd., and now Areva Resources Canada Inc.) led to the discovery of the G, L and S prospects. The G prospect comprises four separate occurrences of radioactive boulders in glacial drift. The host lithologies are coarse arkose, quartzite and sandstone of the Indicator Formation of the Otish Group. Grades in selected float samples ranged from 0.105 to 1.89 % U. Much lower values, but still considered anomalous, were encountered in the outcrops. Uranium minerals are secondary phosphates, main one identified as sabugalite [HAl(UO2)4(PO4)4.16H2O], which is yellow and belongs to the Autunite group. Follow-up exploration included regional geological mapping, regional lake sediment surveys, airborne radiometric survey, prospecting, gridding with detailed geological mapping, magnetic surveys and soil sampling. Drilling was undertaken in 1982, and included short Winkie holes and longer wide diameter holes. No mineralization was intersected in the drill holes. It also became apparent that the mineralized boulders belonged to stratigraphically lower beds than those of more promising L prospect. Hence the exploration effort shifted to the L prospect.
