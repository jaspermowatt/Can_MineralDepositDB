Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The G, S and L prospects in the eastern Otish basin were discovered during 1979-'80 by Séru Nucléaire Canada Ltée., the predecessor company of Cogema Canada Ltd. (now Areva Resources). Intensive exploration was conducted by the company until 1985 in joint venture with SDBJ (Societé de Developpment de la Baie James (later succeeded by Soquem). The L prospect is located approximately 80 km northeast of the Matoush deposit, where significant indicated resource of uranium has been outlined. It bears striking geological similarities with the Matoush deposit except that it is hosted by stratigraphically higher strata of the Otish Group. The prospect was drill tested by Séru Nucléaire, and the company estimated resources on the basis of 33 holes on 50 m centres and over a strike length of 612 m (Caillat and Raynal, 1984). Using a cut-off grade of 0.1 % U and mineralized intercepts of 0.2 m to 6.9 m, a total of 350000 tonnes at cutoff grade of 0.1 % U averaging 0.595% U, and 0.5 g/t Au. These are historic resources (non-compliant with NI 43-101). Surface exploration and drilling located two other similar occurrences about 5 and 12 km northeast of l prospect, namely OELV-290 and OELU-299, respectively (Pauwels, 2005).

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization is associated with a subvertical gabbro dyke striking N70°E, which was emplaced along a fault zone in the flat-lying strata of the Otish Group and has the south-side downdrop of at least 150 m. The gabbro dyke formed a sill-like protrusion at the level of the dolomite horizon which is 10 to 20 m thick, and is much deformed because of the intrusion. Uranium mineralization appears to have pooled below the carbonate unit in the sandstone and also in gabbro (Caillat and Raynal, 1984). Mineralized zone is ribbon-like along the fault, and has been traced for 1700 m, but is continuous only over the eastern 600 m. Its depth extent is less than 110 m below the surface. Mineralized zones are pervasively altered and brecciated, and the gabbro is bleached grey. Width of mineralized zone varies up to a maximum of 7 m. Drilling has shown that nearly half of the mineralization is in sandstone of the Indicator formation. Much of the rest of mineralization is in gabbro, and the alteration minerals in it include albite, carbonate, quartz and sericite. Clay alteration, sericitization and silicification in sandstone may be partly or wholly due diagenesis. Elements associated with the uranium mineralization include Bi, Au, Ag, Cu, Pb, Se and Te (Pauwels, 2005). The mineralization has many similarities with that seen at the Matoush prospect, except that it is at a higher stratigraphic level.
