Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Uraninite occurs in quartz-biotite schist interbedded with gneissose quartzite of Archean age. These rocks form the basement of the lower Proterozoic Nonacho Group sediments. The mineralized units are 10 to 100 ft length and 3-4 inches wide. A number of mineralized units make up a mineralized zone or a showing. The N-14 (see DEPNO 1408), about 315 m E-SE, is another similar showing in James Mountain area.
