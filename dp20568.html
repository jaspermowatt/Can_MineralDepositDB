Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Airborne radiometric surveys indicated anomalies in 1968-69. Five trenches were blasted on west side of the peninsula. There is insignificant amount of U except in Trench 4. The radioactivity in other trenches were due to clots of K-feldspar laths. There are discordant pegmatite dyke-like masses in biotite gneiss. The pegmatites are narrow, have no particular trend and of no lateral extent. The largest pegmatite noted on TOP 1-21 and TSU 108 claims was noted to be 40 ft in length.

(Applies to: regional geology) The rocks underlying Tops and TSU Claims are Biotite gneiss, granite gneiss and younger granite of Fort Smith Belt. These rocks are correlative with Tazin Group in Beaverlodge area, Saskatchewan and are Archean to lower Proterozoic age. The granitization has obliterated most of the original features.
