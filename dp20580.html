Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The initial TSU Claims were staked in 1970 by COMINCO to cover the radiometric anomalies discovered by the G.S.C. airborne reconnaissance survey of 1969. Additional claims "A" and "B" were staked adjoining TSU claims on TSU Property. Exploration by COMINCO included detailed helicopter-borne and ground radiometric surveys, prospecting, sampling and 9 trenches.

(Applies to: mineralization) The porphyritic granite is anomalously radioactive caused by dispersed concentration of radioactive minerals. The showings with significant grades of uranium occur as chloritic fracture-fillings with fine-grained pitchblende. The radioactive shears in granitic rocks carry up to 0.3 % ThO2 but usually less than 0.04 % U3O8. The common radioactive mineral is uranothorite. U concentration increases in areas of intense quartz mobilization and shear zones.
