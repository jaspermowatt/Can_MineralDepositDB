Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The mineralization is fracture-controlled. More than 76 radioactive spot highs -1000 to >15000 cps (SRAT-SPP2 scint) occur on an isolated 50 m x 80 m and 8 m high knoll surrounded by lake and muskeg. Spot highs are related to fractures and fracture sets trending 034 to 050 and 330 to 354 degrees. Ten of the 12 spot highs which register >15000 cps are concentrated in an 11 m x 16 m area of conglomeratic and quartz-vein brecciated rocks. The quartz-feldspar fragments are cut by 2 sets of quartz veins. Fractures are filled by quartz, hematite, chlorite, limonite and pyrite.

(Applies to: rock descriptions) The host rocks are cataclasized or mylonitized, partially albitized and silicified porphyroblastic feldspar-quartz-chlorite cataclasite of basement and Nonacho Group quartz pebble conglomerate and quartzite.
