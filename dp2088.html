Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) This zone, discovered in 1978, consists of a group of 15 anomalies which occur along a length of 300 m in one segment of the microdiorite (diabase?) dyke trending 140 degrees. The dyke is laterally displaced 125 m to north and 250 m in south by two dextral and sinistral faults respectively. The width of the dyke varies 25-30 m. Anomaly - AN-23, the most important in this zone and with greatest lateral extent, occurs 17 m from the feldspathic quartzite contact with the dyke. A massive pitchblende vein 2 cm wide is traced to 1.5 m inside an open fracture trending 134 degrees and subvertical.

(Applies to: location and access) The location of the most important anomaly, AN-23, (sample N-502B) is chosen to represent this 300 m long Northern Zone.
