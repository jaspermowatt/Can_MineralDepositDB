Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Mineralized float, grading as high as 4 % U in small samples was discovered in 1982 by prospectors following up on a uranium lake sediment anomaly in eastern Otish basin for Cogema (Canada) Ltd. Two clusters of radioactive boulders are located about a kilometre east-southeast of small Lac Tion. An area 2.5 x 4 km that covered the discovery was gridded for detailed mapping, soil sampling, VLF-Resistivity and magnetic surveys. Drilling was carried out in 1984, however these deep holes did not locate the source of radioactive boulders. In 1985 short Winkie drill holes located a 10 m wide dolerite dyke that trends northeast and occupies a fault. Radioactivity was found on the south contact of the dyke. One sample from the radioactive zone yielded 0.05 % U over 0.8 m. The host strata are of siltstones and sandstones belonging to the Peribonca Formation and include beds of carbonate cemented breccias. At the showing these are sheared and mylonitized. Alteration minerals include chlorite, clay and barite. Detailed soil and lake sediment surveys indicate that the uranium mineralization extends along the northeast-trend of the dyke for 2.5 km. Further follow-up was curtailed on the notion that the mineralization is stratigraphically y too high relative to the more promising L prospect.
