Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The Grehawk deposit was mined between 1957 and 1959. Ore delivered to the Faraday Mill during 1958 totalled 52,517 short tonnes (47,643 metric tons) grading 0.08114 % U3O8. The company reported that about 181,440 metric tons averaging 0.065 % U3O8 (0.055 % U) remained in the ground around the workings when the mined closed (Griffith, 1986).

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs in a system of pegmatite-granite dykes intruding metagabbroic gneiss. Radioactive minerals are most abundant where mafic contents are higher, and where the hosts are fractured and friable. Variable amounts of chlorite were found with the ore, which was never found in the metagabbro unless in contact with the ore hosts.
