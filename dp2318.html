Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Westmin Resources Ltd. commissioned an airborne radiometric survey over the northeast part of company's property in 1978. The survey was carried out by Geoterrex Ltd. over the area of NTS Sheets 66 H 4, 5, 6, 7, 10, 11, 12; G 1, 2 and 8. The R 22 was one of the anomalies recommended by Geoterrex for further exploration. A brief ground check in 1979 located radioactive boulders. Regional geochemical surveys (shallow water and lake bottom) along with geological mapping and prospecting, were conducted by Westmin staff in the region during 1981. Detailed exploration in 1982 included boulder prospecting, soil radon, scintillometer, VLF and magnetic surveys. Work in a new grid comprising 50 and 25 m line-spacing and total of 44 km of lines, revealed a total of 255 radioactive boulders, most of them of the Itza, Showing Lake and Oora formations of the Amer Group, and some of younger syenites. The boulders and cobbles showed readings up to 15,000 cps in McPhar TV -1 spectrometer. They were mineralized along fractures. A suite of 8 samples showed assays averaging 23.5 lbs/ton uranium, with highest value of 63 lbs/ton (2.9 % U3O8. These data suggest the possibility of syngenetic sedimentary mineralization upgraded later as the vein-type or unconformity-associated mineralization (Evoy and Volz, 1981; Kretschmar, 1982). One of the boulders of this type assayed 676 ppb Au along with 480 ppm U. Geophysical surveys indicate a possible local bedrock source of the boulders. A diamond drill program is recommended to test unexposed source of the boulders indicated by VLF-EM anomalies (Nicholls, 2005).
