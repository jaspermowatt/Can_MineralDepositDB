Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Boulders in the main 3 km long train are mostly white to pale salmon coloured, coarse feldspathic quartzite of Oora Lake Formation, 68 of which averaged 0.11 % U; secondary yellow coating occurs along fractures. Scattered boulders over 2 km width across the main boulder train are rounded arkose-siltstone of the Showing Lake Formation, 26 of which averaged 0.34 % U. All the boulders are of the 'sandstone - tabular' type. Some of the boulders however show hematite-clay alteration, indicative of processes at the sub-Thelon Formation unconformity. Hence the possibility of unconformity-associated deposits exists in the region and a lot of exploration was focused on it (Hattie, D. W., 1981).

(Applies to: regional geology) Sub-Thelon Formation unconformity is exposed close to the claim area; fractures in the Amer Group strata here show kaolinization, chloritization and weak radioactivity regarded as Unconformity-associated.
