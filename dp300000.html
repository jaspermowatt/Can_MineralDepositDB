Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) At Blue Hills, the hosting felsic volcanic of Ordovician age dips steeply to the north and appears to be the north limb of a tightly folded, west plunging anticline. Interpretation of ground geophysical, geochemical and radiometric work indicates that the southern limb of the anticline is located 300-400 metres south of the Main Zone (DEPNO-300002).

(Applies to: discovery and development) Shell Canada staked the area in 1980 and carried out preliminary geological and geochemical work. Regional mapping was done by Chorlton (1980) for Newfoundland Dept. of Mines. From 1985-87 B.P Selco held the area, which was acquired by CEGB Explorations Canada in 1987. Since 1987 there had been no activity because of low uranium price and demand till 2005, when Commander Resources assembled approximately 40,000 hectares of the land in Blue Hills and White Bear area of Southern Newfoundland covering the Hermitage Flexure. The company continues the exploration in 2007.

(Applies to: genetic model) The showing was first found in quartz-feldspar porphyry related to the Ordovician Baggs Hill Granite and was thought to be of magmatic-hydrothermal type of U mineralization. The peribatholithic model of exploration was pursued. However further investigations and discovery of other U showings in the Blue Hills area indicated that uranium concentrations are stratabound in metavolcanics and metasediments of the Bay du Nord Group and may have volcanogenic origin.

(Applies to: alteration) One lobe of the Baggs Hill granite is intensely albitized. This type of alteration occurs regionally along a prominent shear zone along the north-northwest side of the Dolman tuff, and pervasively in the Piglet rhyolite that underlies the Dolman tuff to the east and southeast. Association with a sodic metasomatite is suspected by the third compiler.
