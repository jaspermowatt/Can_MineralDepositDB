Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Ground scintillometer prospecting discovered U showings by Shell Minerals and worked by C.E.G.B. in early 1980s. In 2005 Commander Resources acquired 40,000 hectares of land in southern Newfoundland covering a strike length of more than 100 km and also acquired an additional 1600 claims covering the Hermitage Flexure geological belt. An agreement with Pathfinder Resources increased another 1429 claims to the east and west of the Commander Resources ' claims. In 2005 fall Commander performed mapping, grab, channel and chip sampling, prospecting and back hoe trenching. The prospecting continues into 2007.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) Analyses of 111 rock chip samples from many showings in the Blue Hills property revealed 18 samples with >0.18 % and 33 samples with >0.09 % U3O8 and 57 samples returned values >0.045 % U3O8 . Highest values reported were 0.56 %. A channel sample from the Main showing at Blue Hills ran 0.11 % U3O8 over 1.5 m, 0.18 % U3O8 over 1.4 and 0.14% U3O8 over 1.3 m. Three of the 5 composite rock chip samples of the bedrock from the trench assayed 0.10 to 0.18 % U3O8 consistent with the channel sample assays. One grab sample was assayed at 2.88% U and 1400 g/t Mo.

(Applies to: mineralization) Detailed mapping, prospecting and sampling show that the uranium mineralization to be stratabound within fine-grained volcanic host and continues over for 10 m.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) The showing also consists of over 300 metavolcanic boulders extending over an area 1200 m long N-S and varies from 70-400 m in width. Boulders range from 20 cm to 1.5 m with 500 - 25,000 cps radioactivity. The transport distance of the boulders is thought to be short - less (> 1 km).

(Applies to: genetic model) The uranium concentration may be a result of a) the intrusion of the Peter Snout Granite into the Bay du Nord Group causing uranium precipitation either directly from the magma or indirectly as a result of the heat of intrusion, or b) uranium enrichment in specific siliceous volcanics of the Bay du Nord Group.

(Applies to: genetic model) The fine grained metavolcanic rocks in this area are intensely and pervasively recrystallized and albitized. Association of the uranium mineralization with epigenetic sodic metasomatism is also a possibility.
