Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Westfield Minerals Ltd. in 1976-78 prospected the area and discovered high grade boulders and radioactive anomalies. From 1979 to 1982 Northgate performed geochemical and geophysical prospecting including stream and soil sampling that indicated linear mineralized limestone in outcrop and soil anomalies that gave values up to 285 ppm U. Airborne and ground magnetic, radiometric and VLF-EM surveys done and radon soil gas measured. Diamond drilling was attempted but abandoned because the holes collapsed. The Deer Lake Basin is again being actively explored for uranium in 2006-07.

(Applies to: property geology) The limestone of the Rocky Brook Formation (Middle Deer Lake Group) hosts epigenetic stratabound uranium, which can be traced up to several kms along strike. The Rocky Brook Formation consists of mainly gray, green and red interbedded mudstone and shales with minor limestone beds. They are interpreted to have had deposited in shallow lacustrine environment in fault controlled basin as Post-Ordovician Overlap sequence.

(Applies to: mineralization) A limestone bed, 20-30 cm thick, hosts uranium mineralization. It is underlain by green-grey silty mudstone and overlain by gray shales. The mineralized limestone is detectable by geochemical and radiometric anomalies and is traceable for 12 km.
