Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Ground prospecting and tracing the magnetic trends east of Doucette prospect led to the discovery of DT-1 Prospect 4 km east of Doucette just downhill from the eastern extension of the linear magnetic high.

(Applies to: mineralization) U mineralization is sediment hosted in staurolite porphyroblastic semipelitic schist and interbedded tuffs of mid-Ordovician Bay du Nord Group. Radioactivity is concentrated over 30 cm width in schist cut by quartz veins. The U mineralization is mainly syngenetic, stratiform in nature in felsic volcaniclastic rocks. Some remobilization occurred during the later deformation and metamorphism. The source of uranium is considered to be the volcanic rocks. The association of U mineralization with the aplite dykes and quartz veins may be related to the post deformation Devonian granitic intrusives.

(Applies to: rock analysis and sample assays) Sampled assayed by Commander Resources in July 2005 ranged from 599.5 to 722 ppm and August 2005 from 0.002 to 0.108 % U3O8.
