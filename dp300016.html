Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1980-91 Shell Canada Resources discovered an area of high radioactivity at Lost Pond by ground follow up of an airborne radiometric anomaly. In May 2006 Hard Rock Uranium examined, sampled and the carried out combined radiometric, magnetic and EM survey over 100 sq km at Lost Pond and discovered several radiometric anomalies including Dome-1 and Dome-2 and U3 area 2 km west of Dome-1. In September 2006, Hard Core Uranium combined with Birchpoint Resources and formed Ucore Uranium Inc. A 40 km grid was cut on Lost Pond showings and detailed, mapping, geology, geochemistry, geophysics, trenching and diamond drilling have been carried out in 2006-07.

(Applies to: mineralization) A magnetite-rich syenite phase of the Late Proterozoic Hare Hill Intrusive Suite was observed to be highly radioactive, and contain, in addition, substantial REE. Fracture filling and disseminated uranium mineralization is also found in Fleur-de-Lys type calc-silicate bearing schists affected by strong hematization and by clay alteration. There is likely more than one cause of uranium enrichment in this area.
