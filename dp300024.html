Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Radioactive anomalies and radioactive boulders started uranium exploration in Deer Lake Basin in 1976 and 1978 by Westfield Minerals Ltd. In 1979 Northgate Exploration Ltd. explored the area by carrying out geochemical and geophysical surveys and located Firko anomaly, which was confirmed by subsequent radon survey and diamond drilling. The area was reverted back to the crown by 1984. Inco Gold Ltd. held the mineral rights in 1988. The U exploration actively in the Deer Lake Basin restarted in 2005 and continues in 2007.

(Applies to: mineralization) Uraniferous beds in the area correlate with similar horizons in the Cormack area and therefore have strike length of about 27 km, but attain no appreciable thickness.
