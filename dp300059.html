Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 2002, carbonatite complex was identified by South Bay Exploration Ltd. Six drill holes were drilled in 2006.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) Eden Lake lies within a regional zone (8km x 25 km) of anomalously high eU and eTh defined by GSC airborne radiometric survey in 1980's. Subsequent ground scintillometer by the Manitoba Geological Survey identified "hotspot" at Eden Lake.

(Applies to: alteration) Widespread hydrothermal alteration, fenitization of the carbonatite host rock.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) 6 targets defined by geochemical signature, magnetics, low ground to focus on linear features that might control the emplacement of mineralized bodies. Six holes totalling 765 m were drilled. Four holes intersected carbonatite containing REE bearing apatite, within zones of carbonate-altered rocks (50 metres long in hole 3).

(Applies to: diamond drill hole assays) No economic grades found in 2006 drilling, but only 5 of 10 targets were tested.
