(Applies to: discovery and development) Discovered in 1969 by drilling an airborne radiometric anomaly in a completely overburden-covered area with uranium-bearing float. There was 5182 metres of drilling in 61 drillholes on 4 zones.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization recognized at Boulder Lake is dominated by uraninite in pegmatites interpreted to be derived from partial melting of adjacent pelitic rocks. Best results are from a 1969 drillhole with 0.19 % U3O8 over an apparent width of 9m. including 1.5 m. at 0.49% U3O8 at Anomaly C.

(Applies to: geophysical surveys) In 2005, detailed airborne magnetic survey (80 m altitude, 150 m line-spacing). Previous airborne and detailed ground geophysics.
