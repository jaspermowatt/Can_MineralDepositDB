Qualified comments: (Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Called North East Athabasca Project in 2005, and North East Wollaston Project in 2006, both explored by CanAlaska Uranium Ltd.

(Applies to: location and access) The location of the Snyder Lake (where the best U3O8 analysis was found) is from http://gnss.nrcan.gc.ca/gnss-srt/searchName.jsp?language=en. The CanAlaska North East Project spans the Manitoba-Saskatchewan border.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Prospecting and lake-sediment sampling were performed in 2005. The focus of the project is shear-hosted deposits and other basement-hosted unconformity-type deposits in an area where many uranium showings were found in the late 1970s. Surface sampling in 2006, based on 2005 results. Assay results revealed multiple areas returning 0.5 to 11.1% U3O8 and some samples returned high molybdenum values. The uranium mineralization is associated with granitic and altered sedimentary rocks.
