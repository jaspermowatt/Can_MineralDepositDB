Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The FUKI outcrop was discovered during a vehicle-borne scintillometer survey in 1968. The property was staked for Nissho-Iwai Canada Ltd. and exploration was carried out by the Power Reactor and Nuclear Fuel Development Corporation, of Japan. Work on the property, during the decade prior to the uranium moratorium in 1980, consisted of geological mapping, radiometric surveys, trenching, property surveys and at least 2616 metres of diamond drilling in 49 holes.

(Applies to: reserve/resource assays) The FUKI deposits are estimated to contain 477,500 tonnes grading 0.033 % uranium and have an average thickness of 1.36 metres (Assessment Report 8105).

(Applies to: mineralization) Secondary uranium mineralization is largely concentrated in the basal conglomerate and occurs as films on pebbles and in the matrix of loosely consolidated conglomerates and carbonaceous sediments. Autunite is the only uranium mineral identified.

(Applies to: regional geology) The area is underlain by granite and quartz monzonite of the Cretaceous-Tertiary Okanagan Batholith. Unconformably overlying the plutonic rocks are tuffs and flows and related volcaniclastic sediments of the Eocene Marron Formation, Penticton Group. The volcanics are cut by Eocene Coryell syenite and monzonite intrusives and dykes, and younger dacite feeder dykes, correlative with Marron Formation.
