Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Low temperature U-Ni mineralization formed initially as supergene enrichment in tectonic traps by weathering of basement rocks, and was later redistributed during diagenesis of the Athabasca Group. Pre-Athabasca weathering led to supergene enrichment of uranium and associated metals, which was later remobilized during burial metamorphism, diagenesis and faulting of the Athabasca Group. Unconformity Associated Uranium: Type 1 of SMDI; Discovery in 1978 of the Key Lake deposits associated with graphitic metasediments was the key to additional discoveries based on its deposit model.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Discovered in 1975. 1985-1997; open pit; reserves in 1990: 47306 t U in 2.77 Mt ore grading 1.71 % U; total production (2 open pits + cobble ore): 73900 t U averaging 2 % U in 3.695 Mt of ore when mining ceased in 1997. Key Lake ores mined from Gaertner and Dielmann open pits, and till containing high grade cobbles derived from the 1 km long zone between them by Pleistocene glacier and deposited to the southwest.

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) Cameco reported proven reserves remaining on December 31, 2006, and 61,900 metric tones of 0.52% U3O8, which has been left in the ground. The mill processes ore from McArthur River.
