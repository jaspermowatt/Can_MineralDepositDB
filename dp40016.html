Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) A glacial bounder train led to discovery in 1978; outlined by 488 drill holes; min-able resources in 1981: 19300 t U in 1.2 Mt of ore averaging 1.6 % U and 1.59 % Ni; plus 2300 t U in lower grade ore. in 1988-1989 test mining from a 185 m shaft; revised estimate of mine-able reserves in 1990: 13850 t U in 0.363 Mt of ore averaging 3.816 % U at cut-off grade of 0.424 % U; same figures reported in 2001. Most of the high grade ore occurs at northeast end of the deposit, where ore zone is as much as 150 m wide and occurs at the sub-Athabasca unconformity; basement ore extends for minimum 100 m below it. The main zone is under water at southwest end of Mink Arm of Midwest Lake; subsidiary discontinuous small zones, referred to as A, B and C zones, occur to the northeast along Mink Arm over 2.5 km strike zone. The property lay dormant from 1995, when the focus was on the opening of the nearby McLean Lake facilities under the same ownership, until approximately 2002-2003, during which time ownership changes culminated by renewed exploration and development. AREVA (69.16% ownership) is the operator of the property as of 2006, with Denison Mines (25.17%) and OURD (5.67%). In December, 2007, Areva announced the decision to go ahead with development of an open pit, expected to produce about 14000 metric tons of uranium metal. An underground zone, and the Mae occurrence to the North may be developed subsequently.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs in the Paleohelikian Sandstone above an unconformity with Aphebian basement rocks and in the basement below the unconformity, with the bulk of the high grade material located in either the basal conglomerate or in the upper basement unit near the unconformity. It generally follows a northeast-trending structural corridor parallel the strike of a basement metasedimentary graphitic unit. High-grade lenses within the corridor are elongated to N70E, contain 1-4 m thick sections of massive uranium oxides and niccolite surrounded by thin clay envelopes. They are separated by lower grade mineralization 1-2 m in thickness and grading 0.05-0.5% U3O8. Local mineralized lenses occur along steep faults above ('perched') and below ('deep basement mineralization') the main unconformity mineralization.
