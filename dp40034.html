Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) This deposit was discovered in 1992 at sub-Athabasca unconformity 705 m below the surface by drill testing of the 10 km long Saskatoon Lake EM conductor, which trends 330°. The test revealed 0.62 % eU over 0.7 m in the discovery hole. The main Anne zone is 500 m long, has variable width up to 125 m and thickness of a few metres to a few tens of metres. The Collette zone, located on the same conductor 1.5 km to the NW, may be as large.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization at Shea Creek occurs in bends in the traces of a conductive graphitic unit and in fault zones discordant to the northwest trend of local lithologies. Displacements appear to strike north-northeast (clockwise of the lithological trends). Mineralization at the Anne Zone occurs in three styles and settings: fracture-controlled zones in the pre-Athabasca basement, zones of perched pervasive and fracture-controlled mineralization in sandstones above the unconformity, and zones of disseminated, nodular and massive mineralization proximal to the unconformity. The mineralized zones in the sandstone are hosted by the Shea Creek Sequence, the sandstone-dominated, 97 m thick, lowermost unit of the Athabasca Group in the Shea Creek-Douglas River transect.
