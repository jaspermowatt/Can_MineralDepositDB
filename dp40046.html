Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The showing is located near southwest boundary of the Thelon Game Sanctuary, and about 30 km southwest of Beaverhill Lake which is nearest large lake. Most of the claims are located close to the boundary of the flat-lying Thelon Formation and metamorphosed basement rocks. Access to the claims is by float- or ski-equipped aircraft to Boomerang Lake, which is located 2 km north of main mineralized zone intersected by drill holes.

(Applies to: discovery and development) --The Boomerang Lake prospect is the first polymetallic unconformity-type uranium occurrence identified in the southwestern part of Thelon Basin. It is comparable with the Key Lake deposit in Athabasca basin in terms of the geological setting. It was discovered by drilling in 1983 at a depth of about 100 m. Initial exploration in this part if the basin was during 1979-1982 when most of it was under exploration permits and claims held by several major companies. Surface exploration led to a few uranium-bearing veins and fracture fillings (Gibbins, 1985). The area of Boomerang Lake prospect was staked by Hudbay Oil and Gas Company Limited where airborne EM survey detected conductors in overburden covered area. They were tested by 5 percussion drill holes totalling 663 m in 1980, three of which intersected the unconformity of flat-lying Thelon sandstone with graphitic metapelite in basement at depth of 90 to 147 m. --In 1982 Urangesellschaft Canada Limited and Alberta Energy Company entered into joint venture agreement with Dome Petroleum Limited, which by then had acquired the assets of the Hudson Bay Oil and Gas Company Limited, to further explore the area. Detailed geophysical and geochemical surveys in 1982 better defined a group of northeast-trending conductors near Boomerang Lake. They were tested in 1983 with 36 diamond drill holes totalling 3429 m, which led to the discovery of mineralization in a few holes with larger alteration hallow (Davidson and Gandhi, 1989). The best assay was from hole BL-83-21, which intersected 22.4 g/t Au, 12.3 g/t Ag and 5,003 ppm U3O8 over 0.5 meter in strongly altered sandstone at the faulted unconformity contact. --During 1991-'92 PNC Exploration Canada Limited staked claims Boom 1-5 covering the area of the conductors, and drilled of 9 holes in 1991 totalling 1773.9 m and 12 holes in 1992 totalling 2678.8 m, along northeastern extension of the conductors drilled earlier. (Davidson and Shigeta, 1992). The two best intersections were in holes BL-92-40 and 48 in the basement just below the unconformity; the former in chloritized pelitic gneiss contained 401 ppm U, 709 ppb Au, 444 ppm V, 70 ppb Pt and 84 ppb Pd, and the latter in unaltered garnetiferous gneiss contained 280 ppm U, 390 ppb Au, 31 ppb Pt and 41 ppb Pd and 365 ppm Ni. --From 1992 to 1997 the property remained dormant. In 1998 Uravan Minerals Incorporated revived exploration with geophysical survey of selected lines and drilling of 10 holes totalling 1322 m at the locality where mineralization was intersected in 1983. The most significant intersection was in hole BL-98-52 from 83.5 to 84.5 m, immediately beneath the unconformity. The section assayed 10.17 g/t Au, 595 ppm U, 358 ppb Pt, 497 ppb Pd, 5.7 ppm Ag: Superimposed on, and beneath this zone is an interval of strong Ni, Co, Cu and As. The section 84.0 - 85.0 m contained >10,000 ppm As, 3,640 ppm Ni, 6,090 ppm Co, 419.5 ppm Cu (Uravan Annual Report, 2004). In 2005 the company entered into a joint venture with Cameco Corporation, and jointly acquired additional ground adjacent to the Boomerang property viz., STD, BW, ER and SL claims adjacent to BM claims. In 2007 a multi-faceted surface sampling program was conducted on the expanded property. Since then, further exploration is on hold pending the acquisition of a Land Use Permit. Meanwhile, Uravan has swapped its shares in Boomerang Lake with Cameco for a handful of Cameco prospects in the Athabasca Basin.

(Applies to: mineralization) Uranium-bearing mineralization occurs along fractures in basement rocks and in the Thelon Formation, as well as along the unconformable contact between the two. The U-Au-Ni-Cu-Co-As metallic signature clearly defines the Boomerang style of uranium mineralization with the polymetallic unconformity-type uranium deposits that occur in the Athabasca Basin such as the high-grade Cigar Lake and Key Lake deposits, and represents the first unconformity-type uranium showing in the Thelon Basin (Uravan Annual Report, 2004; Davidson and Gandhi, 1989). The best intersection was in strongly altered, poorly consolidated sandstone immediately above the unconformity; several other intersections in sandstone and basement rocks within 7.5 m of the unconformity showed lower, but distinctly anomalous, concentrations of U-Au. All mineralized intersections are in or close to fractured and/or brecciated host rocks that are both altered and proximal to the unconformity.
