Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Early efforts of uranium exploration in 1960s focussed along the basal unconformity of the Baker Lake Group in eastern part of the Baker Lake basin. Following the recognition of Unconformity-associated uranium deposits in the Athabasca basin in Saskatchewan, a helicopter-borne radiometric survey along the margins of the Angikuni and Yathkyed sub-basins of the Dubawnt Supergroup. Radioactive conglomerate was detected during this survey, which led to the follow-up airborne magnetic and EM surveys and detailed ground scintillometer, magnetic and VLF surveys. Prospecting done at the same time revealed numerous fracture-controlled pitchblende-hematite-carbonate veins within the Archean metavolcanics adjacent to the overlying Paleoproterozoic conglomerate of the Angikuni sub-basin. These veins form the Lac Cinquante deposit. Drilling done on the deposit by 1982 totalled 19812 m. The drill indicated resources are 11.6 million pounds of U3O8 (Aberford Resources Annual Report, 1982). Average grade is very close to 1.03 % U3O8 (0.848 % U).

(Applies to: property geology) The main hosts are brittle deformed Archean metamorphosed tuffaceous metasediments. The main styles of mineralization are fracture-hosted pitchblende veins, disseminated pitchblende with sulphides in brecciated hosts, and fracture controlled, brecciated, hematite-pitchblende-quartz-carbonate veins.

(Applies to: mineralization) Colloform and sooty pitchblende fills veins and hairline fractures, forms selvages on altered volcanic fragments in breccias and is disseminated through pervasively hematized and chloritized wall rocks. Multiple generations of pitchblende are indicated by textural and reflectivity variations. Galena, possibly of radiogenic parentage, and molybdenite aggregates are commonly associated with pitchblende concentrations. Chalcopyrite is associated with pitchblende in carbonate matrix of breccia and carbonate veins. No silver phases have been identified. Coffinite, covellite and digenite occur as near surface oxidation products. Polymetallic mineralization is regarded as the result of two mineralizing processes. Firstly, the low grade copper+zinc±lead mineralization was associated with the exhalative sediments. Subsequently, deformation was localized in the exhalative unit and resulted in increased permeability and porosity. This provided access to the CO2-rich mineralizing solution through this horizon. Deposition of uranium, molybdenum and silver occurred by reaction of the solution with the carbonaceous sulphide-bearing sediments and mafic volcanic rocks. Remobilization of uranium and copper likely formed the uranium-copper association seen in the open fracture-controlled veins (Miller et al., 1986). The present topography of the deposit area is near to the paleosurface on which strata of the Baker Lake Group were deposited as indicated by relict patches of hematitic paleoweathered greenstone, remnants of the basal breccia of the group, clastic dykes in the greenstone belt. Autunite was reported as disseminations and in veins in the basal conglomerate of the Baker Lake Group and its basement. Role the unconformity in the mineralization process, if any, is not clear.

(Applies to: regional geology) The series of deposits and occurrences are located adjacent to the northeastern margin if the Angikuni sub-basin, Baker Lake group, and is hosted in the metavolcanic and lesser metavolcaniclastic rocks of the Archean Henik group.
