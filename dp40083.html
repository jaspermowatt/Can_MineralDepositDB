Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) An airborne EM and magnetic survey in 1977 revealed strong conductors in the Neoarchean basement in the vicinity of unconformity with the Late Paleoproterozoic Baker Lake Group. The exploration was based on the model of Key Lake deposit at the sub-Athabasca Group unconformity in Saskatchewan. The targeted anomaly is in glacial drift covered area, which forms a northeast-trending depression bounded by low hills. Maximum relief in the area is 70 m with local relief of 10 m. Outcrops comprise less than 5 % of the area. Following detailed ground geophysical surveys and prospecting, diamond drilling was carried out during 1977-1978. Twenty-five holes totalling 1797.5 m were drilled at the Rib prospect. Of these 14 holes intersected mineralization. The best values encountered were 0.52 % U3O8 (0.441 % U) and 0.63 % MoS2 over 2.6 m. The drill holes were widely spaced and the intersections varied widely in grade. Hence no resource estimate was undertaken.

(Applies to: mineralization) Polymetallic mineralization of the Rib prospect is regarded as hydrothermal. Source of the mineralizing solution is however not known. Most features of the mineralization are similar to those of the Lac Cinquante deposit located several kilometres to the northeast in the Angikuni sub-basin. The main difference is in the principal host rock, which are the brecciated quartz-feldspar mylonite at the Rib prospect and the chert-sulphide-chlorite bearing graphitic tuff at the Lac Cinquante deposit. Graphitic sulphide-bearing exhalative sediment or tuff horizons occur in proximity of the mineralized zones at the Rib prospect, but they appear to have only a minor influence on the mineralizing process (Miller et al., 1986).
