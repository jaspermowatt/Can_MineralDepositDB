Qualified comments: (Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) The first uranium airborne and ground prospecting in the area surrounding this prospect was undertaken in late 1960, and covered the region from Bissett Lake to the eastern end of Baker Lake. Radioactivity was discovered in the Paleoproterozoic rocks of Baker Lake basin overlying the Archean basement. Further exploration in 1974 included airborne radiometric and magnetic surveys, and ground follow-up of strong airborne anomalies. This work located the promising 74-1E and 74-1W showings in the Kazan Formation. During 1975-1976 the showings were investigated further by the Cominco-Pan Ocean Joint Venture. The work included ground IP, resistivity, magnetometer and VLF-EM surveys, lake water and soil geochemical surveys, geological mapping on scale 1 : 10000, and diamond drilling totalling 1413 m in 17 holes during 1975, and 1373 m in 11 holes during 1976. The drilling tested a strike length of 700 m. The best drill hole was KZ-10, which intersected mineralization at 3 intervals along a 120 metre length, including 0.84 % U3O8 (0.71 % U) over 5.4 m at about 110 m depth.
