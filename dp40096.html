Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) During 1969 New continental Oil Company of Canada conducted an airborne radiometric survey (flown by Scintrex) and an airborne magnetic survey (flown by Geoterrex) over the Claim Groups 1 and 2 held by the company. The radiometric survey resulted in the discovery of the 69-4 showing comprised of 3 northerly trending en echelon zones within a 200 m wide corridor. The magnetic survey aided structural interpretation. The follow-up exploration in 1970 included reconnaissance and detailed geological mapping, prospecting, ground geophysical surveys and diamond drilling (AQ wire line). The drilling totalled 2274 feet (693.1 m) in 6 holes. The best intersection was at the north end of the eastern zone, with an intersection of 1.68 % U3O8 (1.43 % U) over 2.4 m. This and other prospects in the region were acquired in 2005 by Kaminak Gold corp., and exploration initiated in 2006 in joint venture with Pacific Ridge Exploration Ltd.
