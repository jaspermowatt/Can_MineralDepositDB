Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The 1982 exploration focused on search for Unconformity-associated uranium deposits at the basal unconformity of the Baker Lake sequence, which is best exposed at the eastern end of Baker Lake and along its southern margin, particularly at the west end of Thirty Mile Lake. It forms an open syncline trending ENE. It was deposited on Archean/Paleoproterozoic basement of paragneiss, amphibolite and foliated granitoid rocks. Recent work on the Baker Lake sequence describes lithofacies assemblages representing alluvial fan, braided river, ephemeral lake and eolian depositional environment (Rainbird et al., 2003). The lower South Channel Formation is exposed at the east end of Baker Lake, and grades upward into grit-sandstone unit, the Kazan Formation, which is 600 m thick. It is in turn succeeded by voluminous potassic volcanic unit, the Christopher Island Formation. The 1982 exploration focused on the south side of Baker Lake Basin viz., the Bisset Lake-Thirty Mile Lake area. The Kazan Formation here is in fault contact with the basement. No significant mineralization was found in the basement, except for some weakly radioactive boulders. Prospecting extended to the north in the Pita grid area north of Bisset Lake, where two new showings, the KR81-37 and DP-14 were discovered in the Kazan Formation. They are similar to other 'Kazan type' occurrences.
