Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Aquitaine Company of Canada carried out radiometric survey in 1969 at 200 m line spacing, and found several anomalies that led them to stake the adjacent groups of PRO and BRO claims. In 1970 the company carried out more detailed airborne survey at line spacing of about 700 ft or 213 m (Fraser, 1970, AR 060383), prospecting, geological mapping and diamond drilling. The drilling was done on the Main showing (PRO 10-11 claims) and the Faucon showing (PRO 28-29 claims) about 3 km to the northeast, and also their favourable stratigraphic zones to the west. In all 8170 m (26800 ft) were drilled in 37 holes. First nine holes were drilled on the Faucon showing, and the remainder holes were drilled on the Main showing. They revealed some mineralized intersections that are rather short and of grade too low to be of economic interest. Knox (1980, p.110) mentioned drill indicated 7 million tonnes of mineralized rock grading 0.08% U3O8, but did not give the source of these figures. Three other showings named Showings A, B and C were also discovered but were not drilled. In 1972 the company carried out a program of soil and lake sediment geochemistry and geological mapping. In 1977 further exploration was carried out by Cominco Limited in joint venture with the property owners Aquitaine Company and Siebens Oil and Gas Company. The work involved geological mapping of the PRO and BRO claims plus BRY, BRI, BRU and ADD claims to the west-southwest, on scale 1 : 1000. In addition detailed mapping and radiometric survey was done on the showings on scale 1 : 1000 and 500. Core of the holes drilled in 1970 was relogged. Showings A and B, which were not drilled before, were tested with 3 and 5 holes, respectively, totalling 455 m in 8 holes using JKS-300 and X-Ray drills. Showings C, D and E were remapped but no drilling was done on them. Mapping revealed the 'Main East' showing which is a faulted extension of the Main showing. The earlier known Faucon showing was remapped, core of the 9 holes drilled in 1970 was relogged, and one new hole was drilled on it to check its eastern extension. Uranium North Resources Corporation acquired the property in 2007, and conducted drill programs during 2008 and 2009 to confirm and extend the historic resources of the Main zone. The company reported NI 43-101 compliant resources of the Main Zone in August 2009, incorporating the previously confidential historic drill data submitted by Acquitaine Company of Canada Limited in 1971 to the Atomic Energy Control Board, and provided by the Geological Survey of Canada on the request by Uranium North. The resource figures released in News Release 09-13 on August 6, 2009 are based on cut-off grades ranging from 0.01 to 0.06 % U3O8. At the highest cut-off grade of 0.06 % U3O8 the tonnage is 3.062 million tons averaging 0.108 % U3O8, which amount to the resources of 7.288 million lbs of U3O8 viz., 2.778 million tonnes @ 0.051 % U totalling Inferred Resources of 2803 tonnes U. Detailed resource calculations were published in a NI 43-101 compliant report on the SEDAR website (Campbell et al., 2009). (Armitage and Sexton, 2012) have increased this estimate based on additional 2011 drilling.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization is in silty arkose lenses interbedded with rhythmically laminated siltstone. Individual lenses are up to 1.8 m thick and 25 m long (Blackwell, 1978). No carbonate marker unit was found. The beds strike 060° and dip to the south. Lineations plunge 5 to 30° to the west. The beds are intensely deformed, and minor recumbent folds are common. They are affected by two well defined reverse faults and five other suspected ones. Correlation of beds is difficult near the mineralized zone but seems simpler to the west. Uranium minerals, mainly uraninite with subordinate brannerite and uranophane, occur as finely disseminated grains less than 1 mm in diameter but some are as much as 2 mm long on an edge. Uraninite also forms 'concretions' up to 2 cm in diameter and concentrically layered (also seen in Main East, Faucon, and A and B showings). Brannerite is identified in polished section one sample, and it is not certain how widespread it is. Secondary uranium minerals are scarce. Mineralized arkoses range from white to brick red but are commonly greyish pink. Red colouration appears to be due to oxidation of magnetite. There is however no one to one correlation of magnetite and uranium minerals. The reddish colouration is not a reliable guide to mineralization.
