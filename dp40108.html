Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Faucon showing was tested with 9 drill holes in 1970. Several radioactive horizons were intersected, with best results in two holes 200 m apart on east and west sides of the surface exposures at the shore of Main Lake of the property. They assayed 0.13 % U3O8 over 2.4 and 0.102 % U3O8 over 2.5 m in holes AML-1 and AML-4, respectively. The host unit is a siltstone interbedded with arkose. It trends 090° to 110° and dips 20 to 40° to the south. Reinterpretation of the drill core during 1977 showed the mineralized horizon is down dropped by a fault trending 060°. Brecciation is common along the fault. Surface exposure of mineralization is on the down dropped block. One hole drilled in 1977 (# 77-6) about 45 m NNE of AML-1, intersected 0.165 % U3O8 over 0.8 m at 30 m, and correlates with the two high grade intersections mentioned above. This mineralized zone is apparently displaced to the northwest of the AML-4 by a NNW-trending fault in an area of thick overburden. Earlier faults that affected the strata are low angle south-dipping reverse faults formed on overturned limbs of recumbent faults, with apparent displacements of several hundred metres. Also present are parallel low angle normal faults (lag faults related to thrusting) and numerous bedding plane faults. Another set is of a high angle (30 to 70°) north dipping reverse faults. The youngest NNW-striking faults are vertical and have sinistral displacement up to several kilometres. They also appear to have a normal east side up component.
