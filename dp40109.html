Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Conglomerates of the Montgomery Lake Group in DEN claims were originally prospected for gold by Selco Exploration Company in 1961. The company drilled 21 AX holes of 200 to 300 feet length and 8 X-ray holes totalling 335 feet. Where the conglomerates have been drilled for gold, they are thin and poorly packed with pebbles up to 1 cm. Gold assays up to 3 g/t over 0.6 m have been obtained and radioactivity is low. Original claims were allowed to lapse. Recognition of their basal Paleoproterozoic position (Eade, 1964) stimulated interest in their uranium potential. The DEN claims were restaked in 1969. During 1969-'70 Denison Mines Limited conducted airborne and ground scintillometer, EM, and soil radon surveys. These showed anomalies that are related to known pyritic conglomerate horizons. The company drilled 6 holes totalling 2102 feet (640.7 m) into the base of the Montgomery Lake sequence. The holes intersected 3 main beds of slightly radioactive, pyritic quartz pebble conglomerates, one of which contained 0.017 % U3O8 over 12 feet (3.7 m). Better grade sections ranged up to 0.02 % U and 0.03 % Th over 0.6 m. The conglomerates examined to date have not been considered to be of economic interest.
