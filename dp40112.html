Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1978, Taiga Consultants Limited staked a number of claims and acquired Permit 523 to explore for Unconformity-associated deposits beneath a remnant of Early Mesoproterozoic Ellice Formation at the southeast end of the Bathurst Inlet Fault Zone. The siliciclastic formation is preserved here in a long narrow graben trending north-northwest. The claims were optioned to Giant Yellowknife Mines Limited, who explored them in 1979-'80, and staked additional claims nearby. An airborne radiometric survey carried out in 1979 was followed up in 1980 by prospecting, mapping, VLF-EM and radon surveys. Four conductors were located at the eastern boundary of the Ellis Formation. Some radon anomalies were reported. During 1981 Eldorado Nuclear Limited carried out surface evaluation of the property that included VLF-EM and HLEM surveys along 4 lines and mapping. A search for paleoregolith did not reveal any evidence for it. Spectrometer (GAD-6) readings on weakly radioactive sandstone and conglomerate by S.S. Gandhi of Geological Survey of Canada in 1981 showed high Th/U ratio. Scintillometer work by him revealed up to 6 times the background radioactivity in biotite gneisses of the basement to the east.
