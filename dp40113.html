Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Surface exploration during 1997 in an area of mineralized boulders led to a mineralized outcrop with high Au and Co values; the locally derived boulders contain notable amounts of U, Mo, W, Ni and As. The DON 1-3 and AL 1-2 claims were staked at the end of June, 1997, and were registered in the name of Stephen Masson of Flin Flon, Manitoba. Athlone Resources Limited optioned the claims with an agreement to earn 100 % interest in them subject to a royalty interest. The claims adjoin the JAN 1 claim held at that time by Claude Resources Inc., the AL 1-2 claims on southwest side of it and the DON 1-3 claims on the northeast side. They were previously held by Hemlo Gold Mines Inc. (Noranda), who carried out surface examination that located in claim AL 2 a 20 m long outcrop of layered calc-silicate grading at its north end into a sulphide-rich calc-silicate zone named the Raven showing/zone (Dudek, 1994; Sanguinetti, 1998, p. 6). The DON 1-3 claims are in the area of JAN 2 (F13099) claim, which was staked in 1986 on the northeast side of JAN 1 (F13102) by Claude Resources Inc., and explored along with JAN 1during late 1980s (Young, 1989). In 1997 Athlone Resources Limited conducted surface exploration in the DON and AL claims, including trenching of the Raven zone in AL 2 claim (Sanguinetti, 1998). These claims lapsed in 2003.

(Applies to: mineralization) The Raven showing and the mineralized boulders in its vicinity are essentially similar to the mineralized boulders found in the area about 1.5 km to the ENE in the LES -1 claim. The showing is the only significant evidence to date of mineralization in bedrock in this area. It does not have the whole suite of minerals and metals in the boulders, and thus represents only a part of the complex picture of mineralization. The gossanous boulders in its vicinity are described as lithologically and mineralogically similar, enhancing the interpretation that the boulders are derived from local source(s)). The best assay for gold encountered during the 1997 work was 6.9 g/t in a sample from a small calc-silicate gossan boulder located 100 m west of the showing (Sanguinetti, 1998). Weak radioactivity was encountered in another gossan boulder at the north end of the showing. The showing is located at the southwest end of a northeast-trending regional airborne magnetic high through the LES-1 claim (Charbonneau and Swettenham, 1986). It parallels the regional structural trend of the metasedimentary rocks, which contain magnetite and local concentrations of pyrrhotite. Glacially transported mineralized boulders are also concentrated in that direction.

(Applies to: regional geology) The region straddling Nunavut-Manitoba border near Saskatchewan was mapped by Eade (1971, 1973), Weber et al. (1975) and Schledewitz (1980). They regarded metasedimentary strata with calc-silicates in the belt trending southwest from Sandybeach Lake as part of the Paleoproterozoic Hurwitz Group. The unit that hosts mineralization at Sandybeach Lake is close to the Archean paragneiss but Eade (ibid) noted it as a subunit possibly equivalent of the Hurwitz Group. The same unit was regarded as part of the Wollaston Group by Dudek (1994), and his nomenclature was followed by Sanguinetti (1998). Later work by Aspler and Chiarenzelli (1997) and Aspler et al. (2001) indicate that the unit is probably a part of the intracratonic Upper Hurwitz Group deposited ca. 1.91-1.86 Ga. The platform-shelf sequence of the Wollaston Supergroup was deposited at about the same time. Boundary between the two groups is well defined in Saskatchewan (Anonymous, 2003) but is obscure in Manitoba and Nunavut. Both groups were deformed during the Trans-Hudson Orogeny and intruded by the Hudson Granitoid Suite ca. 1.85 Ga (van Breemen et al., 2005). Because of the deformation and metamorphism, and scarcity of outcrops, it is difficult to sort out strata of the two groups and boundary between them. Lithological characters however suggest that the host unit in Sandybeach Lake area and similar mineralized strata extending for some 100 km southwest in Manitoba, are comparable with the Ameto and/or Watterson Formation of the Upper Hurwitz Group that were deposited below the wave-base in intracratonic basin (Aspler et al., 2001).

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) The Raven showing is the only significant mineralized bedrock in large area of numerous mineralized boulders at Sandybeach Lake, including the high grade Darcy's cluster 1.6 km to the N65°E in LES-1 claim.
