Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Airborne radiometric survey conducted by Uranerz Exploration and Mining Limited in 1976 revealed anomalies that were staked as ERZ claims. In 1977 prospecting in the area revealed numerous radioactive areas which were staked as BON, NIP, GRG, JNS and JUD claims. In 1978 ground magnetic and VLF surveys were carried out on the claims. Magnetic survey was useful in detailing structure. The VLF survey did not show any anomalies. Further prospecting led to numerous radioactive boulders and some bedrock mineralization in Grid 1 in BON claim group, which was spotty, and in Grid 2 two radioactive boulder trains and bedrock mineralization in the SHA and PYT claims at the east end of the company's property. At the SHA claims a narrow fan or train of 500 boulders reading between 10000 and 15000 cps on SPP-2 scintillometer trends west-northwest over a length of 3 km. Assays of 52 boulder samples averaged 0.53 % U3O8. The boulder fan (or train) in the PYT claims 2 km to the north trends parallel to it. The source of the SHA claim fan was determined as local at the south end of Horned Lake by a glacial study. The target was tested by two drill holes, and mineralization was intersected in them at depths of about 20 m. The grades were however low.

(Applies to: mineralization) Two types of mineralization are observed in the Uranerz claims (BON-ERZ-JNS-SHA-PYT): 1) pitchblende and secondary uranium minerals in reducing horizon of phyllitic metapelites, and 2) uraninite associated with magnetite in interbedded mica schists. The first type is found mainly in outcrops, and commonly shows counts of 2000 to 15000 on SPP-2 scintillometer. The second type is seen mainly in boulders that show 1500 -2000 cps. A petrographic study of selected samples showed that meta-arkoses and metapelites contain uranium-enrichment in biotitic and pyritic layers (V. Voultsidis, Appendix 2, 5 pages, in Hopfengaertner and Male, 1978). The host strata are granoblastic but weakly schistose. Some rocks exhibit two foliations; S1 is layer parallel and S2 is at low angle to it. The grain size is between 0.1 and 0.5 mm. The metamorphic grade is transitional between greenschist and amphibolite facies. The textures indicate that uranium concentration is premetamorphic. Where biotite-rich layers alternate with sericite-rich layers, uranium is concentrated exclusively in the biotite-rich layers. Uranium occurs as spherulitic pitchblende of maximum 0.01 mm diameter. The spherules lacks zoning and shrinkage cracks. Coffinite and sooty pitchblende are absent. These features are consistent with the metamorphosed character of uranium mineralization, which is probably synsedimentary. Accompanying sulphides are pyrite, chalcopyrite and galena.
