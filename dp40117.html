Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Main East showing was discovered in 1977, within a fault block 700 x 900 m located about 1.5 km east of the Main Showing. It is hosted by one of the siltstone horizons (lowermost of 4 horizons) exposed on the southwest side of the Extension Lake. The host unit is 1 to 2 m thick laminar siltstone. It contains concretions of uraninite (?) up to 1 cm in diameter, which commonly make up about 1 % of the rock and locally as much as 5 %. Uranium also occurs in thin silty arkosic lenses, with or without magnetite. Near the Extension Lake shore the mineralized arkose lenses are numerous, and some of them are deformed into wispy lamellae. The beds dip 5 to 50° to the southwest, averaging about 25°.

(Applies to: property geology) Faulted extension of the host horizon of the Main showing located about 1.5 km to the west; continuity of mineralization over that length however appears unlikely
