Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Airborne radiometric survey conducted by Uranerz Exploration and Mining Limited in 1976 revealed anomalies that were staked as ERZ claims. In 1977 prospecting in the area revealed numerous radioactive areas which were staked as BON, NIP, GRG, JNS and JUD claims. In 1978 ground magnetic and VLF surveys were carried out on the claims. Magnetic survey was useful in detailing structure. The VLF survey did not show any anomalies. Further prospecting led to numerous radioactive boulders and some bedrock mineralization in Grid 1 in BON claim group, which was spotty, and in Grid 2 two radioactive boulder trains and some bedrock mineralization in the SHA and PYT claims at the east end of the company's property. Both boulder trains trend northwest, and are 2 km apart and about 3 km long. The northern one is in the PYT claims. Some samples of its boulders and outcrops contain uranium in the range of 0.25 to 0.60 % U3O8. One of the outcrop was trenched in 1978. The trench is located about one third of the way northwest from the southeast end of the boulder train and is 23 x 1 x 1 m in size. Radioactivity was recorded and samples were taken at 2 m interval along its length, which is across the easterly trend of the bedrock. The highest uranium values were found in biotite-rich, finely banded quartzite within 2 m of its boundary with dark grey phyllitic metapelite on the north side. The radioactive zone is about 0.5 m wide with assays up to 7500 ppm U3O8 (Reid and Walkow, 1978).
