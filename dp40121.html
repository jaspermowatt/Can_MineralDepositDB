Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Showing 76-3 is located close to the centre of NTS 66 H 5 at the southwestern corner of a 4 km long north-south lake. It was discovered by Western Mines Limited in 1976. It occurs in the Showing Lake Formation, which also hosts the Westmin Showings 76-4, 76-5, 77-2 and 77-3 along 12 km long the keel zone of a tight syncline trending west-southwest to the margin of the toposheet (Young, 1979). On the other side Westmin Showing 77-1 is in the same unit and is located 3 km north-northeast of 76-3, near a brittle northwest-trending fault with a left-lateral displacement of about 2 km. Radioactivity at Showings 76-3, 76-4 and 75-5 occurs in siltstone beds that are interbedded with arkosic sandstones. Showing 76-3 is on the south side of the synclinal axis and the bedding dips 80° to the south indicating the beds are overturned. Outcrop is a small one on shore of a lake in area of scarce outcrops. Continuous chip samples over a length of 2.44 m across the siltstone bedding gave chemical assays averaging 0.09 lb U3O8 /short ton or 0.038 % U (Schildler, 1977, p. 25). The mineralization is finely disseminated.
