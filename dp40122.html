Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Discovery of the showing was made in 1977 by Western Mines Ltd. (Westmin) in an area of outcrops of Showing Lake Formation about 1 km sq between two lakes. The formation comprises interbedded siltstone and arkosic sandstone. Uranium occurs mainly in sandstone beds that are reddish in colour, and to a lesser extent in siltstone beds. They sandstone beds are relatively thicker than the siltstone beds and contain relatively little magnetite. The bedding in the area strikes easterly and dips 40 to 75° to the south. The mineralized zones are in the basal part of the formation that overlies the Oora Lake Formation of cross-bedded feldspathic quartzite. The mineralized zones are low in grade. These and other formations of the Amer Group are moderately tightly folded on kilometre scale. They are displaced about a kilometre by a left lateral brittle fault, which trends northwest and is located just southwest of the outcrop area. The fault also displaces an earlier brittle fault that trends north-northwest and displacement on it is right-lateral for a distance of few hundred metres.
