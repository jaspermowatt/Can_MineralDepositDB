Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Discovery of the Showing 76-9 resulted from follow-up of reconnaissance geology and geochemistry in 1976. Detailed radiometric survey of showing was done on 1 sq mile area around it. A high grade boulder was located and it assayed 8.48 % U3O8 (Nutter, 1979). A larger grid was established on upper units of Amer Group in an area measuring about 16 sq miles in NTS 66 H 6. The work in the grid included stream and lake water and sediment surveys, soil geochemistry, prospecting, packsack drilling, underwater scintillometry, VLF EM and Proton Mag surveys. Discovery of a possible Thelon outlier in the NW corner of 66 H 6 and numerous red hematized and silicified boulders (possibly regolithic) suggest proximity to the sub-Thelon unconformity. The high-grade boulder and other radioactive boulders are in a triangular block of Ayagaq Lake Formation; however outcrops of Showing Lake Formation occur 5 km to the southeast (source direction of glacially dispersed boulders).

(Applies to: mineralization) The high grade boulder is fractured siliceous siltstone weighing about 10 pounds. It was found during the course of reconnaissance prospecting, A 5 lb sample of it yielded 8.48 % U3O8 by chemical assay. Chemical analysis showed 66.8 % SiO2 and 2.63 % K2O. The boulder also contains an estimated 1 % chalcopyrite and 3 to 5 % pyrrhotite, which accounts for its rusty weathered surface. Fracture planes within the boulder contain yellow uranium oxides. A positive print of an autoradiograph on one half of the boulder clearly shows that uranium has been leached from the weathered rind of the boulder (Schindler, 1977, p. 28-29). Thin section shows a quartz vein, which is fractured indicating cataclastic deformation. Detrital quartz grains also show fracturing due to radiation damage. Crude banding in the rock is apparently relict bedding. Uranium is generally disseminated through the rock, and follows the relict bedding viz., uranium appears preferentially concentrated along compositionally favourable beds. Introduction of uranium after the sedimentation is indicated by micro solution fronts (caries textures) where concentration of uranium is high. Electron microprobe work at Dalhousie University, Halifax, indicated that pitchblende occurs in grains 2 to 3 microns in diameter, and they contain equivalent of 84.11 % U3O8 and 11.15 % PbO. Thirty element semi quantitative spectrographic analyses of samples from the boulder by two independent laboratories showed anomalous contents of Ba, Bi, Co and V, and high contents of Sn (300 ppm), and variable amounts of Pb (0.7 to 10 %). Angularity of the boulder suggests that it is close to the source to the south east within the area underlain by the Amer Group.
