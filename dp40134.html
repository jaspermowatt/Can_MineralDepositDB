Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Radioactive boulder field of sandstone-siltstone, with assays up to 33.1 lb U3O8/ton (1.7 % U3O8), and an average of 1.6 lb U3O8/ton, was discovered in 1979 at an airborne radiometric anomaly by Urangesellschaft Canada Ltd. A grid measuring 100x100 m was established for detailed mapping, magnetic survey and quaternary geology study. The surface work pointed to local bedrock source, which was confirmed by drilling in 1979 of 8 holes totalling 3032 feet (925 m) that intersected stratiform mineralized lenses. The drilling defined a small low grade mineralized zone. The textures in the mineralized rocks indicate that pitchblende disseminations and aggregates predate deformation and metamorphism of the host beds. Samples of lake edge water and sediment were collected at Uranium Pond prior to the drilling of the proposed holes as prelude to the regional geochemical survey in 1980. Analyses of 15 lake water samples showed a range of 0.02 to 1.9 ppb U and 14 lake sediment samples ranged from 0.05 to 12.3 ppm U.
