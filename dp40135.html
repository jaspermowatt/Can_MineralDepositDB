Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) IFO (Urangesellschaft) exploration (AR 81731): During 1982 two significant radioactive boulder trains were discovered. High grade mineralization in some boulders show remobilization in fracture. Exotic mineral assemblages and a pitchblende age determination of 1270 Ma indicates episode of uranium remobilization and redeposition. Follow-up work in 1983 concluded that the pitchblende nuggets were deposited in Roegen moraine, and implies that the source is up-ice trend of 140 degrees. Further prospecting in new discoveries of pitchblende nuggets within the bounds of the original boulder train and on the east side of Ifo Lake thus expanding the width of the mineralized train. Graphitic black shale and mudstone occur up-ice in the 80-1EX grid. Glacial studies of distinctive indicate that the source is northwest of the shale within the grid area, and radioactive boulders are not found on the other side of it. The prominent glacial ice direction is revealed by several easily distinguishable boulders e.g., those with dense accumulations of pyritic/graphitic material. Trains of such boulders are traceable directly back to magnetic highs which flank the main EM conductor. In areas of moraine north of the 80-1EX grid, pitchblende nuggets and the pyritic/graphitic boulders co-exist, which suggests the source of the nuggets is related to an EM conductor defined along a bay on the east side of the lake, although some scepticism remained as to the source of the nuggets and their relationship to the anomaly. In 1988 additional radiometric prospecting was undertaken 'up-ice' of the geophysical target. No significant radioactivity was found in the up-ice boulders. On the down-ice side of the conductor there was an evidence of overlying tills of graphitic material related to a dolomite horizon. This close association of the two lithologies is supported by the detailed boulder mapping. Some of the pitchblende nuggets found earlier had remnant coatings of a dolomitic host rock, which also suggested that the dolomitic horizon was closely related to the graphite.
