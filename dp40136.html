Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Pete Grid is mostly in SAN 24 claim and extend a short distance north into SAM 25 claim. Detailed prospecting in 1983 defined four separate narrow radioactive boulder trains trending northwest in the area of bouldery Roegen moraine. Geological mapping defined white orthoquartzite (Ayagaq Formation) in contact with black slate in the west central part of the grid, and feldspathic quartzite and siltstone in the eastern part of the grid. EM anomalies suggest that the black shale is graphitic. The mineralized boulders are feldspathic quartzite. Further detailed studies in 1984 showed that the western trains are derived from feldspathic quartzite whereas the easternmost train is derived from siltstone. This was interpreted to be due some structural control of the mineralization rather than a stratigraphic control along a favourable horizon, or possibly a combination of the two. Trace element analyses show that anomalous uranium values are associated with elevated contents of Cu, Ba, Ag and Pb. Magnetic and VLF-EM anomalies that trend northwards then turn sharply east ward. The anomalies are open to south and east.
