Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The Pete Grid South is the southern extension of the Pete Grid in SAN 24 claim, which lies just north of Binky 1 claim viz., on the north side of latitude 65° 15' N (in NTS 66 G 8). Radioactive boulders, including some high grade ones, were discovered in SAN 24 claim during surface exploration in 1983 within the Pete Grid. Prior to that the Showing 76-4 in east central part of the grid was found during 1976 by Westmin Resources Ltd. The southern extension of the grid covers much of Binky 1 claim and extends a few hundred metres south into Binky 2 claim. Its baseline trends 015°, and is located in the western part of Binky 1 claim. Radioactive boulders in the grid area were located during 1983, and comprised siltstone and arkosic quartzite. A detailed study of surficial geology in 1989 showed that the terrain is distinctly less rugged than that in the Pete Grid area to the north, except for the resistant orthoquartzite in the western quarter. No significant new radioactive boulders or outcrop were encountered that were not found in earlier work. It was noted however that much of the overburden in the north-western part of the grid comprises hematized and graphitic schist and slate intermixed with orthoquartzite rubble. This confirms the bedrock source of the graphitic schist. An airborne anomaly trending northeast was detected at the site during earlier work. Additional ground work during 1989 included VLF-EM, HLEM, magnetic, resistivity and radiometric surveys. These indicated that the beds of shale, siltstone and arkose are closely associated, and are folded moderately tightly. The rubble and boulders of the schistose rock are only marginally more radioactive than the background for the region. Two other parallel VLF conductors, one about 1.5 km to the northwest and the other about 0.8 km to the southeast, may represent repetition of the same graphitic unit due to folding. The middle one, which extends for 2.5 km from the Pete South Grid in Binky 1 claim to the Pete Grid in the SAN 24 claim, is noteworthy in that some high grade radioactive boulders found in the SAN 24 claim may represent later enrichment of the lower grade synsedimentary mineralization at the sub-Thelon Formation unconformity. Showing 76-4 is described by Knox (1980, p. 119) as a radioactive horizon at the margin of an outcrop 300 x 200 m at the north end of 3 km long lake. Immediately north of the outcrop there are abundant large angular blocks of highly calcareous feldspathic sandstone. The beds dip 78° S and strike 086°. Thinner interbeds of siltstone increase in abundance up section. Rhythmic bedding is characteristic of the southern half of the outcrop. A 1 m thick lamprophyre dyke cuts the strata. The radioactive occurrence consists of a red fine-grained sandstone bed 1 to 2 m thick. Grab samples from the zone assayed as high as 0.162 % U3O8. Mapping by Young (1979) defined the host unit as Showing Lake Formation tidalite. The showing outcrop is on the north side of a small syncline plunging gently northeast.
