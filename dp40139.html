Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The KB-5 claim adjoins the KB-8 claim to the northeast where radioactive boulder train of Deep Six Grid is located. The southwest end of the grid is in the northeastern triangular area of KB-5 claim. Glacial mapping in 1982 in this region southeast of Ifo Lake revealed that radioactive feldspathic quartzite boulders in the region were deposited in an outwash terraces part of a later stage of glaciofluvial system (Wiese, 1983). Air photo interpretation of the system showed that it extended farther south where it becomes very wide. Detailed prospecting of sands and gravels in the southern parts of the system led to the discovery of two more highly radioactive feldspathic quartzite boulders (up to 26000 cps) further south beyond the southernmost radioactive boulder found in 1982. This encouraged further prospecting on margins of the glaciofluvial system, which led to two mineralized feldspathic quartzite boulder trains in till, namely the 'Annex' of the Deep Six Grid in KB-5 claim (also referred to as Area A), and the 'RB Hill' in the RB Hill Grid that covers southern part of SAN 45 claim and adjacent Diana 5 claim to the east (also referred to as Area C). A corner of it extends into KB 5 claim (small area referred to as Area B). The two boulder trains trend northwest and are about 3 km apart, hence are believed to have separate sources. During July 1988 a 10-day programme of further study of Areas A, B and C, and also of Area D to the north (northeast shore area of Ifo Lake) was undertaken. It was conducted with helicopter from Urangesellschaft camp at the Lone Gull/Kiggavik deposit about 100 km to the southeast (Wiese, 1988). The Areas A and B are about 0.75x0.5 km in area, and are 1.5 km apart within the claim KB-5. The boulder study indicated the northeast trending boundary between siltstone-sandstone and feldspathic quartzite strata, which was later confirmed by bedrock exposures discovered. Radioactivity is in the siltstone-sandstone boulders down-ice from the lithological boundary with the feldspathic quartzite. It is in the order of total 800 cps (on URTEC Model 135 detector). These readings are considered indicative of the synsedimentary mineralization in the host Showing Lake Formation as found at several other occurrences in the Amer fold belt.
