Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Located at the north end Ifo Lake, which is about 5.5 km long in N-S direction and about 2 km wide. Radioactive boulder field of the large IFO Grid, but located on the west side of Ifo Lake and probably has a different source than other boulders in the main part of the field about 5 km to the east. Discovered in 1979 (Marlatt and Ernsting, 1981). A boulder of interbedded siltstone and arkose yielded assay of 30 lbs/t of U3O8.

(Applies to: regional geology) The radioactive boulders siltstone and arkosic quartzite occur in an area of the moderately tightly folded strata of the lower and middle parts of the Amer Group trending northeast. The boulders are lithologically comparable to the Oora Lake and Showing Lake formations. A few high grade radioactive boulders may represent remobilization of uranium from the lower grade synsedimentary mineralization. Bedrock exposures are few in the area covered by largely by the glacial drift. EM anomalies in the area indicate the presence of graphitic shales found as small boulders in the area.
