Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The showing was discovered in 1979 on a small rubbly outcrop of interbedded grey siltstone and pink arkose. Anomalously radioactive angular boulders of similar lithologies were found several hundred meters to the northwest (in the direction of latest ice movement) north of a water pond. Radioactivity in the outcrop is rather uniform in the range of 1000-2000 cps, and in the boulders is similar to slightly higher. Assays on 3 samples assayed 0.08, 0.11 and o.29 % U3O8 equivalent (Marlatt, 1981).

(Applies to: property geology) The mineralized zone in the bedrock exposure is traceable for 20 m and has a width of 1 to 2 m, and dominated by arkosic quartzite. A northerly trending fault scarp runs adjacent to the outcrop. Sedimentary slump feature on centimetre scale is observed in the outcrop. Pink liesegang banding on scale of half a meter is oriented across the bedding and resembles bedding plane. The beds in the area commonly dip 30 to 40° to the north-northwest. Minor folds plunge 40° to the southwest. Slaty argillaceous beds occur to the north and south of the showing area. The mineralized beds resemble those in the Showing Lake Formation.
