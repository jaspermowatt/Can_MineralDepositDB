Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) A 15 km long strong conductor zone was detected by airborne geophysical survey during 1978 in the Amer fold belt just east of the boundary of overlying Thelon formation. Ground work in 1979 located exposures of graphitic beds and fragments derived from them in glacial drift at the southeast corner of SAN 13 claim. A local grid, namely Graphite Grid, was cut over a 1.2 km section of the conductor that trends 015°, the direction which is one of a few local deviations in the regional northeasterly trend. The deviations mark S-shaped jogs in the strike of the conductor. Exploration on the Graphite Grid included geological mapping, and geochemical and ground geophysical surveys. The local deviation in regional conductor trend in the grid coincides with a fault zone. Uranium values of 7.0 and 6.7 ppm were found in drainage sediments in the vicinity of conductor on its sides. Outcrops of orthoquartzite of Ayagaq Formation occur on east of the grid. Attitude of its beds is variable. Stratigraphy of the Amer Group in this area is not well known. The graphitic zone is adjacent to the Ayagaq Formation, and is regarded by some workers as stratigraphically below the formation. It may however be part of a younger argillaceous unit e.g., the Resort lake Formation, in which case the juxtaposition may be due to structural complexity. Boundary of Thelon Formation is located about a kilometre to the west and is approximately parallel to the conductor trend. A combination of graphitic zone in the Paleoproterozoic metasedimentary basement, a fault along the zone and the exhumed cover strata of continental siliciclastic Thelon Formation, corresponds to the setting of the classic Key Lake deposits in the Athabasca basin in northern Saskatchewan. Discovery of high grade pitchblende 'nuggets' in glacial drift in the Ifo Lake area northeast of Graphite Grid is encouraging in this regard. A semi regional study of till along with prospecting and geochemical survey was carried out in 1983, which included the Graphite Grid area. The results however were not encouraging (Wiese, 1983).
