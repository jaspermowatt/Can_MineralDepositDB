Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1979 Westmin Resources Ltd. commissioned an airborne radiometric survey over southwestern part of the company's extensive properties. This survey over the area of NTS Sheets 66 G 1,2; B 13, 14, 15, 16 and C 1, was carried out by Kenting Earth Sciences Ltd. Regional geochemical surveys (shallow water and lake bottom) along with geological mapping and prospecting, were conducted by Westmin staff from 1976 to 1979. Follow-up surface exploration in 1980 included Kenting airborne anomaly R 75 in what the company referred to as 'Area 4 of Interest'. An extensive radioactive boulder field was discovered in this overburden-covered area, with the main concentration in a fan-like area along the main trend of glacial dispersion in direction 325° (Brown, 1981, and Appendix G therein by L. Bayrock). The boulders are of the Thelon Formation, mainly of highly fractured and altered, yellow-orange, partly granular conglomeratic sandstone with anomalously high radioactivity vs. size ratio. A suite of 17 of these boulders assayed 2 lbs per ton uranium, and one sample assayed 22 lbs per ton. The higher grade mineralization appears to be associated with reduced (bleached) sandstone boulders. The possibility that these boulders may be associated with Unconformity-associated uranium mineralization at the base of the Thelon Formation was tested in 1980 by two drill holes, H-80-4-01 and -02, located at the potential bedrock source of the boulders. The first one is a vertical hole that intersected 280 m of Thelon sandstone. The second hole drilled at -45° intersected several breccia zones. A subsequent petrographic study of samples of the boulders and drill core revealed the presence of phosphate-cemented sandstones and probable sedimentary phosphatic breccias (Brown, 1981; Young, 1980; note NTS # C16 should be B16 in the title of report by Young). Radioactivity is associated with apatite, which occurs commonly as soft pinkish mineral in matrix of breccias and phosphatic sandstone.
