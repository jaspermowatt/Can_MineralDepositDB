Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Westmin Resources Ltd. commissioned two airborne radiometric surveys over the company's extensive properties; first one in 1978 by Geoterrex Ltd. over the northeast part of the property (NTS Sheets 66 H 4, 5, 6, 7, 10, 11, 12; G 1, 2 and 8), and the second one in 1979 by Kenting Earth Sciences Ltd. over the remainder of the property (NTS Sheets 66 G 1,2; B 13, 14, 15, 16 and C 16). Regional geochemical surveys (shallow water and lake bottom) along with geological mapping and prospecting, were conducted by Westmin staff from 1976 to 1980. As a follow-up work in 1981 six anomalies were selected, namely R 46A, R 23, R 75, R 22 and B 13B. The R 46A anomaly was located by the Kenting airborne survey. A large boulder fan of mineralized feldspathic sandstone of the Oora Lake Formation was discovered in 1981 on a drumlin approximately 600 m southeast of the airborne anomaly. Additional radioactive boulders were also found to the north of the fan. Of the 252 radioactive boulders found more than 25 % were highly anomalous, and analyses of 32 samples representing them showed average content of nearly 13 lbs/ton uranium. In addition a suite of six small samples from frost boils near potential bedrock source averaged 99 lbs/ton uranium (Evoy and Volz, 1982, p. 20). One relatively larger boulder of the Oora Lake quartzite assayed 58 lbs/ton (2.6 % U3O8) and contained notable values of Ag, As, Ba, Co, Mo and Ni. A glacial study of the boulder fan by L. Bayrock in 1981 indicated the location of potential bedrock source. During 1982 various geochemical and geophysical surveys were conducted over the grids covering the area of radioactive boulders.
