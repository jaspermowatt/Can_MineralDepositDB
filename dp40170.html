Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Reconnaissance surveys were carried out in 1977 by Gulf Minerals Canada Ltd., in permits 450 to 454. The work included airborne radiometric-mag-VLF-EM survey, lake water and lake sediment surveys, and geological mapping. More detailed follow-up work resulted in discovery of the Bear Mountain, Ron and John showings. In 1978 prospecting led to the discovery of the showing located approximately 8 km south of the northern boundary and 2 km west of the eastern boundary of 86M/1. The company established Grid 1 over this area and conducted mapping, prospecting, ground radiometrics, EM-16 and IP surveys. Soil sampling was performed on a 1,000-foot square grid pattern resulting in assays up to 64 ppm U. In addition the helium content of the soil was tested. - During 1979 Gulf Minerals performed mapping at a scale of 1:5,000 and completed tens of km of IP, EM-16 and ground radiometric surveys over Grid 1. A total of 594 soil samples were taken at 30-metre intervals along 100-metre-spaced lines. Whole rock and specific geochemical analyses were performed on 367 outcrop samples taken on the Bear Mountain grid. The area was tested by 1,955 m of diamond drilling in 15 holes. - As at February 2006, the northern half of 86M/1 is covered by prospecting permits. These were issued on Feb. 1, 2005. The Permits are part of Triex Minerals (50%) and Pitchstone Exploration's (50%) Dismal Lake project.

(Applies to: mineralization) Outcropping uranium mineralization, identified in the field as pitchblende (fine powder along fractures), was initially found to occur within a 3-inch-wide horizon exhibiting malachite staining. This horizon was traced for about 50 feet along strike. The host rock is Unit 11 sandstone. This site was at the top of a knoll just west of a diabase at the western end of Bear Mountain. Further work identified five separate stratigraphic horizons containing uranium mineralization. - Twelve Bear Mountain sandstone samples along this zone averaged 18.8 lbs/ton U3O8, with a high of 35.9 lbs/ton U3O8 (values ranged from 0.06-1.79% U3O8). Another nine boulder samples averaged 24.4 lbs/ton U3O8. - Elevated copper and arsenic values are associated with the uranium. Grab sample E-0153, for example, assayed 14,160 ppm U3O8, 1,600 ppm Cu and 3,650 ppm As (AR 061839).

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) - Drilling intersected Unit 11 fine-grained to medium-to-fine grained mature sandstone with 2-7% pink feldspar and minor black opaque minerals. The drill holes were collared in this unit and it was found to be up to 245 metres thick. Occasional green siltstone beds less than 5 mm thick are interbedded with the sandstone. Unit 10 was found to be pyritic and was interpreted to be the cause of two parallel north-south IP anomalies. Radiometric anomalies recorded in the holes were found to be associated with either diabase or fractures. - One hole cut minor sooty pitchblende along a narrow fracture
