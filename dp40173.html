Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Hudson Bay Oil and Gas Limited conducted an airborne radiometric survey in 1978 over a number of claims that are located on or close to the unconformity between the Paleoproterozoic crystalline basement and Mesoproterozoic continental siliciclastic strata of the Hornby Bay and Dismal Lakes groups. Follow-up ground work included geological mapping, prospecting, geochemical survey, and IP, VLF-EM and magnetic surveys in selected areas. A train of radioactive sandstone boulders located in 1978 is in overburden covered area near BDIGM Lake. The boulders are of super mature sandstone comparable with the Cape MacDowell unit of the Hornby Bay Group exposed in the area, hence interpreted as locally derived. To test this 3 widely spaced holes were drilled along a 3.5 km strike length of the nearly flat-lying sandstone unit. Uranium mineralization was intersected in the third hole at 141.4 m and assayed 8600 ppm U over 5 cm. The mineralized bed resembles the radioactive boulders, and its surface projection is near the boulder train.
