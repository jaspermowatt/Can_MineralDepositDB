Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Radioactivity at FAT claims was detected in 1977 during an airborne survey by Hudson bay Oil and Gas Limited. It occurs in a zone of volcanic rocks along the north side of Fault River that follows the major northeast-trending Fault River Fault. The zone was trenched in 1978. The trenches revealed weak radioactivity in the highly fractured and oxidized Paleoproterozoic volcanic rocks beneath the Mesoproterozoic siliciclastic strata of the Hornby Bay Group exposed immediately to the north. Drilling was done underneath the trenches in 1979, with holes collared on the basal conglomerate of the Hornby bay Group. The drilling failed to intersect any uranium mineralization at the unconformity or in the volcanic rocks below.
