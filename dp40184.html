Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Weakly radioactive quartz-hematite-chlorite-chalcopyrite veins of the Cobalt showing occur in a megacrystic quartz monzonite intrusive of the Hepburn batholith with some dioritic layers. They are located within a 200 x 200 m area. There is also area also one vein of cobaltite-niccolite in the area; it is up to 20 cm thick, and is exposed for 4 m along easterly strike. It dips 70° to the north. A hole was drilled in 1983 at 60° inclination along azimuth 206° intersected, which intersected the vein at a depth of 24 m. This10 cm long drill core intersection assayed 49338 ppm Ni, 15700 ppm Co, 32858 ppm As and 41357 ppm Bi. No radioactivity is associated with it. Another hole drilled in its vicinity to test a VLF-EM anomaly also failed to encounter any radioactivity. The drill holes were short, totalling 92 m.
