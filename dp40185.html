Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The copper showings at McLaren Lake were first discovered during 1930-'31 exploration venture of Dominion Explorers Limited, under the guidance of Thayer Lindsay, which involved a group of geologists and prospectors traversing from Hunter Bay on Great Bear Lake to Coronation Gulf of Arctic Ocean. Some trenching and sampling were done on the showings at that time. The property was restaked as LKS claims in 1968 by Anaconda Petroleum Limited, and was explored that year by trenching, detailed geological mapping, ground magnetic and electromagnetic surveys (Roed, 1968). Six of the eleven important copper showings are concentrated over a strike length of 500 m on the main McLaren Lake Fault, where it meets an easterly trending subsidiary fault on its north side, namely the Fantasia Fault. A total of 122 samples from a number of trenches were assayed, and values up to 3.1 % Cu over 6 feet were reported. Minor silver contents and traces of gold were noted, but no uranium was found. The presence uranium was however established as a result of an airborne geophysical survey done in 1977 by Hudson's Bay Oil and Gas Company Limited. Follow-up surface work in 1978 included geological mapping and prospecting, lake bottom geochemistry and ground EM and IP surveys in the region. Uranium distribution, determined on surface by McPhar TV-1 spectrometer, was found to be spotty at the McLaren Lake showings. Assays of 3 selected grab samples from the main copper-rich zone were between 0.05 and 0.12 % U3O8 (King et al., 1978). The best assay of 0.3 % U3O8 was from a locality about 2 km from this zone in direction 065°, which was discovered by the airborne radiometric survey. It is near the eastern contact of the Mackenzie diabase dyke. It has been suggested that uranium here was possibly mobilized and reconcentrated during intrusion of the dyke.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Numerous copper showings occur along the main and subsidiary brittle faults; the most important ones are in a 500 m strike zone near the east end of McLaren Lake; they are explored by several trenches.
