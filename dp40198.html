Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Understanding of the geological setting and mineralization of the Dominique-Peter orebodies, discovered in 1982 on the east side of Earl River gneiss dome, led to reconnaissance diamond drilling in 1983 to test the favourability of the west side of the dome. The results confirmed the favourability. A percussion drilling program in 1984 discovered the Dominique-Janine North (DJN) deposit , which is hosted by the Peter River gneiss at the boundary of the dome. The deposit trends north and is 160 x 50 m in plan. It occurs near surface on the east side, and reaches a depth of 65 m depth to the west. Reserves for the deposit were first reported in 1987, and were revised in 1989. Overburden stripping for open pit mining was carried out in 1988. Mining commenced in 1990, and by 1991 the deposit was mined out. Adjacent deposit, about 150 m to the south-southwest, namely the Dominique-Janine Extension (DJX) deposit, was mined during 1995-1997.

(Applies to: mineralization) The Dominique-Janine deposit bear many similarities with the larger Dominique-Peter deposit. Both deposits occur at the boundary between the banded Peter River aluminous gneisses at the boundary with the feldspathic Earl River gneissic dome. The boundary zone is variably mylonitized, and includes ultramylonites. Mineralization occurs in the mylonitized zone.
