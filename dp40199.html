Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The Dominique-Janine Extension (or South) deposit was discovered in 1986, following the discovery of the adjacent Dominique-Janine North deposit to the NE in 1984. The DJX deposit is 250 x 20 m in plan and up to 100 m deep; It is close to surface on the east side, but as much 10 m below the surface on the west side. It was mined by open pit during 1994-'97. It produced ~ 1182 t U from 394928 t ore at 0.3 % U.

(Applies to: mineralization) The Dominique-Janine Extension (or South) Zone is a north-trending, 800 m long structural zone that is 40 to 80 m wide, and includes the DJX Open Pit mine at the north end, DJX Underground Mine in the middle, and two fault bounded pods at the southern end, namely the North and South Pods, situated on the northwest shore of Cluff Lake. The pods are at the overturned unconformity between the Athabasca Group sediments and the gneissic basement. Only the South Pod has not been mined. At the north end of the large DJX open pit, mineralization occurs in narrow but rich, vertical N-S veins and stringers in 10 to 30 cm clay gouges located within shear zones 3 to 6 m wide. The shear zones are localized at the contact between the footwall granitoids and the Peter River aluminous gneiss that forms the hanging wall. A set of northwesterly faults offset the main ore zone. In the central portion of the open pit the ore opens into a wide tectonic zone controlled by N-S shear zones. Several well mineralized faults trending N 60 to 140° E impart additional some control on the ore. The mineralization in this area tends to be discontinuous and displays the 'zone à boule' feature as noted in the DJ North Pit. In the south part of the DJX pit the ore zone is narrower, and extends as two well defined N-S westerly dipping shear/tectonic zones mainly in altered aluminous gneiss. This trend is often offset by N60-70°E faults.
