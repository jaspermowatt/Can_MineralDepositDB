Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Early uranium exploration during 1969-'85 was regional in nature, comprising airborne magnetic and EM surveys, regional till, vegetation, lake and stream geochemical surveys, and geological mapping. Bulk of the work was carried out by Marine Oil Company from 1978 to 1981. In 1990, Cogema Resource Inc., Areva Resources Canada Ltd. as of 2006, initiated the Shea Creek Project that included deep-penetrating Geotem magnetic-EM survey. Follow-up ground magnetic, gravimetric and moving loop EM surveys identified a graphitic conductor, named the Saskatoon Lake Conductor trending 325°. By mid-nineties additional work extended the conductor's defined length to 25 km. Drilling began in 1992, and hole SHE-2 intersected favourable alteration and 0.6 % U over 0.7 m in a graphitic shear zone at a depth of at depth of 700 m. Drilling to the north along the conductor led to the discovery of Anne zone nearby in 1994, and of Colette zone in 1996 about 2.5 km to the northwest. The first indications of the Kianna zone, located about 600 m NNW of Anne zone, were obtained in hole SHE-63B drilled in 1997. It was followed by further drilling in 2000. In 2004, Areva Resources Canada Inc. signed a joint venture agreement with UEX Corporation to continue exploration along the Shea Creek trend (Saskatoon Lake conductor). In 2005, the economic significance of the Kianna zone was realized when hole SHE-114-5 intersected 27.4 % U over 8.8 m.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization at Shea Creek occurs in bends in the traces of a conductive graphitic unit and in fault zones discordant to the northwest trend of local lithologies. Displacements appear to strike north-northeast (clockwise of the lithological trends). Mineralization at the Kianna Zone occurs in three styles and settings: fracture-controlled zones in the pre-Athabasca basement, zones of perched pervasive and fracture-controlled mineralization in sandstones above the unconformity, and zones of disseminated, nodular and massive mineralization proximal to the unconformity. The mineralized zones in the sandstone are hosted by the Shea Creek Sequence, the sandstone-dominated, 97 m thick, lowermost unit of the Athabasca Group in the Shea Creek-Douglas River transect. Mineralized pods or lenses may be stacked. The perched and basement hosted lenses display westerly dips. Grades of up to 48% U3O8 have been intersected in drill holes.
