Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Several small showings were discovered in 1978 near the Black Creek, which flows north into Asiak River, during regional mapping by Uranerz Exploration and Mining Ltd. Follow-up exploration in 1979 included ground radiometric and magnetic surveys, detailed mapping and trenching. These showings are in one of claims in a large claim group in the Asiak River area explored by Uranerz in 1970s. This claim group was later acquired by UNOR Inc., which also acquired the Bear Valley properties to the west in 1998 from Hornby Bay Explorations Ltd. The company has planned a major uranium exploration initiative in the region for uranium including the Asiak Claim Group, which has also been explored for diamonds.

(Applies to: mineralization) Polymetallic character of mineralization is indicated by the assays on 12 channel (30x6 cm) samples from the trenches as follows: U3O8 : 0.001 to 0.245 %; other metals in some of these samples: Cu : 0.01 to 0.05 % (7 samples) and one assay of 8.11 %, Pb : 0.12 to 0.20 % (2 samples), Zn : 0.13 to 0.31 % (2 samples), Ni : 0.01 to 0.04 (6 samples), and Co : 0.01 to 0.02 % (6 samples).
