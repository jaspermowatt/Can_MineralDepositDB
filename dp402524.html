(Applies to: regional geology) This occurrence is near the southwestern boundary of the White Bear Mountain Belt in felsic volcanic and volcaniclastic rocks of the Aillik (former Upper Aillik) Group. The rocks are affected by tight isoclinal folds with schistose axial planes and reverse faults attributed to the Hudsonian Orogeny. Later deformation has produced cross-folds, at least 15 km to the northeast in an area mapped in detail. Surrounding these rocks are the pre- and/or synkinematic granitoid intrusions, which include the Walker Lake and Burnt Lake granites.
