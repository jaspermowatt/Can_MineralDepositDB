Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) 6.5 km S of West Micmac Lake and 15 km NW of the Michelin Prospect. Access is by float plane or helicopter from Postville or Makkovik.

(Applies to: mineralization) Uranium and molybdenum mineralization occurs in sulphidic biotite-garnet schist in structural contact with footwall quartz-sericite schists and porphyroblastic, hematized granite. Drill results as of 2007 indicate that the main mineralized zone consists of two moderately to steeply dipping subparallel sheets that locally coalesce; there are numerous smaller zones. The mineralized zones apparently extend under a thrust front along the southern edge of the occurrence and are cut off to the north by a later fault. Description from MODS (Stapleton and Smith), Jan 3, 2008.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Early prospecting near this prospect involved the sampling of several boulder fans with grades in the range of 0.2% to 0.3% U3O8. 11 diamond drill holes were completed in 1981, four of which intersected uranium mineralization in biotite and in hornblende schist. MoS was also intersected.
