Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The Midwest A deposit is hosted in Aphebian basement rocks, in overlying sandstone, and at the unconformity. It consists of several sub-parallel high-grade mineralized structures (with U-Ni-As-Cu-Co) that follow the local ENE striking foliation. These structures are surrounded by low-grade remobilized and clay-rich mineralization that has formed in the typical pancake morphology. The most pronounced of these lenses occurs in the sandstone just under the contact with a chlorite zone and immediately under a conglomerate marker horizon found about 175 meters below surface. This conglomerate layer has been somewhat disturbed and locally destroyed by the mineralization-associated quartz dissolution. Another layer occurs just above the unconformity. The mineralized structures have structurally-controlled roots extending well down into the basement 10s of metres below the unconformity. Where a mineralized graphitic structure is observed in the basement, a zone of strong mineralization will invariably be found in the sandstone above the same structure. At the end of 2006, mineralization had been traced for over 275 metres and was open in three directions.
