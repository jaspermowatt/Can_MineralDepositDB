Qualified comments: (Applies to: miscellaneous comments) The Pele Mountain property includes 257 mining claims in 24 contiguous historical claim blocks in Joubin and Gunterman Townships. These claim blocks were in the hands of Rio Algom before they were allowed to lapse in the 1990's when the Elliot Lake mines closed down. The central part of the Pele Mountain property consists of the former Aquarius Porcupine Gold Mines - Pardee Amalgamated Mines property, where a large zone of mineralization was defined historically; this zone has been re-evaluated (Cochrane et al., 2007). Other claim blocks that have been included in the property are the Pecors property to the east, the Calder-Bousquet to the west, and the St. Mary Uranium Mines block to the northwest of the Aquarius-Pardee block. The north part of the Pele Mountain property was formerly the Stancan and Kerr-McGee properties. The Abeta block occurs to the west of the Pele Mountain property, but is not included in it.

(Applies to: regional geology) The Elliot Lake District is characterized by early Paleoproterozoic sedimentary rocks and minor basal volcanic rocks of the Huronian Supergroup (Matinenda Formation, Elliot Lake Group), which unconformably overly Archean basement. The Matinenda Formation consists mainly of quartzite, quartz arenite, sub-arkose, grit and quartz-pebble bearing polymictic conglomerate. The conglomerate beds or lenses are referred to as "reefs" and are located in relatively thick parts of the sequence that were deposit in west-northwest trending topographic depressions in the Archean basement, known as "channels". There are four channels recognised in the Elliot Lake District, from south-southwest to north-northeast, the Nordic Channel, the Pardee Channel, the Pecors Channel, the Whiskey Channel, and the Quirke Channel. The Huronian strata are folded in a broad, regional, gently northwest-plunging syncline (the Quirke Syncline) and a complementary anticline (Chiblow Anticline) to the south. The Quirke, Denison, Can-Met, Stanrock, Spanish American, and Panel mines are located along the Quirke Channel on the north limb of the Quirke Syncline, while the Nordic, Stanleigh, Lacnor, and Buckles mines, and the Pele Mountain Resources property, occur in the south limb. The Pronto Mine is located in the south limb of the Chiblow Anticline, and is cut off by the regional prominent Murray Fault.

(Applies to: property geology) Uranium mineralization in Pele Mountain's Elliot Lake property (now called Eco Ridge Mine project) is hosted by polymictic conglomerate beds within and near the base of the Ryan Member of the Mississagi Quartzite in the Matinenda Formation, Elliot Lake Group. These rocks rest on early Paleoproterozoic Huronian volcanic rocks, which unconformably overlie Archean basement granitoid rocks and greenstones. The mineralization coincides with the Pardee Channel, on the south limb of the Quirke Anticline. The Pardee zone or block central to the Pele property, which includes an Adit, has been subjected to preliminary evaluation, and is sited in the Main Conglomerate Bed (MCB, also named the Pardee Reef) 10-15 metres above the unconformity with the Archean basement. The MCB is relatively continuous, and extends beyond the area under evaluation. Mineralization has been recognized in another conglomerate horizon, the Basal Conglomerate Bed (BCB) at the base of the Ryan Member. The northwest-striking Pecors Fault traverses the property from the west side of Pecors Lake, and has produced offsets in the mineralized reef, as well as cross-cutting Nippissing diabase dykes.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization comprises detrital thorian uraninite that is associated with concentrations of detrital pyrite, both concentrated preferentially toward the base of the reef, as well as many secondary radioactive minerals such as pitchblende, brannerite, coffinite, uraniferous pyrite, and a uranium oxide-aluminium-silica mixture. Secondary minerals are highly significant in the basal conglomerate, where grades are positively correlated with the presence of abundant pyrite.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Pele Mountain Resources submitted a Project Description to the Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission in fall, 2008. The development schedule proposed may be delayed due to a drop in uranium prices current in 2009.

(Applies to: mineralization) Enrichment in light and heavy REE have now been evaluated, and a recent technical study suggests that REE may constitute a significant by-product (Ciuculescu, 2011). Monazite contains about 10% of the REE in the Main Conglomerate Bed, while about 10% of the REE occur in the uranium minerals uraninite, pitchblende, coffinite, and brannerite. Monazite is a major carrier of REE in the Basal Conglomerate as well, but the BCB contains less monazite than the MCB and a greater proportion of REE in the uranium minerals, especially coffinite.
