Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Regional geological mapping in 1976 of the Westmin permit area covering NTS 66 H5 and H6, and 66 G1 and G2, showed that the basal orthoquartzite of the Ayagaq Lake Formation in the northern part of sheets H5 and H6 has quartz pebble conglomerate beds near the boundary with the Archean basement to the south. The quartzite trends east-northeast and its boundary with the basement is not exposed. Some of the quartz pebble conglomerate beds in white orthoquartzite are radioactive. At Showing 76-2 weathered quartz pebble conglomerate beds gave total count reading of 25000 cpm on McPhar TV-1 Spectrometer (Schindler, 1977, p. 12-13). Radioactive mineral has not been identified, but it is noted that vuggy pyritic quartzite zones, ranging in length up to 20 m and thickness up to 2.5 m occur in the orthoquartzite in northern part of sheet H6. This observation suggests that the radioactive conglomerate beds may be comparable in character with the well known Paleoproterozoic Huronian pyritic quartz pebble conglomerate beds in the Elliot Lake mining district of Ontario. To the west-southwest in sheets G1 and G2 white orthoquartzite shows facies change to pink to rose-red orthoquartzite and to pale green argillaceous quartzite. The latter contain thin fissile slaty beds that are slightly higher in radioactivity than the background.
