Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Area 3 is rectangular, about 4.4 km long in north-south direction, with width averaging about 3.5 km in the northern part and 2.5 km in the southern part. Most of it is covered by overburden. Bedrock geology based on scarce outcrops, glacial drift and 4 vertical holes drilled in 1980 along a 2.75 km long northwest line, show shows that the central part of the area is underlain by the Oora Lake Formation, most of the southeastern part is underlain by the Showing lake Formation and the rest is underlain by the Thelon Formation. Prospecting after the drilling in the same year found a small pebbles containing 38% U. An air photo interpretation of overburden postulated that a local source of the pebble (1980 report to company by L.A. Bayrock). Mineralogical study (report to the company by A.R. Miller, 1982) of the pebble indicated that the mineralization is of the unconformity-associated type. In 1981 the property was explored by radar-controlled helicopter-borne radiometric survey, additional prospecting, soil geochemical and track etch surveys. Work in 1982 comprised retrieval of the track etch cups. geological mapping, radon soil survey, and ground geophysical surveys including VLF-EM, magnetometer and Induced Polarization surveys. The results defined a discrete magnetic low associated with a weak conductor and a resistivity low in the area of high track etch response. Geological mapping and the magnetic survey provided a better control on the location of the sub-Thelon Formation unconformity in the property area (Nicholls, 2005, p.24).
