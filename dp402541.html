Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1980 exploration of Permits 663 (NTS 66 H 7) and 664 (NTS 66 H 8), was undertaken by Union Oil Company of Canada Ltd. Three showings, numbered 80-14, 80-15 and 80-16 were discovered in 1980, and two others, numbered 81-01 and 81-03, were discovered in 1981. Showing 80-14 was discovered by prospecting and is located on an outcrop of feldspathic sandstone. A composite chip sample was collected, which assayed 680 ppm U3O8, 12 ppm Th, 33 ppm Cu, 21 ppm Ni and 420 ppm MoS2. The showing was examined and prospected in detail during 1981. Radioactivity is noted along 8 m strike length. Another zone of rock chips in glacial till was discovered 120 m to the east. Two pits were dug, one on each zone. The exposure of the eastern zone is 1x1 m, and it was chip sampled across strike over 35 cm, which assayed 180 ppm U, 26 ppm Th, 21 ppm Cu and 89 ppm Mo. A higher grade sample from the zone assayed 810 ppm U, 26 ppm Th, 33 ppm Cu and 140 ppm Mo. A pit dug 3 m west of the outcrop encountered disaggregated radioactive subcrop within 20 cm of the surface. The most radioactive of the 3 samples from the pit assayed 1260 ppm U, 30 ppm Th, 400 ppm Cu and 640 ppm Mo. The western zone comprises heterogeneous till containing rounded cobbles and boulders of granite, chips of siltstone, and angular fragments of radioactive feldspathic sandstone. A sample of radioactive material assayed 54 ppm U, 14 ppm Th, 82 ppm Cu and 13 ppm Mo. Traces of malachite were observed on some pieces.
