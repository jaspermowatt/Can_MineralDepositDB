Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1980 exploration of Permits 663 (NTS 66 H 7) and 664 (NTS 66 H 8), was undertaken by Union Oil Company of Canada Ltd. Three showings, numbered 80-14, 80-15 and 80-16 were discovered in 1980, and two others, numbered 81-01 and 81-03, were discovered in 1981. Showing 80-15 was found by prospecting on the southeast shore of South Amer Lake, and comprised one radioactive boulder and one zone of anomalous radioactivity in soil. Follow-up exploration in 1981 led to discovery of 28 radioactive boulders, ten of which were bulk sampled. Three radioactive soil anomalies were defined, trenched and sampled. The boulders and anomalies occur in an area 300x75 m. The trench on the lake shore comprises disaggregated sub-crop of buff, medium grained, apparently thin bedded feldspathic sandstone containing up to 1.5 % malachite, a black oxidation stain and shows 1000 to 2000 counts per second on scintillometer. Three samples from it at depths of 5, 20 and 35 cm have contents of 3900, 830 and 460 ppm U, 133000, 4600 and 680 ppm Cu, 143, 23 and 70 ppm Mo, and 18, 16 and 10 ppm Th, respectively. Additional 57 soil samples were taken on a rough grid over the area two other trenches east of the shore, which showed anomalous area extends 100 m to the east of shore, which is referred to as Showing 15A. Bedrock is not exposed except in one trench in the grid that has the disaggregated 'C' horizon at depth of 0.3 to 1.5 m. The rock is cleaved, and apparently represents a unit stratigraphically above the bedrock seen at Showing 15B, which is about 100 m to the southeast along the lake shore where 25 large angular radioactive boulders of thick bedded, pink to dark red feldspathic sandstone. They show radioactivity in range of 700 to 4000 cps in a single boulder. They are distributed just east of a 120 m long shore outcrop of similar feldspathic sandstone, which has strike varying from 340° to 006°, dip of 20° to the east and background radioactivity of 180 to 220 cps. Hence the radioactive boulders are locally derived and likely represent felsenmeer from the bedrock. Area east of the showings is concealed by glacial till. Nine of the boulders assayed gave range in values as follows: 14 to 2200 ppm U, 0 to 55 ppm Th, 12 to 290 ppm Cu, and 0 to 1960 ppm Mo.
