Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1980 exploration of Permits 663 (NTS 66 H 7) and 664 (NTS 66 H 8), was undertaken by Union Oil Company of Canada Ltd. Three showings, numbered 80-14, 80-15 and 80-16 were discovered in 1980, and two others, numbered 81-01 and 81-03, were discovered in 1981. Showing 80-16 was discovered by prospecting in 1980. It showed anomalously high total counts on scintillometer in a bed of quartz pebble conglomerate in basal quartzite of the Amer Group. The conglomerate is exposed discontinuously along strike length of 6.5 km. In addition to the Showing 80-16, nine other anomalously high radioactive localities are found in the conglomerate, which is structurally in a folded thrust sheet on the north limb of a syncline. It is about 1 m thick, and is stratigraphically near the top of the thick quartzite of the Ayagaq Formation. Clasts in the conglomerate are composed entirely of quartz, and are up 2 cm in length and elliptical in shape. They are well packed in quartz sand matrix that comprises 20 to 30 % of the rock. Radioactivity in the conglomerate was checked with Urtec spectrometer, which showed that in each case thorium content was very high relative to the uranium content. An analysis of a composite sample from Showing 80-16 taken in 1980, which had total counts of 2080 per second, returned results as 4.7 ppm U and 570 ppm Th. Nine other bulk rock samples taken in 1981 returned U values in range of 0.4 to 4.0 ppm and Th values in the range of 305 to 1570 ppm (Knox and Gidluk, 1982, p. 138). Furthermore gold values were very low, less than 4 ppb with one value of 12 ppb. Hence the showing was regarded as of no further exploration interest. Mineral phases contain Th and U are not identified.
