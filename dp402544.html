Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1980 exploration of Permits 663 (NTS 66 H 7) and 664 (NTS 66 H 8), was undertaken by Union Oil Company of Canada Ltd. Three showings, numbered 80-14, 80-15 and 80-16 were discovered in 1980, and two others, numbered 81-01 and 81-03, were discovered in 1981. Showing 81-01 is located about 300 m northeast of the better exposed Showing 80-14. It occurs at the upper boundary of the feldspathic sandstone unit whereas Showing 80-14 is in the middle or lower part of the unit. Exploration in 1981 at Showing 81-01 comprised detailed prospecting, and sampling of 8 rock geochemical samples and 15 soil geochemical samples. The mineralization is known from 3 zones of angular fragments of radioactive feldspathic sandstone within a strike length of 150 m. The host unit is exposed in only one small outcrop, which comprises buff and pink feldspathic sandstone dipping 55° to the south on apparently overturned north limb of a tight anticline. A trench in glacial till 10 m south of the outcrop, exposed 10x2 m subcrop from which 4 samples were analyzed. Three of them have metal contents as follows: U : 1100 to 1700 ppm, Cu : 204 to 300, and Mo : 3 to 4 ppm. The fourth sample contained 110 ppm Mo, 50 ppm Cu and 1.9 ppm U. Th contents in the four samples is between 16 and 35. The main or central zone of fragments has the highest radioactivity, and a 1 kg pink fragment contained 3800 ppm U, 17 ppm Th, 209 ppm Cu and 91 ppm Mo. Two other samples form the same zone contained somewhat lower values viz., 2600 and 2300 ppm U, with 310 and 140 ppm Cu and undetectable Mo. A locality 150 m to the east across the south end of a swamp has radioactive angular boulders of feldspathic sandstone in stream bed on the edge of a till ridge to the south. Assays of one fragment weighing 1 kg and of two composite chip samples showed values in range of 2300 to 3800 ppm U, 140 to 310 ppm Cu. The 1 kg sample also contained 61 ppm Mo. The third locality 100 m to the east has a subcrop of angular fragments with relatively low radioactivity and a composite bulk sample assayed 270 ppm U, 26 ppm Cu and 14 ppm Mo. Soil samples of A horizon from gentle slope 10 to 20 m down from the three localities, showed values up to 7.5 ppm U compared with 1.2 ppm background values.
