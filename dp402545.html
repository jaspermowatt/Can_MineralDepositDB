Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) In 1980 exploration of Permits 663 (NTS 66 H 7) and 664 (NTS 66 H 8), was undertaken by Union Oil Company of Canada Ltd. Three showings, numbered 80-14, 80-15 and 80-16 were discovered in 1980, and two others, numbered 81-01 and 81-03, were discovered in 1981. Showing 81-03 is the easternmost of them in Permit 664. It is located 5 km east of Showing 81-01. It was discovered during evaluation of geochemical anomaly 'A' in 1981. It comprises several isolated radioactive spots within disaggregated and slumped talus of siltstone. These spots contained radioactive fragments of a vein up to 5 cm thick, with vuggy quartz, limonite, chlorite and pitchblende. The siltstone talus lies within 50 m north of a line of 4 elongate outcrops of feldspathic sandstone trending east. Its unexposed north boundary with siltstone is somewhere along the gully that has stream flowing west. The sandstone outcrops show overturned beds dipping 55° to the south. It is regarded as a thin, isoclinally folded wedge in the hinge zone of an anticline. Prospecting outlined several spots of anomalous radioactivity over a distance of 300 m on the north side of the gully. Three small trenches were dug. Five rock geochemical samples were assayed. The highest values were in a 1.5 lb sample of vein material that gave a scintillometer reading of 3950 cps, and contained 1300 ppm U and 14 ppm of Cu. Other 4 sample assays were in range of 6 to 220 ppm U and 7 to 16 ppm Cu.
