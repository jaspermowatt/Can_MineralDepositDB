Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Cogema's 1982-'83 drilling revealed promising L, G and S zones. A model based on the L zone emphasized intersection of gabbro dyke with a particular stratigraphic zone, namely the boundary between the dolomite of Lower Peribonca Formation and underlying sandstone of Indicator Formation. The L zone grid was therefore extended 3 km to the southwest and 9 km to the northeast. The whole grid was explored by systematic soil geochemical survey, prospecting, geological mapping, and ground magnetic and VLF-R surveys. These were followed by drill hole fences 500 m apart along the favourable strike zone. The drilling found L-type mineralization at two localities, namely the OBELU 229 on the 2A-46 Grid 12 km to the northeast of L zone, and OELV 290 on the Castor Grid 6 km northeast of the L zone. In the 2A-46 grid the mineralization was intersected in the hole OBELU 229 that dips 70° to the northwest at about 100 m that assayed 0.17 % U over 0.8 m (Pauwels 2005, p. 16). The mineralization occurs at contact of sandstone of Indicator Formation and a gabbro dyke that is tectonized and has carbonate and chlorite alteration minerals. Weak radioactivity was noted at about 10 m in the laminated sandstone-dolomite zone below purple sandstone dipping gently to the southeast.
