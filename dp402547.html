Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Encouraging results on the L prospect led Cogema (Canada) Ltd. to explore further along the northeast trend of mineralization. The Castor and 2A-46 grids were cut about 6 km and 12 km, respectively, to the northeast of the L prospect. The strike zone was drill tested during 1984, by two holes on sections 500 m apart and collared 150 m apart along the sections. On the Castor grid drill hole 290 intersected 0.15 % U over 4.5 m. Two additional holes were drilled 40 m from hole 290 on each side (numbered 296 and 297 on the northwest and southeast sides, respectively). They showed anomalous radioactivity at the level of mineralization in hole 290, but not high enough to merit assaying. The drilling revealed a gabbro sill intruded along gently dipping sandstone of Indicator Formation. The stratigraphic level is lower than that seen at the L prospect, which is close to the top of the formation that is overlain by the basal dolomitic unit of Peribonca Formation. The drill holes showed significant silicification surrounding the uranium mineralization. This alteration and associated radioactivity trend north-northeast. This trend was missed in next set of 3 holes (# 292, 293 and 294) on the section 400 m to the northeast.
