Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Between 1969 and 1979, a number of companies did scintillometer and radon pot surveys to identify radioactive anomalous zones previously defined on aerial radiometric anomaly maps. In 1969, Chemalloy Minerals Limited did diamond drilling on nine anomalous areas located in the Hudderfield and Pontefract Townships for a total of 2234 feet (681 m). In 1977-1978, Copconda Mines Limited did drilling in the Huddersfield Township. Aldershot acquired the claims in 2006 and did an heliborne radiometric and magnetic survey as well as a ground survey the following year.
