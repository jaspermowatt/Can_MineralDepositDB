Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) From Moore and Harris (2005): The JRB-4 area was first staked by J.R. Beauchemin in 1965. In 1969, the area was subjected to a property-wide, systematic ground scintillometer survey. In 1975, J.R. Beauchemin re-staked the area and carried out a program of limited geological mapping, prospecting, outcrop stripping and bulldozing. This program lead to the discovery of six radioactive showings: New, Lac Lafargue, Pond, Lac Effiat, No.5 and No.6. Analyses of rock samples collected from various JRB-4 showings report an approximate uranium concentration range from 340 ppm to 9,000 ppm (18.0 lb/t). In 1978, Dejour Mines Ltd optioned the property from J.R. Beauchemin. In September of that year, the company conducted a preliminary property examination and found that uranium values were erratic.

(Applies to: mineralization) Some radioactive rocks are also fractured and contain pyrite brands overgrown by muscovite filling fractures. In such fractured rocks, biotite is partly altered by chlorite, and thorite by chlorite and thorobastnaesite.
