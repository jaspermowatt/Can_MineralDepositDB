Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) A more significant area than the GSC Showing, although less spectacular, is that covered by the pink radioactive granite to the east of the "GSC Showing". This granite is basically identical to that of Anomaly 60. Sporadic in its radioactivity, it gives results ranging from detection limit (XRF) of less than 20 ppm U (0.047 lbs U3O8/t) to 1019 ppm U (2.40 lbs U3O8/t). This "hot" granite appears to have a northwest trend and extends for 1000m with an approximate variable width of 200m. The center of this showing area lies about 180m to the northeast of the "GSC Showing". The coded location point was the site of Sample 89924 which returned 2.40 lbs U3O8/t, which is the best sample within this zone.
