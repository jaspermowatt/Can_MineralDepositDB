Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) According to Ross (2015), mineralization in the Patterson Lake South property is hosted primarily in metasedimentary basement and to a much lesser extent within the overlying sandstone thought to be Devonian in age. Basement mineralization is commonly focussed in the graphitic pelitic gneiss as fine-grained disseminated and fracture filling uranium minerals associated with hydrocarbons and/or carbonaceous matter and pervasive chlorite and clay alteration. Rusty limonite-hematite alteration correlates with high grade alteration and "wormy" texture. Less common styles of mineralization associated with the highest grades are: semi-massive and hydrocarbon rich mineralization, intense clay alteration with uranium-hydrocarbon buttons, and massive metallic mineralization. The latter zones generally occur along the contact of the graphitic schist and silicified semi-pelite, comprising a high grade mineralized spine. Particularly well mineralized intersections contain thin swarms of dravite-filled breccia. Uranium mineralization in pelites peripheral to the graphitic pelite and spine is disseminated. The dominant uranium mineral is uraninite, with subordinate coffinite, possible brannerite, and U-Pb oxide/oxyhydroxide.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization in the overlying Devonian? sandstone occurs as fine-grained disseminations, sooty blebs, and rarely semi-massive mineralization. There are possibly several ages of uranium mobilization and mineralization.
