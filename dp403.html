Qualified comments: (Applies to: location and access) The Husselbee uranium showing lies within actinolite skarn, on "Discovery Hill", located about 1 to 2 kilometres south of Deep Bay on the west side of Atlin Lake, approximately 17 kms northwest of Atlin.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The property was discovered and staked in 1953, and minor surface work and drilling was completed. Additional trenching was completed in 1967 which was the last year the property was worked on.

(Applies to: mineralization) - The original showing on Discovery Hill, and also one 400 metres to the west, is composed of dark green to black, fine-grained amphibolite consisting largely of bladed amphibolites which can be up to 5 cm long. Light grey-green varieties, forming rosettes, are dominantly actinolite as the major amphibolite which form rosettes. Sparsely disseminated hematite can give the rock a reddish colour. Irregular masses or pods of jasper are also common and they are often mineralized with pyrite, fluorite, and galena. - Uraninite and apatite are present, but the identity of the thorium-bearing mineral is uncertain.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Partially recentralized xenoliths of limestone are present. These indicate a possible volcanic agglomerate or breccia protolith for the amphibolite which may have been part of the Lower Pennsylvanian to Triassic Horsefeed Formation of the Mississippian to Triassic Cache Creek Group (Complex?).

(Applies to: regional geology) Underlying the amphibolite and exposed in areas away from Discovery Hill is a variably textured and heterogeneous granodiorite to monzodiorite with pink porphyritic alkali feldspar. These are most likely part of the Jurassic Fourth of July Creek Batholith of the Coast Intrusions.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) The Fourth of July Creek Batholith has been zircon dated at 171 +1/-5 Ma (Fieldwork 1990, in prep.).

(Applies to: reserve/resource assays) A sample from the top of Discovery Hill assayed 0.012% uranium and 0.16% thorium oxide and a sample 380 metres to the west assayed 0.059% uranium and 0.17% thorium. Another sample from the area assayed 0.14% uranium and 0.04% thorium oxide (Minister of Mines Annual Report 1953). Small calcite and dolomite veins cut the amphibolite and contain disseminated molybdenite, pyrite, and chalcopyrite.. Samples have assayed as high as 0.11% molybdenite (Assessment Report 2786).
