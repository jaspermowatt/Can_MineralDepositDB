Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) - The MacLean Lake occurrence was found in 1957 by prospector MacLean during the course of general prospecting in the area for British Newfoundland Exploration Limited during 1957. Some stripping of outcrops was done at the time of discovery. - In 1967 the company conducted detailed geological mapping, a magnetometer survey and a radiometric survey in a grid area of 350x100 m on scale 1 : 480. Radiometric survey results indicate two en echelon mineralized zones. The high readings are 1000 to 1300 cps on SRAT-SPP2 scintillometer. A magnetic low shows very good coincidence with high radiometric anomalies. Four trenches were blasted to test a strike length of 170 m. The best assay was 0.17 % U on a grab sample. Chip samples from two trenches gave the following results: 0.051 % U over 1 m and 0.034 % U over 2.134 m. A bulk sample of 500 lbs (226.8 kg), collected for metallurgical study, assayed 0.0458 % U.
