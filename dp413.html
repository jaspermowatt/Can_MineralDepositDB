Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) - The Rainbow radioactive boulder train was discovered and defined in 1970 by R.S. Doak. It is over 3.2 km long and up to 45 m wide. Follow-up exploration in 1971 included ground radiometric, radon gas and soil geochemical surveys, glacial studies and diamond drilling. Ten holes totalling 930 m were drilled, five of them on the Rainbow zone and others on the Dufus zone located 15 km to the east-northeast on its strike. The drilling outlined a mineralized zone in bedrock at shallow depth, striking east-northeast and dipping about 60° to the south-southeast. It showed that the mineralized zone is lithologically similar to the radioactive boulders. The zone has a strike length of about 2 km. Additional surface exploration in the area was undertaken in 1974, which included trenching, further boulder studies and radiometric survey. Further exploratory drilling was done in 1975, and included 7 widely spaced holes totalling 795 m. These holes extended the zone to south-southwest for 5 km. The mineralization appears to form en echelon lenses. - During 1974 Brinex resumed boulder mapping, Pleistocene studies, boulder assays, trenching and radiometric surveys over the Rainbow Zone boulder train. Ground magnetic and track-etch surveys of the zone were also carried out that year. A small diamond drilling program was undertaken by the company in 1975. Seven holes totalling 795 m were drilled, which intersected mineralization to a depth of 115 m, and outline the Rainbow deposit. A mineralogical study on specimens from the Rainbow deposit was carried out in 1976. - During the following years of downturn in uranium market on the exploration on the deposit ceased, and concessions held by Brinex expired. With the upturn in the uranium market during 2003, the area of Rainbow deposit was staked by Altius Resources, and later the ownership was later transferred to Aurora Energy, a joint venture company owned by Altius and Fronteer Resources. The new company carried out reconnaissance work in 2005, and a more intensive exploration in 2005-'06, which included and the geological mapping, prospecting and an airborne magnetic and spectrometer surveys, orthophotography, environmental studies, followed by diamond drilling in 1976. A total of 2,483 m were drilled in holes located to confirm historic results from the drilling carried out by Brinex in the 1970s.

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) Aurora Energy Resources has delineated an initial deposit that is at least 300 metres long and up to 13 metres wide. Drilling has intersected the deposit between 15 and 115 vertical metres. It is open for expansion along strike, and has not been drill tested below 150 metres (Aurora Energy Press Release, November 16, 2006).
