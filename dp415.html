Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Two mineralized outcrops were discovered 5 km north of Chitra Lake in 1971 during radioactive boulder prospecting carried out by the Brinex team of Rumpel and Solomons. The outcrops are about 1.5 km apart, and were trenched. One of them has a mineralized zone 22.5 m long and 0.3 m to 0.6 m wide, trending east-northeast and dipping 60 to 70° to the southeast. Diamond drilling done in 1975 totalled 1057 m in 11 drill holes. Eight of these were drilled to test mineralization in the trenched outcrops, and the southern one of which yielded two best intersections in two holes, CH-75-3 and 4 drilled from the same collar. The first intersection is of 6.0 m grading 0.084% U3O8 and the second 6.6 m grading 0.111% U3O8. The three other holes were drilled about 7 km to the east-northeast along the regional trend of host rocks to test a target of anomalous radioactivity. The best intersection in them was 3.3 m grading 0.067% U3O8 in hole CH-75-10. It is apparent that the mineralization occurs in small lenses and is generally low in grade.
