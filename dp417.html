Qualified comments: (Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) A sheet-like stratiform deposit that may contain approximately 454,000 kg of U3O8, and is fairly uniform in grade; proportions of the body included in early historical tonnage estimations grade between 0.117 % to 0.440 % U3O8. Small amounts of V and Cu are also present.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization is concentrated in a mafic tuffaceous unit of the Post Hill Group (formerly the Lower Aillik Group) of circa 2178 Ma age. It occurs as small grains within hornblende and biotite, and locally at higher (ore) grades in veinlets and fracture fillings.

(Applies to: regional structure) The mineralized area, including possibly related Nash occurrences, is within a complex shear zone known as the Nakit Slide.
