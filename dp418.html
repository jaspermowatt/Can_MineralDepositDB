Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization is stratabound, and locally associated with sulphides. The uranium mineralization is concentrated in thin, folded, layers and laminae in the metasediments wherein radioactive particles form inclusions within hornblende and biotite, which also have local inclusions of pyrrhotite and chalcopyrite. Higher grade pitchblende mineralization is concentrated along shear surfaces and in fractures. Relatively high radioactivity also coincides with the axial regions of small folds.

(Applies to: discovery and development) The Inda Lake Trend, which includes the Gear occurrence, was investigated extensively between the late 1960's and mid 1980's. It is currently being explored by Aurora Energy Resources Inc. Drilling in 2006 revealed ore grade uranium as well as measurable base and precious metals along the trend. Recent drilling at Gear has yielded assays of 0.38% U3O8, and intersections of 0.17% U3O8 over 10 m, and has defined mineralization over a strike length of 200 m and extended it to considerable depths (Aurora Energy, Inda Lake Trend, 2007).
