Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Sporadic radioactivity occurs over 3636 metres in banded metasediments ("quartzites" or quartz-rich rocks with foliae of biotite, hornblende and magnetite) and iron formation. It is most intense in the mafic bands of the iron formation. These rocks host abundant pegmatites; coarse molybdenite is developed along their contacts with iron formation and also as disseminations along the foliation planes in the metasediments. Cross faults with graphite and pyrite displace the association.
