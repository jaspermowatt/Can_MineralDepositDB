Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The showing was discovered in 1955 by A. Montague (assisted by L. Montague) prospecting for British Newfoundland Exploration Ltd. During 1956 19 trenches were blasted and couple of packsack drilling in 2 holes were done. The assay results showed low contents of uranium. In 1964 Mokta Canada Ltée acquired a development licence for the Moran Lake area and subsequently discovered uranium mineralization at Lady Lake, the site of the Moran C zone. The company carried out considerable amount of prospecting, trenching and packsack drilling in the Moran A, B and C zones. In 1969 the allowed the licence to expire, and then it was granted to Brinex, but no further work was done on the property.
