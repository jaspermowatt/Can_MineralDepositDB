Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Uranium was discovered near Moran Lake in 1956 by A. Montague prospecting for British Newfoundland Exploration Limited. The company conducted geological mapping and radiometric survey in the Moran Lake area during 1956-1958. Trenching and detailed showed the best values in brecciated and highly altered mafic volcanic unit cut by numerous pink calcite and white quartz stringers. The best trench value was 0.11 5 U3O8 over 11 feet. In 1964 Mokta Canada Ltée acquired a development licence for the Moran Lake area and subsequently discovered uranium mineralization at Lady Lake, the site of the Moran C zone. The company carried out considerable amount of prospecting, trenching and packsack drilling in the Moran A, B and C zones. In 1969 the allowed the licence to expire, and then it was granted to Brinex, but no further work was done on the property. In 1976 the licence was granted to Commodore Mining Co. Ltd., which in turn optioned it to Shell Canada Resources Ltd. (Shell). During 1976 Shell carried out preliminary geological mapping on C zone, and geological, magnetometer and track etch surveys over the nearby B zone. In 1977 the company drilled 15 holes totalling 864 m on the B zone, and conducted intensive exploration of the C zone including geological mapping, systematic trench sampling, scintillometer survey and drilling of 8 hole totalling 497 m. The fault zone hosting mineralization was further explored by ground magnetic, track etch and VLF-EM surveys, followed in winter of 1978 by 17 drill holes totalling 1542 m that led to some high grade mineralization. Further surface exploration and drilling of 15 holes totalling 1581 m were targeted at the uraniferous quartzites. This found low grade mineralization in altered sandstone above the Aphebian-Helikian unconformity which was further outlined in 1979 by 1193 m of drilling in 17 holes and was defined as Lower C zone. However Shell abandoned its interest in the area later that year. Lewis Murphy acquired the Moran Lake claims in 1973 and optioned them to Crosshair Exploration and Mining Corp. in 2004.An airborne gravity anomaly of 4 to 8 milligals was located over the Moran B and C zones which is elliptical in shape and as much as 4 km long. This was later confirmed by ground gravity survey, and invoked analogy with Olympic Dam-type (Iron oxide-Cu-Au) deposit. The company launched a major surface and drilling program in 2006, which included 137 drill holes totalling 21486 m. Of these 48 holes tested the Upper C zone and 10 holes tested the Lower C zone. Additional drilling in 2007 tested both zones by 15 holes, plus 6 tested the upper zone and 5 tested the lower zone. In all the drilling totalled 8211 m. The resource estimate of 2007 is based on 133 diamond drill holes (84 by Crosshair and 49 by Shell) using ordinary krigging to interpolate grades into 10x104 m blocks. The Upper zone is vein-type, and the lower zone is a sheet like body. Hence their resources were estimated separately in 2007.

(Applies to: mineralization) Uranium mineralization occurs in two zones, namely the Upper C (UC) zone and the Lower C (LC) zone. The larger UC zone is hosted mostly by the brecciated and altered mafic volcanic/intrusive rocks of Joe Pond Formation of the Paleoproterozoic Moran Lake Group. This volcanic unit is unconformably overlain by conglomerate-sandstone of the Heggart Lake Formation of the Mesoproterozoic Bruce River Group. The dip of these units is about 45 degrees to the southeast. The LC zone is located north of the UC zone and trends west-southwest, whereas the UC zone trends southwest. Their east ends are less than 200 m apart. The LC zone is hosted largely by the Heggart Lake Formation, which is 50 to 100 m thick and is thrust over from the south side by the Joe Pond Formation, thus repeating the succession in a triangular block about 1000 m wide on the southwest side and pinching out 1200 m to the northeast. Surface projection of the UC zone is along the southeast side of the thrust faulted block of the Joe Pond Formation. The host rocks have undergone complex episodes of oxidation, brecciation, and quartz and carbonate veining prior to introduction of pitchblende (uraninite) with pyrite, chalcopyrite and iron oxides as numerous veins and fracture-fillings. The sulphides range from traces to 5 per cent. Notable amount of Cu, Ag and V are present in the hematized and brecciated mafic rocks of the UC zone. The smaller LC zone is more discrete, stratabound zone of disseminated uranium mineralization in diffuse reduced green sandstone or conglomerate with spotty chlorite, and characterized by diffuse or patchy hematitic overprint.
