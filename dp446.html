Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The showing was discovered in 1964 by Mokta Canada Ltée. The company explored the Moran Lake area under a development licence. The company renamed the two previously found Montague 1 and 2 showings to the southwest as Moran B and C zones. The company carried out considerable amount of prospecting, trenching and packsack drilling in all the zones. In 1969 the allowed the licence to expire, and then it was granted to Brinex, but no further work was done on the property. In 1976 the licence was granted to Commodore Mining Co. Ltd., which in turn optioned it to Shell Canada Resources Ltd. (Shell).

(Applies to: mineralization) Multiple radioactive zones are confined to narrow shear zones and associated fractures in sandstones and pebble to cobble conglomerates of the Heggart Lake Formation. The sandy conglomerate matrix is typically greyish where mineralized. The A zone occurs approximately 1500 m stratigraphically above the unconformity with the older Moran Lake Group, and is in an area affected by faulting. The northeast-southwest faults (the latest generation) are associated with the shearing that is thought to have influenced the localization of mineralization.
