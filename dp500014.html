Qualified comments: (Applies to: host rocks) The host volcanics (St. James) accumulated in a cauldron-collapse setting and were deformed and faulted during cauldron subsidence. They are unconformably overlain by rhyolitic ignimbrite of the Watershed North Rhyolite. The mineralised zone consists of subparallel, steeply dipping veins and fractures, within and parallel to the axial plane of a syncline.

(Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Late Paleozoic acid volcanic province within St. James volcanic massif. The structural setting includes steeply dipping fractures, sub-parallel to trend of a syncline/caldron structure, plus autobrecciation, flow structures.

(Applies to: mineralization) As well as 5,000 to 10,000 metric tons of U, the deposit contains 4578 t Mo average grade 0.15% Mo.
