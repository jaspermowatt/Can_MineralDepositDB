Qualified comments: (Applies to: miscellaneous comments) This entry refers to several, slightly different deposits: Mount Gee, Mount Gee East, , Radium Ridge, Armchair-Streitburg, Hodgkinson, Gunsight, and Shamrock all located within the Mount Painter Inlier. The Mount Painter Inlier contains Paleoproterozoic metasedimentary rocks called the Radium Creek Metamorphics, two phases of Mesoproterozoic granitoid rocks, and Cambro-Ordivician granitoids. The host rocks to the uranium deposits occur in the south-central part of the inlier, and consist of several huge bodies of breccia called the Radium Ridge Breccias. The breccias are classified as: granitic breccia, hematitic breccia, and chloritic breccia. Granitic breccias are derived from local Mount Painter granites and metamorphic rocks, and are variably potassium metasomatized and silicified. Hematitic breccias form lens-shaped bodies at or near the base of the breccia piles, and are characterized by hematite in the matrices and by granitic clasts which may be partly hematized themselves. There may be a gradation from granitic to hematitic breccia. Chloritic breccia consists of granitic and/or hematitic breccias that were intensely chloritized. These breccias are thought to be associated with a major fault system and hydraulic fracturing which exploited earlier zones of structural weakness, with the hematite being introduced during hot-spring activity. Some of the breccias are probably sedimentary in origin.
