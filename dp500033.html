Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The uranium occurs in the sands and interbedded peat that form the Mulga Rock paleochannel, which erodes the Paterson Formation which unconformably overlies granitoid and metamorphic rocks of the Archean Yilgarn Craton. Uranium was adsorbed onto or into the organic matter within the peat. Discrete uranium minerals are observed rarely. The uranium may have been leached from the igneous and metamorphic rocks of the Yilgarn and Albany-Fraser Provinces.
