Qualified comments: (Applies to: host rocks) The real host rocks are phosphate-altered rocks called collophanite, which affect crystalline limestone, or marble, with associated episyenite and albitite. The collophanite occurs preferentially in stockworks resulting from the filling of fractures and/or cavities in the limestones, or as breccias together with limestone, feldsphathic and calc-silicate fragments, which occur to a depth of 150 m. Below this zone, mineralization is disseminated and may appear as impregnations in the host rock and as fillings in vacuoles as well as in the intergranular spaces of feldspathic rocks. There is evidence of supergene enrichment in the upper part of the deposit, where the massive ore occurs.

(Applies to: reserve/resource descriptions) The Itataia mine has geological reserves of 142.5 thousand tons of uranium associated with phosphate, and approximately79,5 million tons of ore with grades of 11% of P205 and 998 ppm of U3O8, which could produce 8,9 million tons of P205 and 79,3 million tons of U3O8. This mine also contains circa 300 million m³ of uranium-free marble,.
