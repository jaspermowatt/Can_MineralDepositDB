(Applies to: development) One open pit and three underground deposits previously mined, with exploration and development potential at three to four other deposits. 20,000 M of drifts and three concrete-lined shafts, one of which is a production shaft.

(Applies to: mineralization) According to the Khan Resources presentation (Quick, 2007), the ore is apparently distributed in lensoid zones within the lower basalt-andesite sequence. The mineralization post-dates extensive hydrothermal alteration consisting of silicification, argillization, and carbonatization; post-ore alteration introduces quartz, fluorite, and carbonate (Chemillac et al, 2005). Calculations by the latter authors conclude, using mass-balance calculations on the compositions of fresh melt inclusions and bulk analyses of altered rhyolites, that these magmas could easily have supplied all uranium in the district.
