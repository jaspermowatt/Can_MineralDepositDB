Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The mineralization is associated with sheets of medium to fine grained leucogranite and pegmatite with granophyric, equigranular, porphyritic and pegmatitic textures. Compositions are not restricted to alaskite, and the term sheeted leucogranite is preferred. There are up to six phases (A-F) of leucogranite is the area, of which the fourth (D) and fifth (E) phases are uraniferous and have intruded the Damaran sequence late-tectonically. In the area of the Rössing Mine, sheets of the uraniferous D phase have been preferentially emplaced in the Neoproterozoic Damaran Khan Formation around the sheared disconformity with the younger Rössing Formation on the south side of a domal structure (D3) called the Rössing Dome, which exposes older migmatites and gneisses. The localization of the uraniferous granite is interpreted by Basson and Greenway (2004) to have been influenced by sinistral transpression (~ D4), causing counterclockwise rotation of the relatively rigid dome with dilatation in its lee. Deformation continued after the emplacement of the U-rich granite, and allowed further U enrichment by hydrothermal fluid along arcs around the S-SE margin of the dome which transgress the orientations of the granite and its host lithologies. It was this enrichment which allowed Rössing to be mineable during years of relatively low uranium prices, whereas occurrences of the same generations of uraniferous leucogranite (Goanikontes and Valencia) were not economic. Current U economics are changing this situation.

(Applies to: regional geology) The uranium deposit(s) occur in the southern portion of the Central Zone of the intracontinental branch of the Damara Orogenic belt, which is part of the Neoproterozoic-Paleozoic Pan-African Mobile belt system which traverses Africa. The southern Central Zone is bounded to the south by the Okanhandja lineament and to the north by the Omaruru lineament (which bisects the central zone into the northern and southern parts). The Paleoproterozoic Abbabis Complex forms the basement that unconformably underlies the Neoproterozoic Damaran metasedimentary rocks that host the late to post-tectonic leucogranites that are associated with the economic uranium mineralization. The Proterozoic basement and metasedimentary sequences are highly and complexly metamorphosed, reaching granulite facies at least at one stage. There are folded at least four times during the complex P-T history, imprinting the Central Zone with a dome and basin structure which is absent north and south of the zone. The earliest deformation recognized was associated with recumbent structures, thrusting and shearing with northwesterly trending fold axes, The later, most prominent fold episode (F3) imparted a northeasterly-striking and steeper structural grain, and is thought responsible for dome and basin structures. F4 overprinted F3, and imparted variable northeast and north-northeast trends west of a north-northeast trending magnetic lineament, the Welwitchia lineament. It is thought that F4 had some influence on emplacement of the late-stage uraniferous leucogranites, which are post-D3, in Cambrian time.

(Applies to: host rocks) There are both equigranular syenogranite to monzogranite bodies and sheeted leucogranites (recognized as being related to the uranium) in the southern Central Zone of the Damaran Orogen. There are at least six generations of sheeted leucogranites (SLGs). Studies combining geochemical and petrographic characterization with structural analysis show that only the fourth (D) and fifth (E) generation of sheeted leucogranites carry appreciable uranium, that they were derived as initial melts from a pristine source unrelated to the sources of other granitoid generations, and not as second generation melts from the older granitoids and gneisses exposed in the area.
