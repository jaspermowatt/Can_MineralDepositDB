Qualified comments: (Applies to: host rocks) The host rocks associated with the metallic Cu and Fe mineralization are carbonatites that intruded later than the large ultramafic pyroxenite and dunite intrusive complex that envelopes them. Cu mineralization superseded massive magnetite.

(Applies to: mineralization) The Phalaborwa complex yields mainly copper, apatite (phosphate and REE) and magnetite pellets with many other by-products. It is only the huge scale of the operation that allows U3O8 concentrate to be recovered commercially. The concentration of U3O8 in the orebody are only about 30 ppm (cited in Harmer, 2000)

(Applies to: location) The location, read from Google Earth, is derived from the central part of the Loolekop open pit. There are several other open pits.
