Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs where the deformed and metamorphosed Paleoproterozoic Lower Cahill metasedimentary rocks are upfaulted against the unconformably overlying Kombolgie Supergroup. Hematitic quartz breccias were developed and graphite-chlorite schist silicified. Two alteration zones surround mineralization: an outer zone of chloritization of ferromagnesian minerals, sericitization of feldspar, and silicification of fault zones; and an inner zone of intense chlorite replacement and removal of silica.
