Qualified comments: (Applies to: deposit geology) The deposit is completely covered by Recent alluvium and Cretaceous sand. Mineralization occurs in chloritized breccia, pegmatoid, and quartz-sericite-chlorite schist of the Cahill Formation, mainly in the breccia and pegmatoid. It occurs within 200 m of the contact with the Archean-Paleoproterozoic Nanambu Complex. It may have once been beneath the unconformity with the sandstones of the Late Paleoproterozoic Kombolgie Group. However it is now located immediately below the unconformity with Cretaceous sands. The mineralization consists of sooty and colloform uraninite.
