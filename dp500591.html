Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) This deposit consists of multiple zones that cover a wide area (100-150 square kilometres), which is or will be exploited by underground mining. High grade ore will be extracted and sent to a mill, while the rest will be extracted by in-situ heap leaching. The location given by various Internet sources for the Saghand site apparently overlaps with numerous magnetite-apatite-actinolite occurrences hosted by and related to the Early Cambrian volcano-plutonic arc rocks. It is uncertain, but possible that the Saghand property and these Kiruna-type iron ore deposits are related, the same, or that they are formed by enrichment of the iron ore deposits during a superimposed metasomatic event that made use of structural zones of weakness, and possibly an influx of carbonatite.

(Applies to: regional geology) The area is underlain by Paleoproterozoic, Mesoproterozoic, and Neoproterozoic crustal elements, multiply deformed, variably metamorphosed, and thrust stacked prior to an Infracambrian unconformity. These rocks are overlain by Infracambrian through Cambrian, largely unmetamorphosed intermediate-felsic volcanic and subvolcanic rocks, dolomitic limestone and gypsum. The latter are unconformably overlain by lower Cambrian red sandstone and conglomerate. Surficial materials that drape areas of high relief are Neogene-Quaternary.
