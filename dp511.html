Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) From Moore and Harris (2005): 1968-1978: The area was explored by Canadian Johns Manville Company Ltd. The company’s ten-year exploration efforts included several geophysical surveys (air and ground based), regional and local scale prospecting and geological mapping, extensive line-cut control grids, grid and regional-scale soil/silt sampling, bulldozer assisted surface stripping and trenching, drill holes (packsack, RC and diamond) . Kish’s 1975 report (DP-310) is first to identify the Mont Laurier area biotite paragneisses as a potential host of uranium mineralization. 1978-1982: SOQUEM optioned the CJM property in 1978. For the next four years, Soquem carried out a number of exploration programs on the No.2, Bear, 3-3d, and Max Zones (± Andy Lake area). These programs included several geophysical surveys (air and ground based), regional and local scale prospecting and detailed geological and structural mapping, two limited radon in soils surveys, and 112 diamond drill holes (~ 4,753 metres total).
