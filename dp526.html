Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) From Moore and Harris (2005): 1969-1973: The Tom Dick area was first staked by Mount Laurier Uranium Mines Inc (“MLU”), during the early stages of the Mount Laurier area uranium staking rush (1969). The company’s claim holdings were considerable, including a north-south swath of claims, which extended from just north of the Tom Dick showing to about 70 kilometres south into Rochon Township.In July 1969 and again in spring 1970, MLU carried out fixed wing spectrometer surveys over their claims. MLU carried out a followup program consisting of ground truthing airborne anomalies, prospecting, line cutting, geological mapping, and ground radiometric surveys. In 1971, MLU carried out a wide spread 80-diamond drill hole program on its various uranium showings; 73 holes were drilled in the Tom Dick showing group area. From 1972 to 1973, exploration programs including geological mapping, hand/blasting and bulldozer trenching, line cutting, limited ground radiometric surveys, and some stream sediment sampling. In 1973, SOQUEM optioned the Tom Dick North Claim Group and focused its exploration efforts on the Tom Dick showing area.
