Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) From Moore and Harris (2005): The Lac Beauboin area was first staked for uranium in 1968 by G. Kildow and F. Charlebois . In February of 1969, Gulf Minerals Canada Ltd. (Gulf) optioned the property from the two prospectors. In September of that year, Gulf hired Seigel Associates Ltd to fly an airborne radiometric and magnetic survey over their Lac Beaudoin claim block. Over the period of September 1969 to November of 1971, Gulf established several cut line grids, which served as control for reconnaissance geological mapping and prospecting. Gulf carried out at least two separate ground radiometrics geophysical surveys (total count scintillometer) over their cut grids, during the 1970 and 1971 field seasons.From early September to end of October 1971, Gulf carried out a 34-hole diamond drill program, testing their higher priority radioactive anomalies in the Lac Beaudoin area. Gulf drilled nine holes on the claims (holes C1 to C9: 466.5 metres), in the area of the single station radiation high, about 1.3 kilometres east-northeast of Lac La Force (or about 1.8 km SE of the Lac Beaudoin showing). In March and April of 1972, Gulf drilled 39 diamond drill holes (holes E1 to E39) to test the 1971 ground total count geophysical anomalies, approximately 800 metres north of Lac La Force (‘Lac Beaudoin showing’).
