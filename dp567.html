Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The Lac Kachiwiss uranium occurrences were discovered in 1968, following a 1967 airborne radiometric survey, by Satellite Metal Mines Ltd., which carried out prospecting, mapping and trenching. Between 1975 and 1978 Getty Mines Ltd. and/or Getty Minerals Company Ltd. (“Getty”) held the Lac Kachiwiss property and carried out geological mapping, soil geochemical and ground magnetic and radiometric surveys, metallurgical testing and 2,173 metres of diamond drilling in 25 holes. Assayed grades are typically in the range of 50 to 10 ppm U3O8, with sporadic higher grades and some areas of much lower grade. Mineralized intercepts in Terra’s 2008 drill holes varied from less than 1 metre to more than 100 metres. Average grades of intercepts were calculated using an across-the-zone cut-off of 50 ppm U3O8.

(Applies to: diamond drill hole descriptions) In 2008, Terra Ventures conducted a diamond drilling program of 12 vertical holes totalling 4399.86 metres of NQ and NQ2 core. The holes were drilled on an approximate 150 by 200 metre grid pattern.

(Applies to: reserve/resource assays) A historical “grade and tonnage” estimate exists based on Getty’s drilling in 1976 and 1978, which does not conform to current standards, cannot be classified using current categories of resource/reserve estimates, and cannot be relied upon. The estimate, made in 1979, was 18.3 million short tons (16.6 million tonnes) grading 0.31 pounds per ton (155 ppm) U3O8 had been outlined.

(Applies to: mineralization) Higher grade zones are typically magnetic and have some visible magnetite or other oxide crystals. Mineralization appears to be magmatic in origin, and shows no significant relationships to structures within the host intrusives.
