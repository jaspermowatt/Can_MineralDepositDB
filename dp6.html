Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) The Agnew Lake Mine was operated by Kerr Addison Mines between 1977 and 1983, and produced about 1.9 million lbs uranium (quoted elsewhere as 850,000 kg U3O8) using surface and underground in-situ leach methods. There is an existing underground infrastructure on the property, plus a decline to the 1,900 ft. level. The property is now being explored by Nyah Resources.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization occurs mainly within oligomictic quartz pebble conglomerate beds associated with arkose, quartzite, and argillite of the basal Huronian Supergroup that unconformably overlies Archean granite in deformed contact. Though the conglomerate is the main host, the associated non-conglomeratic sediments may also be weakly mineralized. The rocks are folded, faulted, and regionally metamorphosed.
