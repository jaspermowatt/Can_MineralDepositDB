Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The general area of the zone (A6 North) straddles the unconformity between the Athabasca sandstone and the underlying deformed and metamorphosed Paleoproterozoic basement. There are two radioactive zones, the T-9N (this showing) and T-15N which is located north of this showing. This showing consists of rubbly hematized red-brown pegmatoid which returned a scintillometer count of 15,000 cps, and is cut by a shear zone at least 2.5 metres wide. Very high radioactivity is associated with the shear zone, and very high values were encountered in hematized but less altered pegmatites in contact with the shear zone. One sample yielded 1.2% U.

(Applies to: location and access) The location of this showing and of T-15N is not clear. SMDI reported the same location for both T-9N and T-15N; its location may be a very general one for the A6 North Uranium zone as a whole.
