Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The N orebody is hosted within Earl River Complex basement rocks proximal to the unconformity with the overlying Athabasca sandstones. It coincides with a transition between granitoids to the west and gneisses to the east; granitoids and gneisses are interlayered in the transitional zone. The orebody itself constitutes a north-south trending zone which exposes a northwest trending vein shuffled by a series of moderately west dipping north-south faults.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization consists of pitchblende and coffinite in an argillic and graphitic matrix, with accessory pyrite, marcasite, chalcopyrite, and galena. Rimsaite, kasolite replacing pitchblende, and wulfenite, encased in uranophane aggregates, occur as vein fillings.
