Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The mineralization is hosted by a contorted sequence of Aphebian quartzite, metagreywacke, and mica schist (metapelites), where the metapelites are interlayered with quartzite. It occurs in fractures and joints within the siliceous beds, preferentially in fold noses. Pyritiferous quartz-mica-graphite schists are reported as part of the sequence. The ore mineral is pitchblende, accompanies by pyrite, and minor chalcopyrite and galena. All zones are cut by intermittently radioactive cross-fractures. Drilling in a similar quartzite-hosted occurrence 1 km to the south yielded intersections of 1.01% U3O8 over 2 m; 0.15% U3O8 over 1 m; 0.025% U3O8 over 1.3 m; 2.19% over 0.5 m; and 0.08 over 0.1 m.
