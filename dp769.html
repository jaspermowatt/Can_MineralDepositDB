Qualified comments: (Applies to: miscellaneous comments) This is the southern extension of the Loredo Deposit.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization is hosted mainly by graphitic schists, which is part of a basement sequence containing zones of interbedded granite, and granitized metasediments and quartzite. Pitchblende fills both northeast and northwest trending fractures in the graphitic schists and interbedded siliceous rocks. In the main No. 1 Zone, which coincides with a shear zone, the vein material is banded, with pyrite on both walls. Bands rich in pitchblende, nolanite, and pyrite occur in the centre of the vein. Chalcopyrite, specular hematite, quartz and chlorite were also observed.
