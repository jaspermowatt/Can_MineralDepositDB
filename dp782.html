Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The occurrence comprises three mineralized fractures (A, B, and C) in northeasterly trending granite and granite gneiss of the Tazin Formation. The Tazin Formation is overlain unconformably by approximately 500 metres of Martin Formation basal conglomerate to the northwest. Zone A is a curved fracture striking from 340 in its northwest part to 290 in the southeast, and is convex and dipping to the south. Radioactivity which coincides with the zone is very high near the bend where the zone widens. Five channel samples yielded an average grade of 0.33% U3O8 over 1.6 metres. The B zone exhibits moderate radioactivity; one sample assayed 0.11% U3O8. Zone C consists of east-trending en echelon fractures which are erratically radioactive.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization in the main zone, 103A, was explored by means of an adit. The best mineralization occurs exclusively in 75 degree SW-dipping fractures coated with hematite, and consists of pods and veins up to an inch thick of carbonate-associated pitchblende and pitchblende films. The greatest concentrations of pitchblende are found in fractured fine-grained granite.
