Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The area of the occurrence is underlain by a thick, northeast-trending sequence of metasedimentary rocks, underlain by leucogranite and granite gneiss. The Eagle Lake Fault zone is the dominant structural feature in the area. An anticline occurs west of the fault and the rocks dip mainly northwest and most folds plunge southwest. A syncline occurs to the east of the fault zone. A series of three mainly sinistral strike-slip faults cut the folds, and are connected by a ladder work of northwest-striking dextral faults. The entire fault zone undulates.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization consists of pitchblende accompanied by minor chalcopyrite, bornite, covellite, magnetite and galena, and occurs mainly in the northwest striking fractures. Native tin has been reported. The zones designated as ore zones preferentially coincide with a slate horizon within interbedded slates and argillites.
