Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) There are three showings for this occurrence, which is generally underlain by northeast-trending, steeply dipping quartzites, amphibolites, granites, and quartzofeldspathic gneisses. The showing 50-TT-75 occurs in the MacFie Fault where it traverses amphibolite, and consists of pitchblende and carbonate-filled fractures; 13 samples assayed on average 0.266% U3O8 over 1.2 m. A second showing consists of a west-northwest trending red pegmatite dyke, and a third showing coincides with radioactive carbonate breccia within granitic rocks.
