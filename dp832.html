Qualified comments: (Applies to: property geology) The occurrences are hosted by a northeast-trending unit of quartzofeldspathic gneiss with biotite-rich and amphibolitic bands with local fracture-filling pegmatites, cut by a brecciated mylonite zone.

(Applies to: mineralization) Mineralization is associated with breccias, cataclastic rocks, mylonite, and chlorite schist. Breccia matrices contain carbonatite and/or quartz, and thin veinlets of carbonatite, quartz, chlorite, and pegmatite (quartz-feldspar). There is intense hematization in the sheared rock, which also contain specular hematite and pyrite. Pitchblende coats fine fractures and occurs locally as stringer zones. Drill intersections of metre dimensions gave grades between 0.5% and 0.56% U3O8.
