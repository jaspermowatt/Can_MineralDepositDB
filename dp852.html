Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) The deposit is hosted by arkoses and conglomerates of the Martin Formation that are interbedded with basaltic and andesitic flows. This sequence is cut by a branching fault system striking NNW and dipping moderately NW. Pitchblende mineralization occurs as massive and colloform veinlets and stringers in calcite veins and in fault breccia cemented with calcite. Mineralization is mainly associated with the mafic volcanics and volcano-sedimentary contacts.
