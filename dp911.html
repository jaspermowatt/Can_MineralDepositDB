Qualified comments: (Applies to: mineralization) U mineralization occurs in a fining upward sequence of oxidized pebble conglomerate, coarse arkosic sandstone, grey micaceous mudstone and fine sandstone. Higher radioactivity occurs in reduced beds and along micro-fractures and with carbonaceous material found in oxidized and reduced beds.

(Applies to: genetic model) The source of the U may have been the underlying evaporites of the upward fining fluvial cycles. The evaporites hosted the uranium-bearing brines. On burial and dewatering, the brines were expelled through the permeable beds of the overlying fluvial beds and uranium was precipitated in reduction sites, usually organic traps. Shell geologists in 1980s noted close association of limestone or limy beds with the U occurrences of Codroy Valley occurrences suggesting that the limestones were deposited by the U rich brines.

(Applies to: discovery and development) Northgate Explorations Ltd. in 1978 staked the small U showings indicated by 1975 geological mapping and carried out stream and soil sampling, radiometric surveys, prospecting and geological mapping. Shell Canada Resources in 1980 investigated the claims but did not locate any new significant U occurrences.
