Qualified comments: (Applies to: discovery and development) Northgate Explorations Ltd. in 1978 staked the small U showings indicated by 1975 geological mapping and carried out stream and soil sampling, radiometric surveys, prospecting and geological mapping. Shell Canada Resources in 1980 carried out airborne spectrometric survey, ground scintillometer survey, geological mapping, prospecting, and geochemical sampling of uranium occurrences. Shell Resources did not locate any new significant U occurrences (Ref ID-7000047).

(Applies to: genetic model) The source of the U may have been the underlying evaporites of the upward fining fluvial cycles. The evaporites hosted the uranium-bearing brines. On burial and dewatering, the brines were expelled through the permeable beds of the overlying fluvial beds and uranium was precipitated in reduction sites, usually organic traps. Shell geologists in 1980s noted close association of limestone or limy beds with the U occurrences of Codroy Valley occurrences suggesting that the limestones were deposited by the U rich brines. The U was probably later remobilized from the limestone and redeposited at reduction sites.
