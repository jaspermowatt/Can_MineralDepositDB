Qualified comments: (Applies to: miscellaneous comments) Felsic subvolcanic intrusions lie on the margin of a late Devonian caldera. Intrusive quartz feldspar porphyry is cut by a mineralized hydrothermal breccia pipe. The breccia is intruded by dykes and plugs of Mount Pleasant Porphyry, the latter which grades at depth into fine-grained granites or microgranites.
